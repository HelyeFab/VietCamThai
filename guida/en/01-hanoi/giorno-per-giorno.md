# DAY BY DAY — HANOI

## Day 28 — October 28, 2026 — First Taste of Hanoi's Magic

![Hanoi's Old Quarter in the evening](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Hanoi_Old_Quarter%2C_9_March_2019-2.jpg/1280px-Hanoi_Old_Quarter%2C_9_March_2019-2.jpg)

### Morning
Arrival in Hanoi from Lan Ha Bay (Cat Ba) by bus, the cruise now over, and check-in at a hotel in the heart of the Old Quarter.
The Temple of Literature is only 2 kilometres from the Old Quarter and Hoan Kiem Lake
, a perfect base for the last days of the trip.

![Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Hanoi_Vietnam_Mausoleum-of-Ho-Chi-Minh-01.jpg/1280px-Hanoi_Vietnam_Mausoleum-of-Ho-Chi-Minh-01.jpg)

**The journey from Cat Ba**:
-
The direct bus from Cat Ba (bus + ferry + bus) takes about 3-4 hours, arriving in the Old Quarter

- Once you've dropped off your luggage, a stroll around Hoan Kiem Lake is the perfect way to begin

### Afternoon
**First encounter with the Old Quarter** (14:00-17:00)
- An orientation walk through
the 36 guild streets of the Old Quarter, created to supply high-quality goods to the royals in the Thang Long Citadel

![Temple of Literature](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Main_gate_of_the_Temple_of_Literature%2C_Hanoi%2C_Vietnam%2C_20240123_0929_3068.jpg/1280px-Main_gate_of_the_Temple_of_Literature%2C_Hanoi%2C_Vietnam%2C_20240123_0929_3068.jpg)

-
Hang Bac (Silver Street) still houses jewellers and money changers

-
Hang Gai, now known as Silk Street, is home to fabric shops and tailors making made-to-measure clothes

**Alternatives in case of rain**:
- A visit to
St Joseph's Cathedral, built in 1886, a reminder of the French colonial presence

- Shopping in the Old Quarter's covered markets

### Evening
**Dinner — First Pho of your life** (18:30-20:00)

**Phở Gia Truyền Bát Đàn** ⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️
- Address: 49 P. Bát Đàn, Cửa Đông, Hoàn Kiếm, Hà Nội
-
The site of the first bowl of pho in Vietnam for many travellers — an almost religious experience

-
Very reasonable prices: around 50,000 VND per bowl

- Hours: 6:00-10:00 and 18:00-20:30

**After dinner**:
-
An evening stroll around Hoan Kiem Lake to see the famous red Huc bridge, Ngoc Son Temple and the iconic Turtle Tower

-
Ice cream on Trang Tien street — an essential Hanoi experience

### Tips of the Day
- **Booking the bus to Ha Long**:
Book at check-in to avoid expensive organised tours

-
Street food costs 20,000-80,000 VND per dish (0.80-3.20 USD). Pho costs 40,000-60,000 VND

- Always carry cash — many vendors don't accept cards
-
The streets overflow with street vendors and motorbikes. Start with a cyclo (rickshaw) for a guided tour through the chaos

## Day 29 — October 29, 2026 — Lakes, Temples and Thousand-Year Culture

### Morning
**Hoan Kiem Lake and Ngoc Son Temple** (8:00-10:00)
-
The temple is open from 8:00 to 18:00 every day

-
Tickets: 50,000 VND per person

-
The ticket office is on the eastern side of the lake near the Pen Tower; to reach the temple, cross the red wooden 'Cau The Huc' bridge

-
The temple displays the preserved bodies of 2 giant turtles that once lived in Hoan Kiem Lake, of great cultural and historical importance

**Temple of Literature** (10:30-12:30)
-
Entrance ticket: about 70,000 VND (~2.50 USD) for adults; open from 8:00 to 17:00

-
Taxi or Grab: 60,000-70,000 VND for the trip

-
A well-preserved traditional Vietnamese building from the 11th century, also the country's national university

### Afternoon
**Vietnam Museum of Ethnology** (14:00-17:00)
-
Address: Nguyen Van Huyen road, Cau Giay District. Hours: 8:30-17:30 (closed Monday). Ticket: 40,000 VND / person = 1.80 USD

-
Located about 8 kilometres from the city centre

-
Plan at least 3-4 hours to explore both the indoor and outdoor exhibits

-
Divided into two sections: an indoor exhibition and an outdoor area with over 10 traditional houses of different ethnic groups

**How to get there**:
-
Taxi or Grab from the city centre, or bus number 14 or 38 from Hoan Kiem Lake

### Evening
**Train Street Experience** (18:30-19:30)
-
Train Street is open to tourists (as of 2024), provided they are seated in a café under the care of the owners

-
It has quickly become the most famous photo spot in Hanoi's Old Quarter

- **Note**: Check the opening status on the spot — some areas may be closed to tourists

**Dinner — Bun Cha Adventure** (19:45-21:00)

**Bún Chả 74 Hang Quạt** ⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️
-
Grilled pork served in a sweet-and-sour fish-sauce broth, with rice vermicelli and fresh herbs

-
Full meal: 40,000-80,000 VND (~2-3 USD)

-
Walk through the Old Quarter at lunchtime and the charcoal smoke from the bún chả grills will hit you on every other street

### Tips of the Day
-
Ngoc Son Temple can get very crowded, so if you want to avoid the crowds head to the temple as soon as the ticket office opens

-
Photography is not allowed inside the indoor exhibition hall of the Museum of Ethnology

-
Watch your clothes: charcoal smoke clings, so avoid your best outfit

-
Bring cash: the ticket office does not accept credit-card payments

## Day 30 — October 30, 2026 — Politics, History and Perfect Street Food

### Morning
**Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum** (7:30-10:30)
-
The mausoleum is open from 8:00 to 11:00. Saturdays, Sundays and public holidays: 8:00-11:30. Open Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday and on weekends

-
For Vietnamese, entry to the Mausoleum is free. For foreign visitors, the ticket price is 25,000 VND (about 1 USD)

-
Precise address: 8 Hung Vuong, Ba Dinh District, Hanoi

**Mandatory dress code**:
-
Visitors must wear formal clothing. Shorts, miniskirts, sleeveless shirts and tank tops are not permitted

-
Cameras, mobile phones and backpacks will be collected by the guards before entry. No filming in restricted areas

**Quan Thanh Temple** (11:00-12:00)
- A Taoist temple dedicated to Tran Vo, in the Ba Dinh district
- A short visit to catch your breath after the solemnity of the Mausoleum

### Afternoon
**French Quarter Exploration** (14:00-17:00)
-
The area was chosen by the French colonial government as the political centre of Tonkin/the North, and later of the Indochinese Federation. You can still see numerous grand buildings constructed in French architecture

- Presidential Palace (external view)
-
Presidential Palace: Location No 2 Hung Vuong - Ba Dinh. Entry is not permitted — visitors can view it from the outside

**One Pillar Pagoda** (15:30-16:00)
-
About half an hour on foot from the Old Quarter. It sits between the Ho Chi Minh Museum and the Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum

-
A Buddhist temple built in 1049 by King Ly Thai Tong, in the shape of a blooming lotus

### Evening
**DIY Evening Street Food Tour** (18:30-21:30)

**Stop 1 — Bánh Mì 25** (18:30-19:00)
- Address: 25 Hang Ca, Old Quarter
-
A famous spot serving a crunchy baguette filled with paté, cold cuts, pickled daikon and carrot, cucumber, coriander and chilli. A sandwich costs around 25,000-30,000 VND

-
It became so famous after opening in 2014 that it is now a tourist attraction in its own right

**Stop 2 — Egg Coffee Experience** (19:15-20:00)

**Cafe Giang** ⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️⭐️
-
39 Nguyễn Hữu Huân — warm, creamy egg coffee, invented in Hanoi

-
Café Đinh (13 Đinh Tiên Hoàng): Hanoi's most authentic egg coffee for 45,000 VND. A secret recipe perfected since 1946

-
Egg coffee ~30k VND. It tastes like liquid tiramisu and is perfect to sip on a cool morning

**Stop 3 — Bia Hoi Corner** (20:15-21:30)
-
The area around Beer Street and Hoan Kiem Lake is lively and well lit

- Fresh local beer at 10,000-15,000 VND
- An authentic atmosphere with locals and travellers

### Tips of the Day
-
The mausoleum welcomes visitors from 7:30 to 10:30, Tuesday to Thursday and on weekends. This limited window creates an experience that feels both exclusive and ceremonial

-
Arriving at 7:00 lets you witness the changing of the guard, a precisely choreographed ceremony

-
The mausoleum closes annually for about two months (typically October and November)

-
Street food is generally safe; the key is to eat at busy stalls (more turnover means fresher food) and stick to fully cooked dishes

-
Drinks — iced coffee, sugarcane juice, trà đá (iced tea, often free at stalls) — add 15,000-30,000 VND. Stalls in tourist areas may cost 10-20% more

## Day 31 — October 31, 2026 — Goodbye, Vietnam

![Dawn flight over Hanoi](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Hanoi_Old_Quarter%2C_9_March_2019-2.jpg/1280px-Hanoi_Old_Quarter%2C_9_March_2019-2.jpg)

The last day of the trip. After 31 days across Thailand, Cambodia and Vietnam, it's time to say goodbye to Southeast Asia and take the international flight home from Hanoi.

### Morning
- **Last Vietnamese coffee**: treat yourself to a *cà phê sữa đá* (iced coffee with condensed milk) or a *cà phê trứng* (egg coffee) in one of the Old Quarter's historic cafés — the best way to close out the trip.
- **Last shopping and souvenirs**: a final stroll through the 36 guild streets for last-minute gifts — silk from Hang Gai, ceramics from Bat Trang, Vietnamese coffee and tea to take home.
- **Farewell pho**: one last bowl of steaming *pho bò*, where it all began.
- **Check-out**: most hotels allow check-out until noon; ask for a *late check-out* or leave your luggage in storage if your flight is in the evening.

### Afternoon / Departure
- **Airport transfer**: Noi Bai International Airport (HAN) is about 30 km from the Old Quarter — taxi or Grab ~250,000–350,000 VND (~€10–14), 45–60 minutes depending on traffic. Alternatively the express bus 86 (~35,000 VND) connects the Old Quarter to the airport.
- **At the airport**: arrive 3 hours early for international flights. Remember to complete any **tax-free refund** on major purchases before security.
- **Return flight**: take-off from Hanoi towards home. 🛫

> **Final tip**: set aside a few dong for that last coffee and for the transfer — many duty-free shops accept cards but the stalls and taxis don't. *Tạm biệt Việt Nam!* (Goodbye, Vietnam!)

---

This guide takes you into the authentic heart of Hanoi, blending a thousand years of history with modern life. Every day is calibrated to give you time to truly savour the city, not just rush through it. And remember: the Vietnamese are incredibly kind — if you get lost, always ask for help!
