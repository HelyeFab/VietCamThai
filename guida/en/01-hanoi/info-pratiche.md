## Practical Info — Hanoi

### Getting There

**From Halong / Lan Ha Bay (Cat Ba) to Hanoi by bus (~3-4 hours)**

Hanoi is the last stop of the trip: you arrive from Lan Ha Bay (Cat Ba island), the cruise now over.
The direct bus combines bus + ferry + bus and takes about 3-4 hours, arriving in the Old Quarter
. Reliable companies like **Cat Ba Express** and **Good Morning Cat Ba** offer frequent morning departures and pickup from the pier; indicative price 250,000-300,000 VND (€8-12) per person. From Halong city the bus connection is even quicker (~2.5 hours).

💡 Ask your Cat Ba accommodation to book the bus at check-out to avoid the expensive tours and have the pickup included.

**For the journey home — from the centre to Noi Bai airport**


For the last day, the transfer from the Old Quarter to Noi Bai International Airport costs VND 350,000–450,000 (~$14–18) and takes about 45-60 minutes
. For 4 people with luggage, the most practical solution is:

- **Grab**:
Grab is widely available at Noi Bai airport. You'll need an internet connection to book, and pickups typically happen at Gate 1 of Terminal 2 for international arrivals
. Price:
300,000–375,000 VND (€12-€15) for GrabCar/GrabTaxi

- **Official taxi**:
Noi Bai Airport has contracts with several taxi companies that maintain service desks immediately outside the arrivals halls. These official providers: apply fixed fares (about ₫350,000-400,000 to the city centre)

- **Public bus**:
Bus 86 runs from Noi Bai Airport to the Long Bien bus station (passing through Hanoi's Old Quarter area) for VND 45,000 (~$1.80), taking 45–60 minutes. Bus 07 goes more directly to Hanoi's Old Quarter


**⚠️ Watch out for scams**:
At Noi Bai airport, book Grab from the arrivals hall before going out — the taxi mafia operates near the exit doors offering rides at inflated fares
.
Another common trick is for taxis to ask for the airport toll of 10,000 VND on top of the fare. This is included in the fare and should not be paid separately
.

### Getting Around

**Grab (ride-hailing app)**

The Grab app gives you access to a complete transport platform with multiple service tiers: GrabBike: motorbike-taxi service starting at 8,000 VND base fare plus 12,000-14,000 VND per kilometre · GrabCar: four-seat sedans


Typical prices:
- **GrabBike**:
Fixed prices shown in the app (usually 10,000-30,000 VND for short distances)

- **GrabCar in the historic centre**:
5 km, 10–15 min, €2–€3 (50,000–75,000 VND)

- **Traditional xe om**:
Negotiate the price (typically 15,000-40,000 VND for short trips)


**On foot in the Old Quarter**

Hanoi's Old Quarter is best explored on foot — it's only 1km across
.
Once you arrive in the Old Quarter, you'll find it compact enough to explore mainly on foot
.

**Cycling**

That said, if you're up for the challenge, many Hanoi hotels offer bike rentals starting at $1 a day
. ⚠️ **Note**:
Keep up defensive cycling practices and avoid the main arterial roads during rush hour
.

**Practical tips**:
-
Avoid the heavy-traffic hours (7-9 AM and 17-19)

-
Don't hesitate or run when crossing Hanoi's streets — keep a steady pace and make eye contact with the oncoming drivers. They'll adapt to you if your movements are predictable

### Connectivity & SIM

**Free WiFi**

Asia is far more modern than you might think, and there's WiFi EVERYWHERE in Vietnam!
Hotel, restaurant, café and shopping-centre guests generally enjoy a free WiFi connection. You'll have free access in all shops, cafés, restaurants, hotels and guesthouses
.

**Local SIMs - Where to buy**

At the international airports of Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City there are endless opportunities to buy a prepaid Vietnam SIM card. In case you miss the chances at the airport, there are mobile-phone shops everywhere in the big cities
.

**Main operators**:
1. **Viettel** (best coverage):
Use Viettel for trips across the country

2. **Vinaphone**:
Vinaphone if you're mainly staying in Hanoi or HCMC

3. **MobiFone**

**Typical tourist packages** (2025):
-
If you're staying 7-14 days, a plan with 15-20 GB of data is more than enough for Google Maps, WhatsApp and social-media uploads. For longer stays, or if you plan to share a WiFi hotspot with friends or upload videos, choose the 30 GB+ package

- Price:
Prices typically start from about AU$5 to AU$50 for basic data packages


**⚠️ Mandatory registration**:
Under Vietnamese law, SIM registration is required for all users — including tourists. Without this step, your SIM could be blocked
.
Remember to bring your passport with you, as they'll need it to register your new SIM card
.

📍 **Where to buy in Hanoi**:
- **Airport**:
There are numerous kiosks selling SIM cards at Hanoi airport

- **Official stores**:
For Viettel offices for SIM cards in Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, or other provinces/cities: check https://vietteltelecom.vn/cua-hang

- **Convenience stores**: Circle K, VinMart for top-ups

### Safety

**General situation**

Vietnam is considered one of the safest destinations in Southeast Asia. Violent crime against foreigners is rare, and most trips are trouble-free. The main risks are petty theft, road accidents and tourist scams
.


Hanoi is generally considered a low-risk destination for travellers. Violent crime against tourists is rare, and the city maintains a strong police presence in the central areas. Most safety concerns involve petty theft, traffic hazards and scams rather than serious criminal activity
.

**Common scams to avoid**

1. **The donut scam**:
On the streets of Hanoi, you'll see many women selling delicious-looking doughnuts. They'll offer you a taste and fill up a whole bag. However, when you say you only want a handful, they'll keep adding more and then charge you an outrageous amount


2. **The tampered-meter taxi scam**:
Hanoi has a well-documented problem with meter tampering — some taxis have "turbo meters" that run 5-10 times faster than the legal rate
.
The taxi meter runs faster than your heartbeat: many taxis flagged down on the street have tampered meters. ✅ The guide's solution: They can help you call Grab (the Vietnamese version of Uber), linked to your credit card for fixed prices, ensuring you won't be cheated


3. **The shoeshine scam**:
I've seen these shoeshiners take people's shoes off to start polishing them, knowing they won't walk off barefoot. Once the polish is done they'll ask for a price that's about 10 times what you'd expect


4. **"Tourist prices" menus**:
Some restaurants have two menus and will show the expensive one to foreigners


**Practical safety tips**

-
As a tourist, you should pay extra attention to scammers, pickpockets and reckless drivers. Stay alert and keep track of all your belongings

-
Keep your valuables in a bag with a zip and wear it in front of you in crowded areas. It's tempting to pull out your phone for a photo, but be careful on busy streets where the motorbikes whizz past

-
Tap water in Hanoi is not considered safe to drink. Even the locals typically boil the water before use. Travellers should stick to bottled or properly filtered water to avoid gastrointestinal illness
