# FOOD & CULTURE - Hanoi, Vietnam

## Where and What to Eat in Hanoi

### Must-Try Signature Dishes

**Phở (Noodle soup) - 50,000-70,000 VND (~€2-3)**

![Bun cha, Hanoi's iconic dish](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/B%C3%BAn_ch%E1%BA%A3_Vietnamese_food.jpg/1280px-B%C3%BAn_ch%E1%BA%A3_Vietnamese_food.jpg)

Hanoi is famous for phở (beef noodle soup), bun cha (grilled pork with noodles), cha ca La Vong (a turmeric-fish speciality), banh cuon (steamed rice rolls) and of course the legendary egg coffee (ca phe trung). Look for Pho 10 Ly Quoc Su or Pho Bat Dan near Hoan Kiem Lake for traditional bowls (open early in the morning)
.
Hanoi's version of phở is delicate: the broth is the star. Sit on a tiny plastic stool, let the steam fog up your glasses and watch the locals eat with ease. This is Hanoi in a bowl: simple, historic and quietly elegant
.

**Bún Chả (Grilled pork with noodles) - 50,000-80,000 VND (~€2-3.50)**

Bún Chả: Marinated grilled pork served over rice noodles with herbs and fish sauce
.
Bun cha is an iconic dish born in Hanoi, so famous that it once brought together an American president and a celebrity chef on plastic stools. Often enjoyed at lunch, bun cha consists of smoky grilled pork meatballs and slices of pork belly served in a bowl of warm fish sauce, sweetened with pickled papaya. You pair it with a generous portion of white rice noodles and a heap of fresh herbs such as perilla, lettuce and mint
.

**Bánh Mì (Vietnamese sandwich) - 20,000-30,000 VND (~€0.80-1.30)**

Crunchy outside, soft inside, and filled with pickled vegetables, herbs and paté - bánh mì is Vietnam's answer to the perfect street snack. In Hanoi you'll find everything from classic fillings to creative variations, all made to be eaten on the move. Grab one with your morning coffee or as a late-night snack - it's quick, cheap and unforgettable
.

**Cà Phê Trứng (Egg coffee) - 30,000 VND (~€1.30)**

Egg coffee (Ca Phe Trung): Invented in Hanoi, this creamy coffee dessert is an absolute must-try. The warm, frothy egg-yolk coffee at Cafe Giang (39 Nguyễn Hữu Huân) or at the modern Cafe Pho Co (19 Lý Quốc Sư) will show you why it's famous. It tastes like liquid tiramisu and is perfect to sip on a cool morning
.

**Xôi Xéo (Sticky rice) - 20,000-35,000 VND (~€0.80-1.50)**

Sticky rice (Xôi Xéo): A popular street breakfast: sticky rice with mung beans and fried shallots, often sold from baskets on the pavement. For the best sticky rice in town I'd pick Xôi bà Thu over Xôi Yến (old Mrs Ba Thu's stall is on Thọ Xuân street)
.

### Our Recommended Restaurants and Street Food

**For Phở:**
- **Pho 10 Ly Quoc Su** (10 Lý Quốc Sư, Hoan Kiem) -
In 2016, the BBC noted that Pho 10 Ly Quoc Su is among the best pho addresses in Vietnam

- **Pho Bat Dan** (49 Bat Dan, Old Quarter) - Traditional and authentic, open from 6:00

**For Bún Chả:**
- **Bún Chả Hương Liên** (24 Lê Văn Hưu) -
The Hanoi restaurant Bún Chả Hương Liên became famous after US President Barack Obama dined there with Chef Anthony Bourdain during his trip to Vietnam in May 2016. The restaurant has since earned the nickname Bún Chả Obama, with photos of his visit on the walls, and also offers an "Obama combo", consisting of the bún chả, a fried seafood roll and a bottle of Hanoi Beer that the president ordered

- **Bun Cha Ta** (Nguyen Huu Huan Street) -
According to Pham Thu Hoai, owner of Bun Cha Ta (Nguyen Huu Huan street), a Bib Gourmand recipient in the MICHELIN Guide Vietnam 2024, precision is essential

**For Restaurants:**
- **ESSENCE Restaurant** (9th floor, Aira Boutique Hotel) -
Located on the 9th floor of the Aira Boutique Hanoi hotel, with a stunning city view, the restaurant offers 100 seats and a VIP room. Essence restaurant is the perfect choice for fine dining in Hanoi, Vietnam, serving Vietnamese cuisine with a touch of European influence
. Price: 500,000-1,500,000 VND (€20-60)

- **Hong Hoai Restaurant** (56 Hang Buom, Old Quarter) -
Hong Hoai restaurant offers a variety of traditional Vietnamese dishes such as bun cha, spring rolls, banh xeo,... with an authentic Vietnamese flavour. This restaurant serves many of Hanoi's signature dishes in its own distinctive style
. Price: 150,000-400,000 VND (€6-16)

- **Banh Mi 25** (25 Hang Ca Street) -
By far the best Banh Mi we tried in Hanoi. Banh Mi 25 is a simple little kiosk in the middle of the street, but you'll find plenty of loyal customers queuing up, along with many foreigners too

**For Beer:**
- **Ta Hien Street (Beer Corner)** -
If you love beer, head to the end of Ma May Street, the hub of Hanoi's nightlife with many hostels and budget hotels. You can enjoy a good, light draught beer for just 5,000 dong (20 euro cents!)

### Markets

**Dong Xuan Market**

Day market: Open from 6:00 to 19:00. Weekend night market: Open Friday to Sunday, from 18:00 to 23:30
.
Dong Xuan Market is not only famous for shopping but also serves as a street-food paradise, where visitors can enjoy Hanoi's signature dishes at reasonable prices. It's a perfect place for both tourists and locals to experience the city's culinary culture. Pho is a must when you visit the market. Many traditional pho stalls on the ground floor serve a clear, flavourful broth with soft rice noodles and finely sliced beef or chicken
.

**Best times to visit:**
-
Dong Xuan Market is open daily from early morning until late evening, typically from 6:00 to 19:00. However, some vendors may start setting up as early as 4:00 and stay open until 22:00

-
The weekend night market (Friday-Sunday) is the best time for the full experience, from 18:00 to 23:30

**What to buy:**
-
The food stalls often sell "banh beo" (a puffy fern-shaped dumpling), pork belly, grilled "nem chua", sausages, Hai Phong bread, Donner Kebab, sweets, sweet porridge from the northern provinces or from Hue city

### Etiquette and Culture

**In Temples:**

Sacred places such as temples and pagodas are plentiful in Vietnam. When you visit them: Dress modestly: shoulders and knees should be covered. Take off your shoes before entering sacred spaces
.
To visit the Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum, you must observe certain rules. Let's start with clothing: you need to arrive with a simple, casual dress code, a long-sleeved shirt and long trousers, or long clothing (shorts, miniskirts and very short outfits are forbidden)
.

**At the Table:**

Practise using chopsticks; if you're not comfortable, ask for a fork
.
To order, you can say "Tôi muốn…", which means "I would like…". Some useful food words include "cơm" (rice), "phở" (noodle soup), "cà phê" (coffee)
.

**General Behaviour:**

In Vietnamese culture the head is considered sacred and the feet impure. Try not to touch anyone's head, and never point with your feet — both are seen as quite disrespectful
.

**Photographs:**

Always ask permission before photographing individuals or culturally sensitive places. That said, in big tourist cities like Da Nang and Hanoi the locals are quite used to tourists and are in fact rather happy when you take photos with them
.

### Useful Words

**Greetings and Courtesy:**
- **Xin chào** (/seen chao/) - Hello/Good morning
- **Cảm ơn** (/gam un/) - Thank you
- **Không có gì** (/khong go zi/) - You're welcome
- **Xin lỗi** (/seen loy/) - Sorry/Excuse me
- **Tạm biệt** (/tam biet/) - Goodbye

**At the Restaurant:**
- **Tôi muốn...** (/toy muon/) - I would like...
- **Bao nhiều tiền?** (/bao nhieu tien/) - How much is it?
- **Ngon** (/ngon/) - Delicious
- **Cơm** (/gom/) - Rice
- **Nước** (/nuoc/) - Water
- **Bia** (/bia/) - Beer

**Essential Numbers:**
- **Một** (/mot/) - One
- **Hai** (/hai/) - Two
- **Ba** (/ba/) - Three
-
Nghìn is the northern Vietnamese form (spoken in Hanoi and the north), ngàn is the southern one (Ho Chi Minh City and the south). If you use one in the other region you'll still be understood without any trouble

**Useful Phrases:**
- **Có thể giúp tôi không?** (/go the zup toy khong/) - Can you help me?
- **Tôi không hiểu** (/toy khong hieu/) - I don't understand
- **Chúc sức khỏe!** (/chook shook khoe/) - Cheers! (for toasting)

Don't worry about perfect pronunciation: the Vietnamese warmly appreciate any effort a foreigner makes to speak their language. Even if it comes out imperfect, they'll see that you're trying. They are wonderfully patient with visitors, and every attempt reads as a sign of respect and genuine curiosity about their culture
.

**Practical tips:**
-
The Vietnamese eat most street food for breakfast/lunch, 7-14. Many pho, bun cha and xoi stalls pop up around 6:30-7:00 and close by lunchtime. Dinner is mainly for restaurants and bars, but 24-hour banh mi and bánh rán (fried doughnut) stalls do exist

-
Food in Hanoi is extremely cheap. A bowl of pho or bún cha costs ~50k VND (a little over $2). Egg coffee is ~30k, street snacks 10-30k
