# DAY BY DAY — Phnom Penh

## Day 11 — October 11, 2026 — **At the Confluence of the Great Rivers**

### Morning
You arrive in Phnom Penh by bus from the coast, after the ferry back from Koh Rong Samloem to Sihanoukville (about 4–5 hours of travel). Once you've disembarked, drop your bags at your accommodation and head straight for the **Royal Palace** (Preah Barum Reachea Veang).

![The Tuol Sleng S-21 Genocide Museum](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/81/2016_Phnom_Penh%2C_Muzeum_Ludob%C3%B3jstwa_Tuol_Sleng_%2801%29.jpg/1280px-2016_Phnom_Penh%2C_Muzeum_Ludob%C3%B3jstwa_Tuol_Sleng_%2801%29.jpg)

The palace is open from 8:00 to 11:00 and from 14:00 to 17:00
, so you have time for a morning visit. 
Admission costs $10 USD per person, cash only
. 

**Strict dress code**: 
bare shoulders and backs are not allowed, clothing must cover the knees, hats and shoes are not permitted in certain areas
. If you're not dressed appropriately, 
you'll have to buy a t-shirt at the gate for $3
.

The complex includes the famous **Silver Pagoda** with 
5,329 solid silver tiles, each over 1 kg
. 
The best time for photos is late afternoon around 16:00 when the light softens
.

**Useful link**: [Royal Palace website](https://www.royalpalace.gov.kh)

### Afternoon
After the palace, stroll along **Sisowath Quay** (the riverfront) to the **confluence of the four rivers** where the Mekong, Tonle Sap and Bassac meet. It's the perfect spot to understand why Phnom Penh is called "Chaktomuk" (four faces).

![The Silver Pagoda](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/2016_Phnom_Penh%2C_Pa%C5%82ac_Kr%C3%B3lewski%2C_Srebrna_Pagoda_%2802%29.jpg/1280px-2016_Phnom_Penh%2C_Pa%C5%82ac_Kr%C3%B3lewski%2C_Srebrna_Pagoda_%2802%29.jpg)

**Rain alternative**: Visit the **National Museum**, a 5-minute walk from the Royal Palace, home to the country's finest collection of Khmer art.

### Evening
**The evening's highlight — the sunset cruise on the Mekong**: after the Royal Palace, this is the not-to-be-missed experience. 
The cruise departs at 17:00 from the riverfront and passes in front of the Royal Palace as the sun begins to set over the great rivers
: one of the most beautiful memories of Phnom Penh, and the perfect way to close out the day. 
Price: $17 per adult, includes tuk-tuk pickup, unlimited beers, soft drinks and fresh fruit
. 
Return at 19:00
.

**Where to book**: 
- [Memorable Cambodia Cruise](https://www.memorablecambodia.com/) - WhatsApp +855 17 226 700
- [Tara River Boat](https://taraboat.com/) 
- Search "Mekong Magic Cruise" on GetYourGuide

**Alternatives if you skip the cruise**: 
- **Friends The Restaurant** (215 Street 13) - 
a social restaurant that trains former street children, excellent tapas, Khmer dishes, fruit cocktails, reasonable prices

- Street food along Riverside Park from 18:00

### Tips of the Day
- **Currency exchange**: USD widely accepted, but 
they prefer intact, clean bills. Keep some riel for small change

- **Transport**: Tuk-tuk for the whole day ~$10-15. PassApp is the local Grab-style app
- **Palace hours**: 
Opening hours are limited and the palace may close for royal events without notice

- **Cruise**: 
Pickup is one-way only; after the cruise you can stroll along the riverfront and call a tuk-tuk via Grab

## Day 12 — October 12, 2026 — **In the Heart of Khmer History**

### Morning
**Essential but emotionally heavy visit**: Start at 8:00 with **Tuol Sleng S-21** (former Khmer Rouge prison). 
Open from 8:00 to 17:00, entry with a valid pass required
. 
Cost: $5 for adults, $3 for under 18s
.

Audio guide available in 11 languages
 (including Italian). 
The best time is early morning on weekdays when there are fewer crowds. Plan 2–3 hours for the full visit
.

**Dress code**: 
respectful attire required, arms and legs covered as at memorial sites
.

**Address**: St. 113, Boeung Keng Kang III
**Link**: [Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum](https://tuolsleng.gov.kh)

### Afternoon
Continue on to the **Choeung Ek Killing Fields** (17 km south). 
Open 7:30–17:30, admission $6 USD includes multilingual audio guide and headphones
. 
The price includes the essential audio guide
.

It's best to arrive early in the morning when it's cooler and less crowded
, but in your case the afternoon will be fine after S-21.

**Transport**: 
Negotiate with the tuk-tuk driver before the trip. A round trip from the center costs about $12–15
. 
The tuk-tuk to visit both sites costs $20–22
.

**Dress code**: 
Strictly modest, required. It's a mass burial site and a sacred memorial. Knees and shoulders must be covered
.

### Evening
After this emotional day, choose something relaxing:

**Option 1 - Aperitivo and dinner at Bassac Lane**: 
Bassac Lane has become one of the best evening spots in Phnom Penh — cozy alleys with quality bars, cafés and restaurants and a far more pleasant atmosphere than simply wandering the market after dark. Alternatively, a quiet dinner along the riverside (Sisowath Quay), with a view over the rivers.

**Note on the Central Market**: if you want to see the **Central Market (Phsar Thmei)**, visit it during the day (open 7:00–18:00), when you can admire its striking ochre-colored Art Deco architecture — one of the city's most photographed domes — and shop among stalls of jewelry, textiles and souvenirs.

**Central Market address**: 
Street 128 (Kampuchea Krom), Phsar Thmei 1 Commune

**Option 2 - Relaxing dinner**:
- **Malis** (near Independence Monument) - 
one of the best Khmer restaurants in Phnom Penh, an open-air courtyard with a tropical garden, statues of Jayavarman VII, air-conditioned rooms available

- **Romdeng** - 
part of the Friends International program, staff trained to help poor Cambodian children break the cycle of poverty

### Tips of the Day
- **Emotionally demanding**: 
I recommend visiting these sites before anything else in Cambodia; save Angkor for later — you'll need that wonder after this immersion in history

- **Water and protection**: 
Bring sunscreen, a hat and water because Phnom Penh can be very hot and humid

- **Cash**: 
Cash only; most tours don't include the entrance fees

- **Respect**: 
There's only one way to visit: in silence. The place is incredibly peaceful thanks to the audio guides

- **Time needed**: 
The visit takes about an hour and a half to fully immerse yourself in the survivors' stories

**Tuk-tuk emergency contact**: Use PassApp or Grab to call a tuk-tuk if you need one.

---

These two days will give you a complete experience of Phnom Penh: from royal grandeur to tragic recent history, from the charm of the rivers to the Art Deco architecture of the markets. It's an emotional journey but a necessary one to truly understand modern Cambodia.
