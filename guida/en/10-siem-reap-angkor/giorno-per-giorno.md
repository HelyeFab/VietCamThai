# SIEM REAP & ANGKOR — DAY BY DAY

## Day 5 — October 5, 2026 — Arrival in the Khmer Kingdom

![Angkor Wat](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/2014-Cambodge_Angkor_Wat_%2821%29.jpg/1280px-2014-Cambodge_Angkor_Wat_%2821%29.jpg)

### Morning
A calm wake-up in Bangkok: today you fly. After breakfast and check-out, your **private transfer** picks you up at The Cotton Saladaeng and takes you to **Don Mueang (DMK)** (~40-60 min with morning traffic). The **Thai AirAsia flight** departs around **10:55** and in just over an hour brings you to Cambodia (~€50 per person): show up for check-in **2.5-3 hours** early, so you leave without rushing.

![Ta Prohm — the Tomb Raider temple](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Tree_roots_and_temple%2C_Ta_Prohm%2C_Angkor%2C_Cambodia.jpg/1280px-Tree_roots_and_temple%2C_Ta_Prohm%2C_Angkor%2C_Cambodia.jpg)

### Afternoon
**~12:15 - Landing in Siem Reap**: you disembark at the new Siem Reap–Angkor International Airport (SAI), about 50 km east of the city. 
From the new airport it takes about 45-60 minutes by road to reach the center
. At the arrivals exit you'll find taxis and tuk-tuks: agree on the price in advance (or book the transfer through the hotel).

Check in at the hotel, take a shower, and the rest of the afternoon is devoted to rest and acclimatization. If you have the energy, take a walk through the center to get your bearings: 
Pub Street stretches for 100 meters between Red Piano Restaurant and Banana Leaf Restaurant
 and during the day it's quiet — ideal for recognizing the sights without the nighttime chaos.

![Beng Mealea overtaken by the jungle](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Temple_%26_jungle._%283746632042%29.jpg/1280px-Temple_%26_jungle._%283746632042%29.jpg)

**First essential mission**: get your **official Angkor Pass** right away (online or through the hotel) to avoid lines at dawn. The **3-day pass** (valid for 10 days) is the ideal choice, because tomorrow you visit the main temples and you can return for Beng Mealea; there is also a **1-day pass**. Prices are updated periodically: check the current official rate and buy **only from the official ticket office (Angkor Enterprise)**, avoiding unauthorized resellers.

**Tuk-tuk for sunrise**: arrange **tonight itself** with your driver (or through the hotel) for tomorrow's departure at 5:00. Standard price: ~€15 for the full day.

**Rain alternative**: a visit to the Angkor National Museum ($12 entry) to prepare for the temples.

### Evening
**Dinner on Pub Street**: 
The street comes alive from 17:00 and becomes pedestrian-only from 19:00
. 

**Recommended restaurants**:
- **Red Piano** ([official site](https://redpianosiemp.com)) - 
Famous for the "Tomb Raider" cocktail, frequented by Angelina Jolie during filming

- **Le Tigre de Papier** - 
Wood-fired pizza, 24h delivery, Khmer cooking classes

- **Amara Dining Bar** - 
Great food and beer, excellent prices during happy hour

**Angkor Night Market**: a few minutes' walk from Pub Street, it's the city's historic night market and has over 200 vendors in traditional bamboo huts with Khmer handicrafts. Alternatively, nearby you'll find the **Siem Reap Art Center Night Market**, just as good. Prices are negotiable everywhere: be ready to haggle (the nearby Khmer Night Market is another option).

### Tips of the Day
- 
Draft beer at $0.75 in the Pub Street bars!

- 
Pregame with $0.75 beer anywhere on the street

- Avoid the "milk scam": 
women with children who lead you to buy expensive powdered milk only to resell it afterwards

- 
Pub Street is closed to traffic from 17:00

- **Optional extra (relaxation after the flight)**: a traditional Khmer massage rounds off the evening perfectly — ~$20 for 1 hour, with local herbs and hot compresses. A great way to loosen up your legs before tomorrow's long day at the temples.

## Day 6 — October 6, 2026 — Fiery Dawn over the Khmer

### Morning

**5:00 - Departure for sunrise**: 
You need to be at the Angkor Wat checkpoint by 5:15 for the sunrise
. 
The gates open at 5:00
 — arrive early to avoid the tour-bus crowds.

**Angkor Wat Sunrise** (⭐ must-do experience):
- Sunrise at Angkor Wat is **extremely crowded**: expect a lot of people and arrive early to secure a good spot
- Position yourself at the left reflecting pool for the best photos
- 
If you already have the pass, you can skip the ticket office and go straight to the temples

- 
After 17:30 visitors must leave the temples, although it is possible to stay until 18:00

**9:00 - Angkor Thom & Bayon**: 
- 
The Bayon is famous for its giant stone faces

- Enter through the South Gate with its 54 statues
- **Visit-order tip**: the **Bayon** is at its best **right after sunrise**, when the crowd shifts toward Angkor Wat — if you can, consider visiting it early, to find it quieter

**11:00 - Ta Prohm** ("Tomb Raider Temple"):
- 
Famous for the jungle-covered ruins, immortalized in Tomb Raider

- The tree roots literally sprout from the walls

**Tuk-tuk for the day**: your driver takes you from one temple to another and waits in the shade. Indicative cost ~€15 for the whole day (split by 4, it's just a few euros each).

### Afternoon
**Lunch**: refresh yourself at one of the restaurants near the temples or head back to the hotel for the pool. 

**Mandatory rest**: The midday heat is brutal — use this time wisely to recover your energy by the pool.

### Evening
A relaxing dinner and planning for tomorrow's day (Beng Mealea + floating village). 
$2 from every ticket is donated to the Kantha Bopha Children's Hospital
 — your money also serves a social purpose!

### Tips of the Day
- **Temple Pass**: there's a 1-day option and a 3-day one (valid for 10 days). The 3-day makes sense because, as planned, you return to the temples for Beng Mealea. The price is subject to change: check the current official rate on Angkor Enterprise
- **Water**: Bring at least 2 liters per person — October's heat and humidity dehydrate you fast
- **Dress code**: to enter the temples you need **covered shoulders and trousers or a skirt below the knee**, out of respect for the sacred sites. 
Children under 12 enter free but must show their passport

- Comfortable shoes are essential: you walk A LOT over uneven stones
- Bring a light raincoat: in October the afternoon downpours are frequent

## Day 7 — October 7, 2026 — Discovering the Jungle Temple

### Morning
**8:00 - Departure for Beng Mealea**: 
65 km from Siem Reap, 80-90 minutes by car on excellent paved roads
. 

**Beng Mealea** — The lost temple:
- 
Since 2020 it has been included in the standard Angkor Pass, or a separate $10 ticket

- 
"Lotus pond" in Khmer, completely overrun by the jungle where only local children play and birds visit

- 
Built by the same king who commissioned Angkor Wat, with a similar architectural style

- 
The best time is at dawn and in the two hours after, for magical light and the absence of tourists

**Transport costs**: for the whole day (Beng Mealea in the morning + floating village in the afternoon) budget **~€20** with a tuk-tuk, or a shared minivan if you prefer air conditioning on the longer stretches. The driver waits for you and takes you from one site to another.

**Local lunch**: 
Local food stalls near the east and west entrances of Beng Mealea for ~$2 (Nom Banh Chok or Bai Sach Chrouk)
. 
The Beng Mealea Restaurant & Lodge has an English menu and clean seating
.

### Afternoon
**Kampong Phluk — the floating village**

In the afternoon you head down toward **Tonlé Sap**, the great lake that swells in the rainy season and transforms the landscape. **Kampong Phluk** is the convenient choice for your schedule — it's closer and can be visited in half a day — and it's at its best **with high water**: in October, at the peak of the rainy season, levels are usually high. It offers a glimpse of lake life:
- Tall wooden **stilt houses** that in October seem to float on the high water
- A **boat** ride among the dwellings and through the flooded **mangrove forest** (often with a transfer to a small paddle canoe)
- Genuine local life and children getting around by boat
- In October the water level is high: it's the best time to see the village in its element

**An honest caveat**: Kampong Phluk can be quite touristy and busy with boats. If you happen to come during a period of **low water**, consider instead **Kampong Khleang**: more authentic and less crowded, though farther away.

Indicative price of the boat ride ~$20 per person (often negotiable or included in a package). Bring cash, a hat and sunscreen.

### Evening
Return to Siem Reap for dinner. If you still have energy, a second evening on Pub Street to try different spots. Pack your bag: tomorrow you set off toward the coast.

### Tips of the Day
- 
Entry to Beng Mealea costs an extra $5 if you don't have the full Angkor Pass

- 
Plan 2-3 hours to fully explore the ruins, following the wooden walkways

- Combine the two stops sensibly: first Beng Mealea in the morning (better light, fewer tourists), then Kampong Phluk in the afternoon, ideal for the sunset over the lake
- 
Don't touch the carvings or climb fragile walls: respect this ancient and sacred site

- At the floating village, agree on the boat price **before** getting on and avoid the insistent vendors at the docks
