# SIEM REAP & ANGKOR — DAY BY DAY

## Day 5 — October 5, 2026 — Arrival in the Khmer Kingdom

![Angkor Wat](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/2014-Cambodge_Angkor_Wat_%2821%29.jpg/1280px-2014-Cambodge_Angkor_Wat_%2821%29.jpg)

### Morning
An easy start in Bangkok: today you fly. After breakfast and check-out, your **private transfer** collects you from The Cotton Saladaeng and runs you to **Don Mueang (DMK)** (~40-60 min in the morning traffic). The **Thai AirAsia flight** leaves around **10:55** and has you in Cambodia in just over an hour (~€50 per person). Aim to be there for check-in **2.5-3 hours** ahead, so you can set off without a rush.

![Ta Prohm — the Tomb Raider temple](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Tree_roots_and_temple%2C_Ta_Prohm%2C_Angkor%2C_Cambodia.jpg/1280px-Tree_roots_and_temple%2C_Ta_Prohm%2C_Angkor%2C_Cambodia.jpg)

### Afternoon
**~12:15 - Landing in Siem Reap**: you touch down at the new Siem Reap–Angkor International Airport (SAI), about 50 km east of the city. 
From there it's roughly 45-60 minutes by road into the centre
. Taxis and tuk-tuks wait at the arrivals exit — agree the price before you set off, or book a transfer through the hotel.

Check in, freshen up, and give the rest of the afternoon over to resting and acclimatising. If you've still got the energy, take a wander through the centre to get your bearings: 
Pub Street runs for 100 metres between the Red Piano Restaurant and the Banana Leaf Restaurant
, and by day it's quiet — perfect for spotting the landmarks without the night-time chaos.

![Beng Mealea overtaken by the jungle](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Temple_%26_jungle._%283746632042%29.jpg/1280px-Temple_%26_jungle._%283746632042%29.jpg)

**First job of all**: sort out your **official Angkor Pass** straight away (online or through the hotel) so you're not queuing at dawn. The **3-day pass** (valid for 10 days) is the one to get, since you'll do the main temples tomorrow and can come back for Beng Mealea; there's a **1-day pass** too. Prices change from time to time, so check the current official rate and buy **only from the official ticket office (Angkor Enterprise)** — steer clear of unauthorised resellers.

**Tuk-tuk for sunrise**: fix it up **this very evening** with your driver (or through the hotel) for a 5:00 start tomorrow. The going rate is ~€15 for the full day.

**Rain alternative**: a visit to the Angkor National Museum ($12 entry) to prepare for the temples.

### Evening
**Dinner on Pub Street**: 
the street comes alive from 17:00 and goes pedestrian-only from 19:00
. 

**Recommended restaurants**:
- **Red Piano** ([official site](https://redpianosiemp.com)) - 
Famous for the "Tomb Raider" cocktail, frequented by Angelina Jolie during filming

- **Le Tigre de Papier** - 
Wood-fired pizza, 24h delivery, Khmer cooking classes

- **Amara Dining Bar** - 
Great food and beer, excellent prices during happy hour

**Angkor Night Market**: a few minutes' walk from Pub Street, this is the city's original night market, with over 200 vendors selling Khmer handicrafts from traditional bamboo huts. The **Siem Reap Art Center Night Market** nearby is just as good. Prices are negotiable everywhere, so be ready to haggle (the nearby Khmer Night Market is another option).

### Tips of the Day
- 
Draft beer at $0.75 in the Pub Street bars!

- 
Pregame with $0.75 beer anywhere on the street

- Watch out for the "milk scam": 
women with children who steer you into buying expensive powdered milk, only to resell it afterwards

- 
Pub Street closes to traffic from 17:00

- **Optional extra (to unwind after the flight)**: a traditional Khmer massage is the perfect way to round off the evening — ~$20 for 1 hour, with local herbs and hot compresses. It'll loosen up your legs nicely before tomorrow's long day at the temples.

## Day 6 — October 6, 2026 — Fiery Dawn over Angkor

### Morning

**5:00 - Off for sunrise**: 
you need to be at the Angkor Wat checkpoint by 5:15 for the sunrise
. 
The gates open at 5:00
 — get there early to beat the tour-bus crowds.

**Angkor Wat Sunrise** (⭐ must-do experience):
- Sunrise at Angkor Wat is **extremely crowded**: expect a lot of people, and arrive early to claim a good spot
- Set up at the left-hand reflecting pool for the best photos
- 
If you already have your pass, skip the ticket office and head straight for the temples

- 
Visitors must leave the temples after 17:30, though you can linger until 18:00

**9:00 - Angkor Thom & Bayon**: 
- 
the Bayon is famous for its giant stone faces

- Enter through the South Gate, lined with its 54 statues
- **Visit-order tip**: the **Bayon** is at its best **just after sunrise**, when the crowd drifts off towards Angkor Wat — if you can, come early and you'll find it quieter

**11:00 - Ta Prohm** ("Tomb Raider Temple"):
- 
famous for its jungle-swallowed ruins, immortalised in Tomb Raider

- The tree roots spill straight out of the walls

**Tuk-tuk for the day**: your driver ferries you from temple to temple and waits in the shade. Reckon on ~€15 for the whole day (split four ways, it's just a few euros each).

### Afternoon
**Lunch**: cool off at one of the restaurants near the temples, or head back to the hotel for the pool. 

**Rest is non-negotiable**: the midday heat is brutal, so use this time to recharge by the pool.

### Evening
A relaxed dinner and some planning for tomorrow (Beng Mealea + floating village). 
$2 from every ticket goes to the Kantha Bopha Children's Hospital
 — so your money does some good, too.

### Tips of the Day
- **Temple Pass**: there's a 1-day option and a 3-day one (valid for 10 days). The 3-day makes sense because, as planned, you return to the temples for Beng Mealea. The price is subject to change: check the current official rate on Angkor Enterprise
- **Water**: Bring at least 2 liters per person — October's heat and humidity dehydrate you fast
- **Dress code**: to enter the temples you need **covered shoulders and trousers or a skirt below the knee**, out of respect for these sacred sites. 
Under-12s get in free but must show their passport

- Comfortable shoes are essential — you cover a LOT of ground over uneven stone
- Pack a light raincoat: the afternoon downpours are frequent in October

## Day 7 — October 7, 2026 — Discovering the Jungle Temple

### Morning
**8:00 - Off to Beng Mealea**: 
65 km from Siem Reap, 80-90 minutes by car on excellent paved roads
. 

**Beng Mealea** — the lost temple:
- 
since 2020 it's been covered by the standard Angkor Pass, or a separate $10 ticket

- 
"Lotus pond" in Khmer, utterly swallowed by the jungle, with only local children at play and birds for company

- 
built by the same king who commissioned Angkor Wat, in a similar architectural style

- 
best visited at dawn and in the two hours after, for the magical light and the lack of tourists

**Transport costs**: for the whole day (Beng Mealea in the morning + the floating village in the afternoon) budget **~€20** with a tuk-tuk, or a shared minivan if you'd rather have air conditioning for the longer stretches. The driver waits for you and shuttles you from site to site.

**Local lunch**: 
food stalls near the east and west entrances of Beng Mealea, around $2 (Nom Banh Chok or Bai Sach Chrouk)
. 
The Beng Mealea Restaurant & Lodge has an English menu and clean seating
.

### Afternoon
**Kampong Phluk — the floating village**

In the afternoon you head down towards **Tonlé Sap**, the great lake that swells in the rainy season and remakes the landscape. **Kampong Phluk** is the practical choice for your schedule — it's closer and doable in half a day — and it's at its best **when the water is high**: in October, at the height of the rains, levels are usually up. It gives you a real glimpse of life on the lake:
- Tall wooden **stilt houses** that in October look as if they're floating on the high water
- A **boat** trip weaving between the houses and through the flooded **mangrove forest** (often with a switch to a small paddle canoe)
- Genuine local life, with children getting about by boat
- The high October water is the best time to catch the village in its element

**One honest caveat**: Kampong Phluk can get touristy and busy with boats. If you happen to arrive during a spell of **low water**, go for **Kampong Khleang** instead: more authentic and less crowded, though farther out.

Reckon on ~$20 per person for the boat trip (often negotiable, or included in a package). Bring cash, a hat and sunscreen.

### Evening
Head back to Siem Reap for dinner. If you're still up for it, spend a second evening on Pub Street trying somewhere new. Then pack your bag: tomorrow you set off for the coast.

### Tips of the Day
- 
Entry to Beng Mealea costs an extra $5 if you don't have the full Angkor Pass

- 
Allow 2-3 hours to explore the ruins properly, following the wooden walkways

- Pace the two stops sensibly: Beng Mealea first thing (better light, fewer tourists), then Kampong Phluk in the afternoon, ideal for sunset over the lake
- 
Don't touch the carvings or climb the fragile walls — respect this ancient, sacred site

- At the floating village, agree the boat price **before** you get on and brush off the pushy vendors at the docks
