## Practical Info — Siem Reap & Angkor

### Getting There

#### Flight Bangkok → Siem Reap

You get to Siem Reap from Thailand on a **Thai AirAsia flight** out of Bangkok. It leaves **Don Mueang (DMK)** around **10:55** and lands at the new **Siem Reap–Angkor International Airport (SAI)** around **12:15** — just over an hour in the air for about **€50 per person**. 
The new airport sits about 50 km east of the city
.

**Recommended airlines:**
- **Thai AirAsia** — 
the cheapest fares on the Bangkok–Siem Reap route
 (flights from Don Mueang DMK)
  - Bookings: [Thai AirAsia](https://www.airasia.com/)
- **Bangkok Airways** — 
more comfortable, but pricier

- **Cambodia Angkor Air** — 
Cambodia's flag carrier

  - Price comparison: [Google Flights](https://flights.google.com/)

**From the airport to the center (transfer ~45-60 min):**
- 
From the new airport it's about 45-60 minutes by road (~50 km)

- 
Tuk-tuks and taxis wait at the arrivals exit — agree the price beforehand

- 
Many hotels lay on transfers on request

### Moving On — to Koh Rong Samloem (October 8)

On October 8 you leave Siem Reap for the island of **Koh Rong Samloem**, on the southern coast. It's a two-leg journey: 
first a van/bus from Siem Reap to Sihanoukville, then the fast ferry across to the island
. 
The road leg is long (set aside most of the day), so book your seats and the ferry at least a day ahead — in the rainy season ferry times can shift
. Choose a departure that gets you into Sihanoukville in time for the last afternoon fast ferry.

### Getting Around

#### Tuk-Tuk (Recommended)

Tuk-tuks run about $2 for short hops around town and $6 from the airport to the centre
. 
As a rough guide: $1-2 (1-2km), $2-3 (2-5km), $5-8 for a return trip with waiting, and $15 for a full day at the Angkor temples
.

**Angkor temple fares:**
- 
full day at the main temples: $25 (split four ways, that's €6.25 each!)

- 
farther-flung temples like Banteay Srei: $23-26


**Insider trick:** 
grab your driver's number so you can call him again
, and 
always haggle — the golden rule is to halve whatever price you're first quoted
.

#### Grab

Grab works here, with GrabCar, GrabTukTuk and GrabBike
. 
Prices are clear and fixed before you book
 — perfect if you don't like haggling.

#### Bicycles

Bike hire runs to less than $5 a day at the Old Market, and some hotels lend them out for free
.

### Where to Stay

**Budget: €22/night for a double room**

#### 1. **Mad Monkey Hostel Siem Reap**

A hostel with a pool, bar and organised activities on Sivatha Boulevard
. Its private double room is perfect for your budget.
- [Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/kh/mad-monkey-hostel-siem-reap.html) | [Hostelworld](https://www.hostelworld.com)

#### 2. **Parklane Guesthouse (Wat Bo Village)**

A guesthouse in traditional Khmer style with a garden, and owners who'll sort you out with reliable tuk-tuks
. 
Quiet neighbourhood, 10-15 minutes' walk from the centre, or $2-3 by tuk-tuk
.

#### 3. **Two Dragons Guesthouse**

Spacious rooms, strong WiFi and a central spot near the Old Market and the restaurants
.

#### 4. **Golden Temple Villa**

Pairs budget prices with a pool — a rare thing at this level
. 
Private doubles $20-35 with AC, en-suite bathroom and breakfast included
.

**Where to book:**
- [Planet of Hotels](https://en.planetofhotels.com/cambodia/siem-reap/guest-houses) — guesthouses from $10
- [Kayak](https://www.kayak.com/Siem-Reap-Hotels) — hotels from $6/night

### Connectivity & SIM

#### Local SIMs (Best Option)

A local SIM costs $5-10 for a huge data bundle that would set you back $50-100 in Europe or North America
.

**Recommended operators:**
- **Smart Axiata** — 
the market leader with tourists. Traveller SIM package: $5 for 30GB, valid 15 days

- **Metfone** — 
best overall coverage; SIM card $1-2

- **Cellcard** — 
Tourist SIM Pack: $6 for unlimited data, minutes and SMS over 7 days


**Where to buy:**
- 
Siem Reap airport: all the main operators have official stands in the arrivals hall

- 
convenience stores in town (though prices are higher)


**Documents:** 
you'll need your passport to register, and they may ask for your Cambodian visa
.

#### WiFi

WiFi is widely available in Western-style hotels, guesthouses, cafés and restaurants in Siem Reap
. 
Out in rural areas it's patchier and slower
.

### Safety

#### General Situation

Cambodia is generally safe for tourists, and Siem Reap especially so. Take common-sense precautions and most visits pass without a hitch
. 
Siem Reap is probably the safest place in the country, well ahead of Phnom Penh
.

#### Common Scams to Avoid
- **Baby Milk Scam** — 
women with children ask you to buy powdered milk; the child is often not theirs

- **Fake Orphanages** — 
bogus orphanage tours run to extract donations

- **Tuk-tuk Overcharging** — 
drivers who pad out the route to bump up the fare

- **Massage Bill Swap** — 
in cheap massage parlours they switch your real banknotes for fakes


#### Practical Precautions
- 
Wear cross-body bags with the zip facing inwards

- 
Walk on the side away from the road to avoid bag-snatchers on motorbikes

- 
Don't get your phone out near the road or on open tuk-tuks

- 
Don't flash large amounts of cash or expensive jewellery

- 
Keep documents and valuables in the hotel safe, carry only the cash you need, and insure your phone and camera


#### Areas to Avoid/Watch Out For
- 
Landmine areas: remote zones outside Siem Reap city and the Angkor temples (which are safe)

- 
Pub Street is lively but safe; steer clear of isolated beaches after dark

- 
Take extra care during festivals (Khmer New Year in April, Pchum Ben in October/November), when pickpocketing rises


#### Emergency Numbers
- **Police:** 117
- **Siem Reap Tourist Police:** 
office opposite the ticket offices of the Angkor Archaeological Park

- **Medical Emergency:** 119
- **Fire Brigade:** 118
- **Italian Embassy:** +855 23 217 306 (Phnom Penh)

**Important:** 
medical facilities in Cambodia are poor, bar a handful of foreign-run hospitals in Phnom Penh and Siem Reap. For anything serious, consider evacuation to Thailand or Singapore
.
