## Practical Info — Siem Reap & Angkor

### Getting There

#### Flight Bangkok → Siem Reap

You reach Siem Reap from Thailand with a **Thai AirAsia flight** from Bangkok. The flight departs from **Don Mueang (DMK)** around **10:55** and lands at the new **Siem Reap–Angkor International Airport (SAI)** around **12:15**: just over an hour of flying for about **€50 per person**. 
The new airport is located about 50 km east of the city
.

**Recommended airlines:**
- **Thai AirAsia** — 
the cheapest fares on the Bangkok–Siem Reap route
 (flights from Don Mueang DMK)
  - Bookings: [Thai AirAsia](https://www.airasia.com/)
- **Bangkok Airways** — 
more comfortable but expensive

- **Cambodia Angkor Air** — 
the Cambodian flag carrier

  - Price comparison: [Google Flights](https://flights.google.com/)

**From the airport to the center (transfer ~45-60 min):**
- 
From the new airport it takes about 45-60 minutes by road (~50 km)

- 
Tuk-tuks or taxis available at the arrivals exit; agree on the price in advance

- 
Many hotels offer transfers on request

### Moving On — to Koh Rong Samloem (October 8)

On October 8 you leave Siem Reap to reach the island of **Koh Rong Samloem**, on the southern coast. The route is in two legs: 
first a van/bus from Siem Reap to Sihanoukville, then the fast ferry from Sihanoukville to the island
. 
The van/bus journey is long (budget most of the day), so book seats and ferry at least a day in advance — in the rainy season ferry schedules can vary
. Book a ride that drops you in Sihanoukville in time for the last afternoon fast ferry.

### Getting Around

#### Tuk-Tuk (Recommended)

Tuk-tuks cost about $2 for short trips in the city and $6 from the airport to the center
. 
Reference prices: $1-2 (1-2km), $2-3 (2-5km), $5-8 round trip with waiting, $15 for a day at the Angkor temples
.

**Angkor temple fares:**
- 
Full day at the main temples: $25 (split by 4 = €6.25 each!)

- 
Distant temples like Banteay Srei: $23-26


**Insider trick:** 
Take your driver's number so you can call him again
 and 
always negotiate — golden rule: divide the initial price by two
.

#### Grab

Grab is available with GrabCar, GrabTukTuk and GrabBike
. 
Transparent and fixed prices before booking
 — perfect if you don't like haggling.

#### Bicycles

Bike rental: less than $5 per day, available at the Old Market. Some hotels provide them for free
.

### Where to Stay

**Budget: €22/night for a double room**

#### 1. **Mad Monkey Hostel Siem Reap**

Hostel with pool, bar and organized activities on Sivatha Boulevard
. A private double room perfect for your budget.
- [Booking.com](https://www.booking.com/hotel/kh/mad-monkey-hostel-siem-reap.html) | [Hostelworld](https://www.hostelworld.com)

#### 2. **Parklane Guesthouse (Wat Bo Village)**

Guesthouse in traditional Khmer architecture with a garden and owners who help with reliable tuk-tuks
. 
Quiet neighborhood 10-15 minutes' walk from the center, tuk-tuk $2-3
.

#### 3. **Two Dragons Guesthouse**

Spacious rooms, strong WiFi and a central location near the Old Market and restaurants
.

#### 4. **Golden Temple Villa**

Combines budget prices with a pool, a rare thing in this price range
. 
Private double rooms $20-35 with AC, private bathroom and breakfast included
.

**Where to book:**
- [Planet of Hotels](https://en.planetofhotels.com/cambodia/siem-reap/guest-houses) — guesthouses from $10
- [Kayak](https://www.kayak.com/Siem-Reap-Hotels) — hotels from $6/night

### Connectivity & SIM

#### Local SIMs (Best Option)

Local SIMs cost $5-10 for huge data packages that in Europe/North America would cost $50-100
.

**Recommended operators:**
- **Smart Axiata** — 
Dominant in the tourist market. Traveller SIM package: $5 for 30GB valid for 15 days

- **Metfone** — 
Best overall coverage, SIM card $1-2

- **Cellcard** — 
Tourist SIM Pack: $6 for unlimited data, minutes and SMS for 7 days


**Where to buy:**
- 
Siem Reap airport: all the main operators have official stands in the arrivals terminals

- 
Convenience stores in the city (but higher prices)


**Documents:** 
Passport required for registration. A Cambodian visa may be requested
.

#### WiFi

WiFi is widely available in Western-style hotels, guesthouses, cafés and restaurants in Siem Reap
. 
Variable and limited quality in rural areas
.

### Safety

#### General Situation

Cambodia is generally safe for tourists, especially in Siem Reap. Most visits are trouble-free if you use common-sense precautions
. 
Siem Reap is probably the safest place in Cambodia, much safer than Phnom Penh
.

#### Common Scams to Avoid
- **Baby Milk Scam** — 
Women with children ask you to buy powdered milk. The child is often not theirs

- **Fake Orphanages** — 
Fake orphanage tours for donations

- **Tuk-tuk Overcharging** — 
Drivers who lengthen the route to increase the price

- **Massage Bill Swap** — 
In cheap massage parlours they swap real banknotes for fake ones


#### Practical Precautions
- 
Use cross-body bags with the zip facing inward

- 
Walk on the side away from the road to avoid bag snatching from motorbikes

- 
Don't take out your phone near the road or on open tuk-tuks

- 
Don't flaunt large amounts of cash or expensive jewelry

- 
Keep documents and valuables in the hotel safe. Carry only cash and insure your phone/camera


#### Areas to Avoid/Watch Out For
- 
Mined areas: remote zones outside Siem Reap city and the Angkor temples (which are safe)

- 
Pub Street is lively but safe. Avoid isolated beaches at night

- 
Take extra care during festivals (Khmer New Year in April, Pchum Ben October/November) when pickpocketing increases


#### Emergency Numbers
- **Police:** 117
- **Siem Reap Tourist Police:** 
Office across from the ticket offices of the Angkor Archaeological Park

- **Medical Emergency:** 119
- **Fire Brigade:** 118
- **Italian Embassy:** +855 23 217 306 (Phnom Penh)

**Important:** 
Medical facilities are poor in Cambodia except for a few foreign hospitals in Phnom Penh and Siem Reap. For serious medical problems consider evacuation to Thailand or Singapore
.
