# DAY BY DAY — Ho Chi Minh City

## Day 14 — October 14, 2026 — First taste of Saigon

### Morning
**Arrival and check-in**

![The Central Post Office of Saigon](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Lignes_t%C3%A9l%C3%A9graphiques_du_Sud_Vietnam_et_du_Cambodge_%281936%29%2C_Saigon_Central_Post_Office.JPG/1280px-Lignes_t%C3%A9l%C3%A9graphiques_du_Sud_Vietnam_et_du_Cambodge_%281936%29%2C_Saigon_Central_Post_Office.JPG)

The bus from Chau Doc to Ho Chi Minh City takes about 6 hours
, so, leaving early from the Mekong Delta, you'll arrive in the late morning. After checking in at your accommodation in District 1, start exploring right away in the historic heart of the city.

**Saigon Central Post Office** (09:00-11:00)

Start at the Saigon Central Post Office, a magnificent French colonial building known for its high ceilings and vintage design, right across from Notre-Dame Cathedral with its red-brick façade and twin towers
.

**Official site:** [Vietnam Post](https://www.vnpost.vn/en-us)  
**Hours:** 7:00-19:00 every day  
**Admission:** Free  

### Afternoon
**Reunification Palace** (13:30-15:30)

![The Cu Chi Tunnels](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Cu_Chi_Tunnels_Vietnam_war.jpg/1280px-Cu_Chi_Tunnels_Vietnam_war.jpg)

Also known as the Independence Palace, it was here that the Vietnam War officially ended in 1975. Today it stands as a symbol of the strength of the Vietnamese people
. 
You'll find underground bunkers, elegant state rooms and even a cinema space, with the reunification hall as the centerpiece. I recommend getting the audio guide for a deeper understanding
.

**Hours:** 7:30-11:00 and 13:00-16:00  
**Ticket:** 40,000 VND (~€1.50)  
**Site:** [Independence Palace](http://dinhdoclap.gov.vn/)

**If it rains:** Visit the **Ben Thanh Market** for indoor shopping.

### Evening
**Authentic dinner in Districts 3-4**

Avoid the tourist traps of District 1 and head to Districts 3-4 for an authentic dining experience.

**Top 3 local restaurants:**

1. **Hủ tiếu Nam Vang Trung Còi** (District 3)  
   
This small shop serves noodles both dry and in soup form, Cambodian-style. It's widely recognized by locals as a neighborhood favorite. Add all the vegetables and drink plenty of the pandan tea they make fresh in-house
.  
   **Address:** Cao Thắng, Phường 4, District 3  
   **Price:** 50,000-70,000 VND (~€2-3)

2. **Phở 66B Cao Thắng** (District 3)  
   
Excellent simple and genuine beef phở. It's very popular during breakfast hours
.  
   **Address:** 66B Cao Thắng, Phường 4, Quận 3  
   **Price:** 60,000-80,000 VND (~€2.50-3.50)

3. **Bún Bò Ganh** (District 3)  
   
Born from the dream of a cooking enthusiast who wanted to bring together Vietnamese cuisine in one place. Here you can savor authentic flavors like the Special Hue Beef Soup and a variety of other regional delicacies
.  
   **Address:** 16 Le Quy Don, Vo Thi Sau Ward, District 3

### Tips of the Day
- **Grab App:** Download Grab to get around easily - it's safer and more convenient
- **Local currency:** 
Admission tickets to the War Remnants Museum cost 40,000 VND (about 1.52 USD) in 2025
 - always keep Vietnamese cash on hand
- **Weather:** October is ideal - dry weather and pleasant temperatures (25-30°C)
- **Language:** English is spoken at the main tourist sites, but learn "xin chào" (hello) and "cám ơn" (thank you)

## Day 15 — October 15, 2026 — History and underground tunnels

### Morning
**War Remnants Museum** (08:00-10:30)

The museum is open every day from 7:30 to 17:30, including weekends and holidays. However, ticket sales end at 11:00 for the morning session and at 17:00 for the afternoon one
.

Adult tickets cost 40,000 VND, children between 6 and 15 years old 15,000 VND, and children under 6 free
. 
If you visit, plan to spend about 1.5 hours here
.

**Address:** 28 Vo Van Tan Street, District 3  
**How to get there:** 
The museum is located 2.3 km from the city center. Its strategic location makes it easily accessible by taxi, motorbike or public transport. Nearby landmarks include the Independence Palace and Notre-Dame Cathedral

### Afternoon  
**Cu Chi Tunnels - Ben Duoc** (13:00-18:00)

Ben Duoc offers a superior experience for most travelers in 2026 with significantly fewer crowds, more authentic tunnels and an exploration area five times larger (100 hectares versus Ben Dinh's 17 hectares), despite requiring an additional 30 minutes of travel. Ben Duoc preserves tunnels closer to their original wartime dimensions, has a memorial temple and positions the shooting range separately to maintain tranquility
.

**2026 tour costs:**
- 
Group tour: $16-25 USD for half-day experiences including transport

- 
Admission ticket: 110,000 VND (about $4.40 USD) at both Ben Dinh and Ben Duoc sites. Includes site access, introductory film and guided walk through the tunnels

- 
Optional shooting range: 40,000-60,000 VND ($1.60-2.40 USD) per bullet with a minimum of 5-10 bullets

**Recommended tours:**
- **Viator Cu Chi Ben Duoc (max 11 people):** [Book here](https://www.viator.com/)
- **GetYourGuide Cu Chi Half-day:** [Book here](https://www.getyourguide.com/)

**If it rains:** The tunnels are always accessible. Bring a light raincoat.

### Evening
**Return and local dinner** (19:00-21:00)

After the tour, unwind with a dinner at one of these authentic spots:

**Kiều Bảo Barbecue Rice Noodles**  

Of all the restaurants I tried here, Kiều Bảo is without a doubt my favorite. The local crowd I saw gathering in front of this place every evening caught my attention. Their barbecue rice noodles are so incredibly good that I couldn't stop eating them for three nights in a row
.

**Phở Hùng**  

This popular Phở restaurant, located on the edge of the Pham Ngu Lao tourist quarter, is one of the best places to try this famous dish. Their dishes are on the pricey side, with a small bowl starting at 70,000 VND (2.74 USD), but they serve generous portions. The restaurant is open every day from 6:00 to 3:00
.

### Tips of the Day
- **Claustrophobia:** 
If you suffer from even a little claustrophobia I don't recommend joining the tunnel section. It's very hot and often crowded inside

- **Clothing:** Comfortable clothes and closed shoes for the tunnels - not open sandals
- **Water:** Always bring a bottle of water - it's hot in the tunnels
- **Photography:** 
Photography is allowed, but please be respectful of the exhibits and other visitors

- **History:** 
The Cu Chi Tunnels are a 250km underground city built by hand under B-52 bombing. Walking even just 50 meters underground gives you a physical understanding of what the people endured that no documentary can replicate

## Day 16 — October 16, 2026 — Chinatown and drinks with a view

### Morning
**Cho Lon (Chinatown)** (08:00-12:00)

**Binh Tay Market** (08:00-10:00)  

The market is open every day from early morning until late afternoon, typically from 6:00 to 18:00. To experience the lively atmosphere and see the market in full swing, it's best to visit during the morning hours when locals often come to shop
.

**Address:** 
57A, Thap Muoi Street, Ward 2, District 6. Hours: 6:00 - 7:30 pm
  
**How to get there:** 
The most convenient option is to take a taxi, which usually costs around 70,000-100,000 VND (3-5 USD) from the city center

**Thien Hau Pagoda** (10:30-11:30)  

Dedicated to the goddess Mazu (Goddess of the Sea), built in 1760 (over 260 years ago). Traditional Chinese architecture with Vietnamese elements, elaborate wood carvings, hanging incense coils. What to see: intricate ceiling carvings, ceramic dioramas depicting Chinese mythology
.

Temple etiquette: remove your shoes before entering certain areas, dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees), ask permission before photographing, observe in silence during prayers, small donations appreciated (10,000 VND recommended)
.

### Afternoon
**Exploring Chinatown** (13:00-16:00)

**Suggested itinerary:**

Binh Tay Market - Thien Hau Pagoda (on foot) - Quan Am Pagoda - Hao Si Phuong Alley - Lunch destination

**Quan Am Pagoda**  

Dedicated to the Goddess of Mercy (Avalokitesvara), built in the 19th century. Architecture: arched entrance, a mix of Buddhist and Taoist elements. Interior: colorful statues, decorations, spiritual atmosphere
. Time needed: 15-20 minutes.

**Local lunch:** **Ba Lu Coffee**  

For a coffee break, try Ba Lu Coffee, an old café famous for its traditional roasting. The coffee is quite strong so try it in the morning or early afternoon if you care about your sleep
.

**If it rains:** Chinatown has many covered temples and markets to explore.

### Evening  
**Rooftop Bars and Bui Vien Street** (18:00-22:00)

**Sunset Rooftop (18:00-20:00):**

**The View Rooftop Bar Bui Vien**  

Located above the lively Bui Vien, The View Rooftop Bar offers a relaxed yet vibrant escape with stunning city views. Whether you're watching the sunset or enjoying the sparkling skyline at night, this place is perfect for unwinding. The cocktails are creative, well-made and reasonably priced. It's a must if you visit Bui Vien
.  
**Address:** 
9th floor of the Duc Vuong Hotel, 195 Bui Vien

**Chill Skybar**  

The most famous includes Chill Skybar on the 26th floor of the AB Tower (Saigon's most glamorous nightspot)
. 
Located in District 1, Chill Skybar offers stunning panoramic views of the city from its rooftop terrace. It has a trendy and sophisticated atmosphere, making it a popular spot for both locals and tourists
.

**Alternatives:**
- **Saigon Saigon Bar** (Caravelle Hotel): 
Where war correspondents watched the conflict unfold

- **Social Club** (Hotel des Arts): Refined and creative cocktails

**Bui Vien Street Night Experience (20:00-22:00)**

The legendary backpacker strip of Saigon. Every evening from 19:00, the street is closed to traffic and transforms into a pulsing open-air party that doesn't stop until 2:00 (and sometimes later). Bars and restaurants line both sides, pouring hundreds of plastic chairs onto the street
.

Vendors move through the crowd selling bia hoi (fresh draft beer) for 10,000-15,000₫ a glass - about $0.40. Music blasts from competing sound systems. Fire dancers, street artists and spontaneous dance circles add to the chaos
.

### Tips of the Day
- **Transport in Chinatown:** 
Grab: 100,000-150,000 VND ($4-6 USD) depending on traffic, 10-15 minutes (morning) or 15-25 minutes (evening)

- **Rooftop Dress Code:** 
Most rooftop bars enforce a smart-casual dress code - no shorts, flip-flops or tank tops

- **Cocktail Prices:** 
Cocktails range from 180,000-350,000₫, but the views are free

- **Bui Vien Safety:** 
Keep your wallet close, some people can be pushy about getting you into bars. The pretty girls who talk to you aren't actually interested in you, they just want your money. Do your research and stay aware of your surroundings

- **Best Time Rooftop:** 
Best at sunset (17:30-19:00) for the best views; after 21:00 for the atmosphere

**Estimated daily budget for 4 people:**
- Transport: 400,000 VND (~€15)
- Temple admissions: Free (voluntary donations)
- Chinatown lunch: 800,000 VND (~€30)
- Rooftop drinks: 1,200,000 VND (~€45) 
- Bui Vien dinner: 600,000 VND (~€22)
- **Total: ~€112 for 4 people**

This concludes your three days in Ho Chi Minh City with a perfect mix of history, culture, authentic food and nightlife!
