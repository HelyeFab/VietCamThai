# DAY BY DAY — Ho Chi Minh City

## Day 14 — October 14, 2026 — First Taste of Saigon

### Morning
**Arrival and check-in**

![The Central Post Office of Saigon](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Lignes_t%C3%A9l%C3%A9graphiques_du_Sud_Vietnam_et_du_Cambodge_%281936%29%2C_Saigon_Central_Post_Office.JPG/1280px-Lignes_t%C3%A9l%C3%A9graphiques_du_Sud_Vietnam_et_du_Cambodge_%281936%29%2C_Saigon_Central_Post_Office.JPG)

The bus from Chau Doc to Ho Chi Minh City takes about 6 hours, so an early start from the Mekong Delta gets you in by late morning. Once you've checked in at your accommodation in District 1, head straight out to explore the historic heart of the city.

**Saigon Central Post Office** (09:00-11:00)

Start at the Saigon Central Post Office, a magnificent French colonial building famous for its soaring ceilings and vintage design, right across from Notre-Dame Cathedral with its red-brick façade and twin towers.

**Official site:** [Vietnam Post](https://www.vnpost.vn/en-us)  
**Hours:** 7:00-19:00 every day  
**Admission:** Free  

### Afternoon
**Reunification Palace** (13:30-15:30)

![The Cu Chi Tunnels](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Cu_Chi_Tunnels_Vietnam_war.jpg/1280px-Cu_Chi_Tunnels_Vietnam_war.jpg)

Also known as the Independence Palace, this is where the Vietnam War officially ended in 1975. Today it stands as a symbol of the strength of the Vietnamese people. Inside you'll find underground bunkers, elegant state rooms and even a cinema, with the reunification hall as the centrepiece. It's worth getting the audio guide to make more sense of it all.

**Hours:** 7:30-11:00 and 13:00-16:00  
**Ticket:** 40,000 VND (~€1.50)  
**Site:** [Independence Palace](http://dinhdoclap.gov.vn/)

**If it rains:** Visit the **Ben Thanh Market** for indoor shopping.

### Evening
**Authentic dinner in Districts 3-4**

Avoid the tourist traps of District 1 and head to Districts 3-4 for an authentic dining experience.

**Top 3 local restaurants:**

1. **Hủ tiếu Nam Vang Trung Còi** (District 3)  
   
This little shop serves Cambodian-style noodles both dry and in soup, and locals rate it as a neighbourhood favourite. Pile on all the vegetables and drink plenty of the pandan tea they brew fresh on site.  
   **Address:** Cao Thắng, Phường 4, District 3  
   **Price:** 50,000-70,000 VND (~€2-3)

2. **Phở 66B Cao Thắng** (District 3)  
   
Excellent, simple, honest beef phở, and hugely popular at breakfast time.  
   **Address:** 66B Cao Thắng, Phường 4, Quận 3  
   **Price:** 60,000-80,000 VND (~€2.50-3.50)

3. **Bún Bò Ganh** (District 3)  
   
Born from one food lover's dream of bringing Vietnamese cuisine together under one roof. Here you can savour authentic flavours like the Special Hue Beef Soup, along with a range of other regional delicacies.  
   **Address:** 16 Le Quy Don, Vo Thi Sau Ward, District 3

### Tips of the Day
- **Grab App:** Download Grab to get around easily — it's safer and far more convenient
- **Local currency:** 
Admission tickets to the War Remnants Museum cost 40,000 VND (about 1.52 USD) in 2025 — always keep some Vietnamese cash on hand
- **Weather:** October is ideal — dry weather and pleasant temperatures (25-30°C)
- **Language:** English is spoken at the main tourist sites, but learn "xin chào" (hello) and "cám ơn" (thank you)

## Day 15 — October 15, 2026 — History and Underground Tunnels

### Morning
**War Remnants Museum** (08:00-10:30)

The museum is open every day from 7:30 to 17:30, weekends and holidays included. Note, though, that ticket sales stop at 11:00 for the morning session and at 17:00 for the afternoon one.

Adult tickets cost 40,000 VND, children aged 6 to 15 pay 15,000 VND, and under-6s go free. Plan on spending about 1.5 hours here.

**Address:** 28 Vo Van Tan Street, District 3  
**How to get there:** 
The museum sits 2.3 km from the city centre and is easy to reach by taxi, motorbike or public transport. Nearby landmarks include the Independence Palace and Notre-Dame Cathedral

### Afternoon  
**Cu Chi Tunnels - Ben Duoc** (13:00-18:00)

Ben Duoc is the better bet for most travellers in 2026: far fewer crowds, more authentic tunnels and an exploration area five times larger (100 hectares against Ben Dinh's 17), even if it does add 30 minutes to the journey. Ben Duoc keeps its tunnels closer to their original wartime dimensions, has a memorial temple and sets the shooting range apart to keep the peace.

**2026 tour costs:**
- 
Group tour: $16-25 USD for a half-day, including transport

- 
Admission ticket: 110,000 VND (about $4.40 USD) at both the Ben Dinh and Ben Duoc sites, covering site access, the introductory film and a guided walk through the tunnels

- 
Optional shooting range: 40,000-60,000 VND ($1.60-2.40 USD) per bullet with a minimum of 5-10 bullets

**Recommended tours:**
- **Viator Cu Chi Ben Duoc (max 11 people):** [Book here](https://www.viator.com/)
- **GetYourGuide Cu Chi Half-day:** [Book here](https://www.getyourguide.com/)

**If it rains:** The tunnels are always accessible. Bring a light raincoat.

### Evening
**Return and local dinner** (19:00-21:00)

After the tour, unwind with a dinner at one of these authentic spots:

**Kiều Bảo Barbecue Rice Noodles**  

Of all the places I tried here, Kiều Bảo is hands down my favourite. What first caught my eye was the local crowd gathering out front every evening. Their barbecue rice noodles are so ridiculously good that I ate them three nights running.

**Phở Hùng**  

This popular phở spot, on the edge of the Pham Ngu Lao tourist quarter, is one of the best places to try the dish. Prices run a little high — a small bowl starts at 70,000 VND (2.74 USD) — but the portions are generous. It's open every day from 6:00 to 3:00.

### Tips of the Day
- **Claustrophobia:** 
If you're even mildly claustrophobic, give the tunnel section a miss — it's very hot and often crowded down there

- **Clothing:** Comfortable clothes and closed shoes for the tunnels - not open sandals
- **Water:** Always bring a bottle of water - it's hot in the tunnels
- **Photography:** 
Photography is allowed, but do be respectful of the exhibits and other visitors

- **History:** 
The Cu Chi Tunnels are a 250km underground city dug by hand under B-52 bombing. Walking even 50 metres of them gives you a visceral sense of what people endured here that no documentary can match

## Day 16 — October 16, 2026 — Chinatown and Drinks with a View

### Morning
**Cho Lon (Chinatown)** (08:00-12:00)

**Binh Tay Market** (08:00-10:00)  

The market is open every day from early morning until late afternoon, typically 6:00 to 18:00. To catch it at its liveliest and in full swing, come in the morning, when the locals do their shopping.

**Address:** 
57A, Thap Muoi Street, Ward 2, District 6. Hours: 6:00 - 7:30 pm
  
**How to get there:** 
The easiest option is a taxi, which usually runs around 70,000-100,000 VND (3-5 USD) from the city centre

**Thien Hau Pagoda** (10:30-11:30)  

Dedicated to the goddess Mazu (Goddess of the Sea) and built in 1760 (over 260 years ago). Traditional Chinese architecture with Vietnamese touches, elaborate wood carvings and hanging incense coils. Look out for the intricate ceiling carvings and the ceramic dioramas depicting Chinese mythology.

Temple etiquette: take off your shoes before entering certain areas, dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees), ask before taking photos, stay silent during prayers, and note that small donations are appreciated (10,000 VND is about right).

### Afternoon
**Exploring Chinatown** (13:00-16:00)

**Suggested itinerary:**

Binh Tay Market - Thien Hau Pagoda (on foot) - Quan Am Pagoda - Hao Si Phuong Alley - Lunch destination

**Quan Am Pagoda**  

Dedicated to the Goddess of Mercy (Avalokitesvara) and built in the 19th century. The architecture pairs an arched entrance with a mix of Buddhist and Taoist elements, and inside you'll find colourful statues, rich decoration and a deeply spiritual atmosphere. Time needed: 15-20 minutes.

**Local lunch:** **Ba Lu Coffee**  

For a coffee break, try Ba Lu Coffee, an old café famous for its traditional roasting. The coffee is seriously strong, so have it in the morning or early afternoon if you value your sleep.

**If it rains:** Chinatown has many covered temples and markets to explore.

### Evening  
**Rooftop Bars and Bui Vien Street** (18:00-22:00)

**Sunset Rooftop (18:00-20:00):**

**The View Rooftop Bar Bui Vien**  

Perched above the lively Bui Vien, The View Rooftop Bar is a relaxed but buzzy escape with stunning city views. Whether you're catching the sunset or the sparkling skyline at night, it's a perfect spot to unwind. The cocktails are creative, well made and fairly priced. A must if you're on Bui Vien.  
**Address:** 
9th floor of the Duc Vuong Hotel, 195 Bui Vien

**Chill Skybar**  

The most famous of the lot is Chill Skybar, on the 26th floor of the AB Tower — Saigon's most glamorous nightspot. Down in District 1, its rooftop terrace serves up sweeping panoramic views of the city, and the trendy, sophisticated vibe draws locals and tourists alike.

**Alternatives:**
- **Saigon Saigon Bar** (Caravelle Hotel): 
Where war correspondents once watched the conflict unfold

- **Social Club** (Hotel des Arts): Refined and creative cocktails

**Bui Vien Street Night Experience (20:00-22:00)**

Saigon's legendary backpacker strip. Every evening from 19:00 the street closes to traffic and turns into a pulsing open-air party that doesn't let up until 2:00, sometimes later. Bars and restaurants line both sides, spilling hundreds of plastic chairs out onto the road.

Vendors weave through the crowd selling bia hoi (fresh draught beer) at 10,000-15,000₫ a glass — about $0.40. Music blares from competing sound systems, and fire dancers, street artists and spontaneous dance circles pile on to the chaos.

### Tips of the Day
- **Transport in Chinatown:** 
Grab: 100,000-150,000 VND ($4-6 USD) depending on traffic, 10-15 minutes (morning) or 15-25 minutes (evening)

- **Rooftop Dress Code:** 
Most rooftop bars enforce a smart-casual dress code — no shorts, flip-flops or vests

- **Cocktail Prices:** 
Cocktails range from 180,000-350,000₫, but the views are free

- **Bui Vien Safety:** 
Keep your wallet close — some touts can be pushy about steering you into bars. The pretty girls who chat you up aren't actually interested in you; they're after your money. Do your homework and keep your wits about you

- **Best Time Rooftop:** 
Go at sunset (17:30-19:00) for the views, or after 21:00 for the atmosphere

**Estimated daily budget for 4 people:**
- Transport: 400,000 VND (~€15)
- Temple admissions: Free (voluntary donations)
- Chinatown lunch: 800,000 VND (~€30)
- Rooftop drinks: 1,200,000 VND (~€45) 
- Bui Vien dinner: 600,000 VND (~€22)
- **Total: ~€112 for 4 people**

And that wraps up your three days in Ho Chi Minh City — a perfect blend of history, culture, authentic food and nightlife!
