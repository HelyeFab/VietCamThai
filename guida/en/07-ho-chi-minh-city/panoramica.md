## Ho Chi Minh City — Overview

Don't just call it Saigon: Ho Chi Minh City is a human puzzle where bloody history and unbridled capitalism dance together on the sidewalks. 
Here modern skyscrapers and French colonial architecture blend into a unique urban panorama, where the Bitexco Financial Tower rises beside the Saigon Opera House
.

![The skyline of Ho Chi Minh City](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/R%C3%ADo_Saig%C3%B3n%2C_Ciudad_Ho_Chi_Minh%2C_Vietnam%2C_2013-08-14%2C_DD_29.JPG/1280px-R%C3%ADo_Saig%C3%B3n%2C_Ciudad_Ho_Chi_Minh%2C_Vietnam%2C_2013-08-14%2C_DD_29.JPG)

It's the largest city in Vietnam and the country's true economic and cultural engine, drawing millions of tourists and locals
. This is not a postcard — it's a living organism where the past never hides, but constantly reinvents itself. 
The profound impact of the Indochina War era added another layer to the city's character, contributing to its resilience, while ancient traditions and spiritual customs continue to thrive alongside contemporary life
.

### History & Culture

The city traces its origins to the Kingdom of Cambodia, where it existed as a small fishing village called Prey Nokor. The area was conquered by Vietnamese refugees, who renamed it Saigon in 1698
. 
In 1859 the city was captured by the French and in 1862 ceded to France by the Vietnamese emperor Tu Duc. As the capital of Cochinchina, Saigon was transformed into an important port and metropolitan center of beautiful villas, imposing public buildings and well-paved tree-lined boulevards
.

![The Notre-Dame Cathedral of Saigon](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d7/Saigon_%E2%80%93_Notre_Dame_Cathedral_%282041896237%29.jpg)

But it's the Vietnam War that truly carved the soul of this city. 
From 1954 Saigon became the capital of South Vietnam, and during the Second Indochina War in the 1960s and early '70s, it was the headquarters of American military operations
. 
On April 30, 1975, Saigon was captured by North Vietnamese forces, marking the end of the war and the reunification of Vietnam. After this event, Saigon was officially renamed Ho Chi Minh City in honor of the revolutionary leader
.

**Insider cultural tidbit**: 
Officially the name is Ho Chi Minh City, but many locals and visitors still call it Saigon. The two names are used interchangeably throughout the country, and the word "Saigon" can still be found on shop signs all over Vietnam, even in Hanoi
. It's not nostalgia — it's cultural identity.

### When to Go

In mid-October (your days: October 14-16) Ho Chi Minh City is still in the full rainy season, but the South is the most forgiving part of Vietnam at this time. 
Mornings are often clear and the rain tends to concentrate in short, intense late-afternoon downpours: October remains a wet month (on average around 270-330 mm spread over many days), but the showers are usually short-lived and rarely ruin an entire day
.

The temperature on your days ranges between 24-25°C at night and 31-32°C in the early afternoon
. 
The abundant humidity carried in from the sea keeps the air moist, making the perceived temperature much higher than the actual one and causing a sticky feeling. Often within ten minutes of standing outdoors at midday, your clothes will be drenched in sweat
.

The weather is very dramatic — clear skies one moment, dark clouds gathering the next, and sudden downpours that quickly leave the streets submerged in calf-deep water, soon followed by the sun shining again
. 
Sneakers are prone to getting wet, so it's advisable to bring sandals. Pack a light raincoat or an umbrella, and concentrate outdoor visits in the morning hours, usually the driest
.

### Orientation

Ho Chi Minh City is massive — it sprawls across 24 districts — but only a handful of them will interest you
. 
The districts surround District 1, which is the city's nerve center, buzzing with major shopping areas and entertainment venues. The Saigon River flows through several districts, including Binh Thanh, District 1, District 4 and District 7
.

**Essential mental map**:

- **District 1**: 
The economic and cultural heart, bordered by Thu Duc City and Districts 3, 4 and 5. Here you'll find the icons of Ho Chi Minh such as the Independence Palace, Ben Thanh Market, Central Post Office and Notre Dame Cathedral

- **District 3**: 
Located near District 1, but with a more peaceful charm that contrasts with the liveliness of its metropolitan neighbor. The slightly slower pace of life is enriched by serene parks and beautiful churches like Tan Dinh Church

- **District 4**: 
An "emerald island" in the heart of Ho Chi Minh City, small and very local
. 
A fantastic spot for street food

- **District 5 (Cho Lon)**: 
Vietnam's Chinatown, called 'Cho Lon'. Famous for its specialized markets and flavorful Chinese cuisine, it offers a plethora of culinary delights alongside historically and culturally rich sites

- **District 7**: 
Associated with the modern Phu My Hung neighborhood, one of the city's fastest developing and modernizing districts

Tan Son Nhat Airport is located in Tan Binh District, about 7 kilometers north of District 1. A taxi costs around 200,000 VND (8-10 dollars) and takes 20-30 minutes
.
