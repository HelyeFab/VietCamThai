# Food & Culture — Koh Rong Samloem

## Food & Culture — Koh Rong Samloem

![Fresh fish and sea at Koh Rong Samloem](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Koh_Rong_Samloen_beach_-_panoramio.jpg/1280px-Koh_Rong_Samloen_beach_-_panoramio.jpg)

On the island you eat what the sea offers that same morning. The cuisine is simple, fresh and almost always fish-based: don't expect fine restaurants, but tables on the sand and evening barbecues under the stars.

### What to Eat

- **Fish and seafood BBQ**: the island's evening ritual. Prawns, squid, barracuda and the catch of the day grilled over the coals, often served with rice and stir-fried vegetables. Many resorts offer a fixed-price "seafood BBQ" set.
- **Fish amok**: the quintessential Khmer dish — fish steamed in a mousse of coconut milk and *kroeung* (a paste of herbs and spices), served in a banana leaf.
- **Lok lak** and **Khmer curry**: meat alternatives for those who want a change from fish.
- **Tropical fruit and smoothies**: mango, pineapple, dragon fruit — perfect for slow breakfasts.
- **Beer and sunset cocktails**: Angkor and Cambodia are the local beers; the beach bars serve cheap cocktails during happy hour.

### Culture & Etiquette

- **Island rhythm**: everything is slow, including the service. Order ahead, especially for dinner, because the kitchen cooks to order.
- **Respect for the local community**: a community of Khmer fishermen lives at M'Pai Bay. Dress modestly away from the beach and always ask permission before photographing people.
- **Tipping**: not mandatory but much appreciated, given the seasonal nature of work on the island.

### Sustainability

Koh Rong Samloem is a fragile ecosystem. A few good practices make the difference:
- **Take your plastic away with you**: waste disposal on the island is limited; keep single-use items to a minimum and use a reusable bottle.
- **Responsible snorkelling**: don't touch or step on the coral, don't collect shells or live starfish.
- **Reef-safe sunscreen**: choose biodegradable sun protection to protect the reef.
- **Water and energy**: electricity often comes from solar panels or a generator — use it sparingly.
