## Practical Info — Koh Rong Samloem

### Getting There

**From Siem Reap to the coast, then ferry to the island**


Koh Rong Samloem is reached from the port of **Sihanoukville**. On **Day 8** you arrive from Siem Reap in two legs — it's a long travel day:

- **Siem Reap → Sihanoukville**: direct van/bus (long, ~8-10 hours, including overnight, ~10-15€ with Giant Ibis or Virak Buntham) or a domestic flight (~1h, Cambodia Angkor Air / Sky Angkor) to save time
- **Sihanoukville → Saracen Bay (Koh Rong Samloem)**: fast ferry, about 45 minutes

**Ferry companies:**
- **Speed Ferry Cambodia** and **Buva Sea** — 2-3 departures a day, ticket ~12-15€ round trip
- Book the morning or early-afternoon departure: the timetable thins out in the afternoon and services can be cancelled when the sea is rough

**Practical details:**
- Confirm with your accommodation the **arrival pier** (Saracen Bay or M'Pai Bay): not all ferries stop at both
- Bring **cash**: there are no ATMs on the island and many resorts don't accept cards


### Moving On — towards Phnom Penh


**There is no direct ferry to Phnom Penh**: the connection is in two legs. On **Day 10** you first return by fast ferry to **Sihanoukville** (Speed Ferry Cambodia / Buva Sea, ~45 minutes) and from there continue overland by **bus/van (or private transfer)** to **Phnom Penh** (~5 hours, ~8-15€ with Giant Ibis or Virak Buntham). Book the morning or early-afternoon ferry so you don't arrive in the capital at night.


### Getting Around

- **On foot**: there are no paved roads or cars on the island. You get around by walking along the beaches and on the jungle trails.
- **By boat**: taxi-boats and tours connect Saracen Bay, Lazy Beach and M'Pai Bay when the trek is too long or the sea allows it.
- **Headlamp**: essential in the evening, because the trails and part of the beaches are not lit.


### Where to Stay

- **Saracen Bay**: the most convenient area and with the widest choice — beach bungalows and small mid-range resorts (~25-50€ a night), some with a restaurant.
- **M'Pai Bay (north)**: cheaper and more backpacker, hostels and guesthouses (~10-25€), a fishing-village atmosphere.
- **Lazy Beach (west)**: very few isolated places to stay, ideal for those seeking total peace and quiet.

> **A note on accommodation**: in recent years, after some changes and infrastructure works, several resorts on the Saracen Bay side have closed. The island itself remains a paradise and the activities don't change, but the choice of accommodation matters much more now: check that your chosen property is actually open and operating and that it has recent reviews before booking.

> Book ahead: places on the island are limited and fill up in high season.


### Connectivity & SIM

- **Limited electricity**: often available only during certain hours, from a generator or solar panels. Charge your devices when you can.
- **Wi-Fi and data**: the connection is slow and patchy — treat it as part of the escape. A Cambodian SIM (Cellcard or Smart) gets spotty reception at Saracen Bay.
- **Cash (USD)**: there are no ATMs on the island. Withdraw in Sihanoukville before leaving and bring US dollars in small denominations.


### Safety

- **Tides and currents**: check the low- and high-tide times; in some stretches the seabed stays shallow for a long way. Don't swim alone at night too far out.
- **Sandflies**: present on some beaches at sunset — use repellent and keep a soothing cream on hand.
- **Jungle trekking**: closed shoes, water and set off in daylight; the trail to Lazy Beach is easy but slippery after rain.
- **Health**: bring a small kit of basic medicines — the nearest pharmacy is in Sihanoukville.
