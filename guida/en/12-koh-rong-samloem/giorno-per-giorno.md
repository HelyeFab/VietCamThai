# DAY BY DAY — Koh Rong Samloem

## Day 8 — October 8, 2026 — Arrival on the island and plankton night

![Beach on Koh Rong Samloem](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b0/Koh_Rong_Samloen_beach_-_panoramio.jpg/1280px-Koh_Rong_Samloen_beach_-_panoramio.jpg)

### Morning
After the last temples in Siem Reap, you head for the southern coast: it's a long travel day. Van or bus from **Siem Reap to Sihanoukville**, where the fast ferry to the island departs.

**The transfer**:
- From Siem Reap to Sihanoukville: daytime bus/minivan (long, ~8-10 hours) or a domestic flight (~1h) to save time
- From Sihanoukville pier to Saracen Bay: fast ferry (**Speed Ferry Cambodia / Buva Sea**), about 45 minutes (~12-15€ round trip)
- The companies run 2-3 departures a day: aim to leave Siem Reap early to catch the early-afternoon ferry

### Afternoon
Arrival at **Saracen Bay**, check in to the bungalow and first swim. No plans: after a full day on the road, this is the moment to drop your backpack and shed the pace of the previous weeks. Confirm in advance that your accommodation is open and operating: in recent years several resorts on the Saracen Bay side have closed.
- Stroll along the two kilometres of white sand in the bay
- A swim in the calm, shallow water, perfect even for those who aren't strong swimmers
- Sundowner with your feet in the sand at one of the beach bars

### Evening
If the journey hasn't worn you out, the evening of the arrival day already offers the island's signature experience: **the night of the bioluminescent plankton**. When darkness falls, move away from the resort lights and take a swim: every movement lights up trails of blue light in the water. The best times are on moonless nights. Alternatively, many resorts organise short kayak outings to see the plankton offshore. If instead you're too tired, no problem: there's the whole next day to make up for it.

> **Tip**: bring a headlamp and cash — there are no ATMs on the island and the electricity can go out at night.

## Day 9 — October 9, 2026 — Snorkelling, jungle and blissful idleness

![Lagoon on Koh Rong Samloem](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Koh_Rong_Sanloem_lagoon.jpg/1280px-Koh_Rong_Sanloem_lagoon.jpg)

### Morning
**Snorkelling and sea**. The waters around the island are clear and full of life:
- Boat tour to the best snorkelling spots (coral, tropical fish) — half a day, ~15-20€
- Kayak rental to explore the coast and hidden coves on your own
- For the laziest: simply a slow breakfast and a hammock — **blissful idleness** is an activity in its own right

### Afternoon
**Mini-trek through the jungle to Lazy Beach (Sunset Beach)**. A trail crosses the island's wooded interior from Saracen Bay to the western side (about 25-30 minutes on foot, closed shoes recommended):
- **Lazy Beach**: a wild, uncrowded beach, one of the most beautiful in Cambodia
- It's the best spot on the island for the **sunset** over the Gulf of Thailand
- Along the way you'll come across the old military structures hidden in the vegetation

Alternatively, a hike or boat trip to **M'Pai Bay**, the fishing village to the north, for the most authentic and local atmosphere.

### Evening
If on the first evening you were too tired for the **bioluminescent plankton**, this is the right night: a swim in the dark among the trails of blue light, or a kayak outing organised by the resort. Otherwise, a fresh-fish dinner on the beach and a hammock under the stars: no obligations, just the island's slow rhythm.

> **Tip**: moonless evenings are the best for the plankton — ask the resort about the moon phase and plan accordingly.

## Day 10 — October 10, 2026 — The Last Day on the Island

![Lagoon on Koh Rong Samloem](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Koh_Rong_Sanloem_lagoon.jpg/1280px-Koh_Rong_Sanloem_lagoon.jpg)

### Morning
No alarm, no transfer: today the island is all yours one last time. It's the day to enjoy what you've discovered without the rush of arrival:
- A slow breakfast and a long swim in the turquoise water of Saracen Bay, while the bay is still silent
- One last snorkel or a kayak outing to the coves you haven't explored yet
- For those who prefer it, simply a hammock and a book in the shade of the palms — **blissful idleness** all the way

### Afternoon
One last tour of the island's loveliest spots before you leave:
- Walk back to **Lazy Beach** or stroll towards **M'Pai Bay** if you haven't been yet
- An afternoon on the beach, a final dip and a few photos in the golden light
- **Pack your backpack calmly** and check that you have enough **cash** for the whole of tomorrow's travel day: there are no ATMs on the island

### Evening
Last night on the island. A fresh-fish dinner on the beach and, if the sky is moonless, one last swim among the trails of **bioluminescent plankton**. Go to sleep with the ferry time already clear in your head: **tomorrow morning (Day 11) you set off again** — fast ferry to Sihanoukville and then bus towards Phnom Penh, the capital.

> **Tip**: book today the early-morning ferry to Sihanoukville. It's the only link to the mainland, and leaving early gives you all the margin you need for the long bus leg to Phnom Penh without arriving at night.
