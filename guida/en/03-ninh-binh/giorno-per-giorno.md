## Day 25 — October 25, 2026 — The Underground Halong Bay

### Morning
You arrive in Ninh Binh on the night bus from Phong Nha (evening departure, 
arriving at dawn after an 8–10 hour journey in a sleeper berth
). Once you've dropped your bags at the homestay and had breakfast, head to **Trang An** to start with the UNESCO boat tour.

![Trang An landscape](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Trang_An_Landscape_Complex%2C_Ninh_Binh_Province%2C_Vietnam%2C_20240202_1456_5313.jpg/1280px-Trang_An_Landscape_Complex%2C_Ninh_Binh_Province%2C_Vietnam%2C_20240202_1456_5313.jpg)

**Trang An Boat Tour** — 
open from 7:00 to 17:00, ticket 250,000 VND per person (about €10)
. 
Each boat can hold up to 4 passengers excluding the rower
, perfect for your group! 
Three routes are available, each 2–3 hours through different caves and temples
.

**Tip:** 
Choose Route 2, recommended for first-time visitors because it offers a good mix of caves, temples and scenery
. 
It includes the longest cave, Cloud Cave (over 1,000 metres), and the film sets from Kong: Skull Island
.

**Useful link:** [Official Trang An website](https://www.tranganlandscapecomplex.com/)

### Afternoon
After lunch (try the Sen Trang An restaurant mentioned in the guides), head to **Hoa Lu Ancient Capital** — 
entrance 20,000 VND per person, open 7:00–17:00
. 
Vietnam's first political and cultural centre, established in the 10th century under the Dinh and Le dynasties
.

**Not to miss:**
- 
The Temple of King Dinh and the Temple of King Le, which preserve the legacy of Vietnam's ancient monarchs
- 
The four sacred temples dedicated to the gods of the four directions: Quy Minh Temple (South), Nguyen Temple (North), Thien Ton Temple (East) and Cao Son Temple (West)

**Rainy-day alternative:** 
Am Tien Cave (Tuyet Tinh Coc), a combination of lakes, mountains and temples with a tranquil atmosphere

### Evening
At sunset, climb up to **Mua Cave** for the most iconic panoramic view in northern Vietnam. 
Entrance 100,000 VND, open 6:00–19:00
. 
Be ready to climb almost 500 steps
, but 
the sunset over the limestone cliffs and rice paddies is unforgettable
.

**Insider trick:** 
Arrive an hour before sunset for the best photographic conditions and to avoid the heat
. 
Begin the climb about an hour before sunset
.

**Dinner:** **Hoang Cuisine** — 
right in the heart of Tam Coc, an open-air spot that lets you soak up the surrounding scenery, with an elegant atmosphere that captures the essence of Vietnamese hospitality
. 
Try the goat meat (a Ninh Binh specialty), fried rice and Vietnamese soups including Bún Riêu
.

### Tips of the Day
- 
Arrive early at Trang An (8:30), but still expect queues and other boats along the route
- 
If you'd prefer a private experience, you can buy extra tickets to reserve the whole boat
- 
There are toilets available at the pier and snacks/drinks for sale at the temple stops
- For tomorrow: 
consider Van Long Nature Reserve as a less touristy alternative — the same spectacular scenery but with practically no tourists, and cheaper (200,000 VND vs 250,000 VND)

## Day 26 — October 26, 2026 — The Awakening of the Rice Paddies

### Morning
**Dawn at Tam Coc** — 
tickets available from 7:30–17:00
, but 
to avoid the crowds, visit early in the morning or in the late afternoon
. 
The boat tour on the Ngo Dong River costs 250,000 VND and lasts 1.5–2 hours through 3 caves
.

**A unique experience:** 
Instead of rowing by hand, your rower sits at the back of the sampan with the oars hooked under their feet and drives the boat forward with a steady, rhythmic push of the legs — a centuries-old technique that grew up because a full day of hand-rowing wrecks your back
. 
Most rowers are women because they are considered more patient and skilled at this technique
.

Entrance ticket: 120,000 VND for adults, 60,000 VND for children under 1.4m. Boat ticket: 150,000 VND per boat
.

**Compared with yesterday:** 
Tam Coc is shorter and more intimate (1.5–2 hours vs Trang An's 2.5+ hours), more authentic even if less polished, perfect for admiring rural villages and rice paddies up close
.

### Afternoon
**Bike ride through the rice paddies** — 
Almost every place to stay lays on bicycles, often free for guests. The ground between the limestone peaks is completely flat, and it's a joy to pedal past the rivers, lakes, mountains and rice paddies
.

**Suggested route:** From the centre of Tam Coc to Bich Dong Pagoda and back (about 15 km). 
During the harvest season (late May–early June), the fields turn golden, creating the most famous view in Ninh Binh
.

**Alternative for the adventurous:** **Van Long Nature Reserve** — 
located 80 km south of Hanoi and 17 km northeast of central Ninh Binh, it's the most remote option, 30 minutes from the city and from Tam Coc
. 
Boat tour 100,000 VND per person, two people per boat, lasting 1.5 hours. Entrance 20,000 VND/person
.

**Van Long advantages:** 
A non-touristy, tranquil and authentic experience, far fewer visitors, absolutely calm waters
. 
Famous for the endangered Delacour's langur — there are about 200 individuals left globally, 120 of which are at Van Long
.

### Evening
**Aperitif:** **Chookie's Beer Garden** — 
a casual and fun setting, known for its relaxed atmosphere and wide selection of drinks, perfect for unwinding after a day of exploring
. 
Juicy burgers, German sausages grilled to perfection and fresh salads
.

**Dinner:** **Minh Toan Restaurant** — 
widely rated the best restaurant in Tam Coc, and for good reason: the food is delicious and properly homemade, and the staff are warm and smiling from start to finish
. 
Recommended dishes: spring rolls, goat pho and mixed fried dishes
.

**Vegetarian alternative:** **Ngoc Linh Restaurant** — 
recommended by the homestay owner for its tofu dishes, serving what is considered "life-changing tofu"
.

### Tips of the Day
- 
If you feel guilty watching your rower work tirelessly, bring some snacks to share — it's normal and appreciated
- 
A small tip for the rower (20,000–50,000 VND per boat) is appreciated but not mandatory
- 
The best time to visit Van Long is in the afternoon (15:30–16:30), when you can see langurs and storks
- 
Be ready for the roving vendors halfway along the route — they're polite, and you can buy snacks and drinks for your rower
- 
The departure point is listed on Google Maps as "Trang An Departure Boat Ticket"
 — handy for finding the spots tomorrow if you move around

By now Ninh Binh will have shown you exactly why it's called "the Halong Bay on land" — but with an authenticity and tranquillity that make it something even more special. Tomorrow, the road to your next destination beckons!
