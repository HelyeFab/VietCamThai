# FOOD & CULTURE

## Where and What to Eat in Ninh Binh

![Grilled goat, a Ninh Binh specialty](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Th%E1%BA%AFng_c%E1%BB%91.jpg/1280px-Th%E1%BA%AFng_c%E1%BB%91.jpg)

### Must-Try Signature Dishes

**1. Cơm Cháy with Mountain Goat** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Ninh Binh's signature dish: crispy rice fried until golden and crunchy, served with lemongrass-grilled mountain goat or goat stew
. 
The rice is steamed, pressed into thin layers, sun-dried and then fried to a perfect golden crispness, served with a rich topping of minced pork, spring onions and often a pinch of goat meat or pork heart
.

**Price:** 
30,000–80,000 VND per portion (€1.20–3.20)

**Where:** Everywhere in the province, but the best are in traditional restaurants

**2. Mountain Goat (Dê Núi)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Mountain goat meat is renowned throughout Vietnam and is an absolute must when visiting Ninh Binh. The goats grazing freely in the mountains make the meat extremely lean and slightly chewy, with an incredibly pure flavour
.

**Main preparations:**
- 
Steamed goat with ginger, goat hot pot (lẩu dê), lemongrass-grilled goat
- 
Specialties include lemongrass-grilled goat and steamed goat with lime leaves

**Price:** 
3–5 USD per dish (€2.80–4.70)

**3. Bún Mọc (Noodle Soup with Meatballs)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

The perfect Ninh Binh breakfast: a comforting noodle soup with a sweet, clear broth made from pork bones, served with rice noodles and salted pork-and-mushroom meatballs (mọc)
.

**Price:** 
1.50–2.50 USD per bowl (€1.40–2.35)

**4. Miến Lươn (Eel Vermicelli)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

A traditional dish common in northern Vietnam, especially in Ninh Binh, often enjoyed as breakfast or a comforting meal. The star of the dish is the tender eel meat combined with fresh vermicelli and served in a fragrant, light broth
.

**Price:** 
0.50–1 USD per bowl (€0.47–0.94)

**5. Exotic Dishes (for the more adventurous)**
- **Xôi Trứng Kiến** (Glutinous rice with ant eggs): 
A rare and unusual dish that gives the glutinous rice a unique nutty flavour and an interesting texture. The ant eggs provide a rich, slightly creamy taste
- 
Price: 50,000–100,000 VND/dish (€2.10–4.20)

### Markets

**Chợ Rồng (Dragon Market)** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

The vibrant, beating heart of Ninh Binh's commercial life. It's a traditional Vietnamese market, full of energy, colour and bustle. It remains the main hub where locals go for their daily needs
.

- **Address:** 
No. 71 Van Giang street, Thanh Binh ward, in central Ninh Binh
- **Hours:** 
Daily from 6:00 to 19:00, with peak activity in the early morning

**What to buy:**
- 
Com Chay (crispy scorched rice): this iconic local delicacy is a must. Look for the plain versions, which are perfect as a vegetarian souvenir. A dish typically costs 30,000–50,000 VND (€1.20–2.00)
- 
Handicrafts and souvenirs: finely woven bamboo baskets and traditional conical hats. Fresh local produce: the vivid displays of tropical fruits such as dragon fruit, rambutan and jackfruit

**Ninh Binh Night Market** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Conveniently located in the heart of Ninh Binh City, along Dinh Tien Hoang Street — one of the city's liveliest and most vibrant areas
.

- **Hours:** 
Every evening from 18:00 to 23:00
- **Street food prices:** 
Most dishes are sold from small stalls, usually priced between 20,000 and 70,000 VND (€0.85–3.00)

**Chợ Đêm Tam Cốc** ⭐⭐⭐

This market is a charming blend of tradition and modernity, offering a variety of local delicacies, including bánh cuốn, nem rán and fresh grilled seafood
.

### Etiquette and Culture

**🏛️ IN TEMPLES AND SACRED PLACES:**
- 
When visiting temples, wear modest clothing and always remove your shoes and hat. You can carry your shoes with you, but don't wear them on your feet
- 
Remove your shoes before entering temple buildings. Speak softly or stay silent in sacred areas. Walk slowly and observe what the locals do
- 
Bow your head to pay respect to the temple and to the Buddha statues inside

**🍽️ AT THE TABLE:**
- 
Wait to be invited to sit down and start eating, especially in people's homes. Use chopsticks correctly — don't stick them upright in the rice (it recalls a funeral offering). Share the dishes: most meals are family-style, and it's polite to try a little of everything
- 
Hold the food bowl close to your face — nose to the bowl is acceptable! Hold the spoon in your left hand when eating soup
- 
When someone offers you a toast, raise your glass lower than theirs if they are older. Say "Một, hai, ba... Dzô!" loudly with everyone — it's a fun and traditional way to toast

**🙏 WITH PEOPLE:**
- 
As a sign of respect toward elders or other esteemed people, such as monks, take off your hat and politely bow your head when addressing them
- 
The head is considered the most sacred part of the body. Touching someone's head, even in a friendly way, is seen as very disrespectful, especially for adults or the elderly. This belief is deeply rooted in the idea that the head is where a person's spirit resides
- 
When visiting a family home, bring a box of biscuits or sweets, fruit, flowers or soft drinks as a gift — never bring anything black. Greet the older members of a family first, shake hands with your right hand and offer plenty of smiles to everyone

**📸 PHOTOGRAPHY:**
- 
There are so many beautiful photo opportunities in Vietnam, and most people will be happy for you to take their picture, but respect their privacy and always ask before raising your camera

**COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:**
- 
No public displays of affection: holding hands is fine, but kissing or hugging in public is frowned upon
- 
Pointing your feet toward people or sacred objects is considered rude, since the feet are seen as the least clean part of the body. Avoid resting your feet on tables or chairs, and when seated, avoid pointing your feet toward others or toward religious objects

### Useful Words

**GREETINGS AND COURTESIES:**
1. **Xin chào** (sin CHOW) - Hello
2. **Cảm ơn** (gahm UN) - Thank you
3. **Xin lỗi** (sin LOY) - Excuse me/Sorry
4. **Không có gì** (KHOM ko zee) - You're welcome/Don't mention it
5. **Vâng/Dạ** (vahng/yah) - Yes (formal/informal)
6. **Không** (KHOM) - No

**ORDERING FOOD:**
7. 
**Tôi muốn...** (TOY moo-oon) - I'd like... / "I want..."
8. 
**Bạn có thể giới thiệu cho tôi món gì?** - What do you recommend?
9. 
**Không cay** (KHOM kay) - Not spicy
10. 
**Ngon** (ngahn) - Delicious
11. 
**Bao nhiêu tiền?** (bow NYEW tyen) - How much does it cost?

**EMERGENCIES AND NEEDS:**
12. 
**Tôi cần sự giúp đỡ** - I need help
13. 
**Nhà vệ sinh ở đâu?** (nyah VAY sin uh DOW) - Where is the bathroom?
14. 
**Tôi không hiểu** - I don't understand
15. 
**Tính tiền, làm ơn** (tinh TYEN lahm uhn) - The bill, please

**💡 Insider Tip:** 
Vietnamese people are incredibly patient with learners. Your pronunciation may not be perfect and you may mix up the pronouns, but effort counts more than perfection. Don't stress too much about getting the tones exactly right when you're just starting out
!
