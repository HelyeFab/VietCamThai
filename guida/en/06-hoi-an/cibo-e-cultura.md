# FOOD & CULTURE

## Where and What to Eat in Hoi An

![Cao Lau — Hoi An's signature noodles](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Cao_Lau_Noodles_-_Cao_Lau_%2B_Spring_Rolls_VND20000.jpg/1280px-Cao_Lau_Noodles_-_Cao_Lau_%2B_Spring_Rolls_VND20000.jpg)

Hoi An isn't just one of the most picturesque cities in Vietnam — it's the country's gastronomic heart.
Here you really can't go wrong: even the most touristy restaurants serve surprisingly good food, which makes the town a must-stop for any food-loving traveller working their way through Vietnam
.

### Typical Dishes Not to Miss

![Hoi An Banh Mi](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Banh_My_Phuong_Hoi_An_quay_banh.jpg/1280px-Banh_My_Phuong_Hoi_An_quay_banh.jpg)

#### **Cao Lầu** - Hoi An's signature dish

Cao Lầu is easily the number-one dish to try in Hoi An. It's the dish most associated with the city — "Hoi An in a bowl"
.

The rice noodles used in Cao Lầu are not just any noodles. They've been soaked in calcium-rich water drawn from the local well, mixed with the ash of local trees from Cham Island. This gives the noodle a yellowish colour and a firmer texture than other rice noodles
.

The dish contains: marinated char siu pork, bean sprouts, fresh vegetables, crunchy rice croutons and a light broth.
The price is between 30,000-50,000 VND (€1.20-2) per bowl
.

**Where to eat it**:
-
Ong Hai (Mr. Hai Restaurant): 6A Trương Minh Lượng, Cẩm Châu

-
Cao Lau Khong Gian Xanh for 40,000 VND (€1.50)

#### **Mi Quảng** - Cao Lầu's golden brother

Mi quang might be a signature dish of nearby Da Nang, but don't underestimate Hoi An when it comes to this central Vietnamese noodle dish. In Hoi An, the stalls serve bowls full of thick, square rice noodles, meat or seafood, broth and vegetables
.

Mi Quang: Another regional specialty, this dish features wide, turmeric-infused noodles in a small amount of rich, savoury broth, topped with a delicious combination of shrimp, pork, a quail egg and peanuts
.

**Price**: 20,000-50,000 VND (€0.85-2)

#### **Bánh Mì** - Vietnam's most famous sandwich

It really is a crime to visit Vietnam and not eat a single Bánh Mì. At 20,000 VND ($0.86 USD) per sandwich, it's an affordable, nutritious and easy option for a full meal to start the day
.

In Hoi An, there are a few places to grab a Bánh Mì, but the most popular shops have to be Banh Mi Phuong (rumoured to sell 3,000 Bánh Mì a day) and The Bánh Mì Queen Madam Khanh (selling 1,000 Bánh Mì a day). Banh Mi Phuong became more famous because it was praised by Anthony Bourdain as the best Bánh Mì in Vietnam
.

**Where to eat it**:
-
Bánh Mì Queen - a really popular spot with options to eat in or take away

- Bánh Mì Phượng (recommended by Anthony Bourdain)

#### **Cơm Gà** - Hoi An's chicken rice

Like its neighbours in Malaysia, Singapore and Thailand, the famous Hainanese chicken rice also made its way to Vietnam and became popular especially in Hoi An. The rice is flavoured with chicken broth, but in some restaurants Com Ga also features a hint of turmeric in the rice
.

Each plate of Com Ga is served with a small bowl of clear soup, dipping sauces and pickled green chillies
.

**Price**: 30,000-50,000 VND (€1.20-2)

#### **Bánh Bao Bánh Vạc** - White roses

Nicknamed white rose dumplings for their resemblance to the flower, banh bao banh vac is another unique local specialty of Hoi An
.
The name of this dumpling, unique to Hoi An, comes from its rose-like shape. Its recipe is apparently guarded by the third generation of the same family that has long supplied these dumplings across Hoi An. The steamed dumpling is made with rice flour and the same well water used in cao lầu. It's filled with minced pork, shrimp and mushrooms
.

**Where to eat it**:

White Rose Restaurant (run by the family that specialises in these dumplings): 533 Hai Bà Trưng, Phường Cẩm Phổ

### Our Recommended Restaurants and Street Food

#### **Mid-range restaurants (100,000-200,000 VND / €4-8)**

1. **Morning Glory**
   
If you're looking for one of the best places to eat in Hoi An, Morning Glory is the one. Although decidedly geared towards tourists and a bit more expensive than the average Hoi An restaurant, Morning Glory is an excellent choice for your first meal in Hoi An
.
   - **Address**: 115 Trần Cao Vân, Phường Minh An
   - **Specialties**: Cao Lầu, fried red fish with mango salad

2. **Êm Comfort Food**
   
Another rising star on the Hoi An restaurant scene is Êm Comfort Food. Perfect if you're looking for a bite in the historic centre but don't want to be in the crowds. Owner Nghia Bui works in the kitchen, at the bar, and is the heart and soul of his restaurant. The menu focuses on Vietnamese and Hoi An classics
.

3. **Son Hoi An Restaurant**
   
Son Hoi An would be my pick, without a doubt. After sunset you lose the river view, but the menu and the wine list are lovely, and the atmosphere is at its best if you grab a table in the part of the restaurant nearest the water
.
   
Order the grilled fish in banana leaf. Delicious
.

#### **Street food and budget spots (20,000-50,000 VND / €0.85-2)**

4. **Vy's Market**
   
I stumbled on Vy's Market thanks to a friend who had taken a cooking course here, and I think they have a brilliant concept. It's not so much a Hoi An restaurant as a small covered market with tables and vendors. You can sit down and order your food from the menu, but you can also walk around and literally peek into the pots first
.

5. **Hoi An Central Market - Food Court**
   
With dishes costing from 20,000 to 50,000 VND ($0.80-2 USD), Hoi An's food market is a fantastic place to get an affordable meal and a taste of the local flavours
.
   - **Hours**: 7:00-20:30

6. **My Quang Bich**
   
As you can tell from the name, My Quang Bich is just as well known for its mi quang as for its cao lau. It's nice to get out of the historic centre every now and then, so if you can rent a bicycle, you might want to enjoy a meal at My Quang Bich. The restaurant overlooks the river, making it a pleasant place to have lunch
.
   - **Address**: 2b Phan Chu Trinh, Cẩm Châu

7. **Phi Banh Mi**
   
Phi Banh Mi has only been open since 2013, but judging by their perfect 5-star rating on TripAdvisor, they've already become a serious contender for the best banh mi in Hoi An. Phi Banh Mi is a winner of the TripAdvisor Certificate of Excellence with a perfect 5-star rating, which is incredible considering they've racked up over 2,500 reviews
.

8. **Ong Hai (Mr. Hai Restaurant)**
   
This was the best bowl of mi quang I ate in Hoi An. Ong Hai serves only two dishes - cao lau and mi quang. Ong Hai garnishes his mi quang with shrimp, roasted peanuts, keropek, quail egg and slices of roast pork
.
   - **Address**: 6A Trương Minh Lượng, Cẩm Châu

### Markets

#### **Hoi An Central Market (Chợ Hội An)**

The official opening hours of Hoi An Central Market are 9:00-18:00. The Central Food Court is open from 7:00 to 20:30 and the Fruit and Vegetable Market from 6:00 to 20:00
.

**What to buy**:
-
Cao Lau: This is Hoi An's most famous dish, and you have to try it here. It consists of thick, chewy, yellow noodles, slices of savoury char-siu pork, fresh vegetables and crunchy croutons

- Fresh ingredients and local spices
-
Smoothies starting from 15,000 VND ($0.65 USD)

**Tips**:
-
Visit Hoi An Central Market early in the morning to experience the bustling atmosphere at its peak and get the freshest produce, as well as to avoid the crowds of tourists who arrive later in the day. Also, don't hesitate to haggle for the best deals; it's expected, and starting your offer at around half the asking price is a good rule of thumb

#### **Other markets to visit**:
-
Thanh Ha Fish Market: For the truly adventurous, a visit to this market requires a very early start (around 4-5 in the morning!). This is where the fishing boats bring in their catch, and it's the freshest fish you'll ever see

-
Night markets: they typically come alive around 17:00 and stay lively until 23:00 or even later at weekends

### Etiquette and Culture

#### **At the table**

Wait to be invited to sit down and start eating, especially in homes. Use chopsticks correctly - don't stick them straight up in the rice (it resembles a funeral offering). Share the dishes: most meals are family-style, and it's polite to try a bit of everything. Use the serving ends of the chopsticks or a spoon when taking food from shared dishes
.

Pass dishes at the table with both hands or your right hand (not the left), and hold the spoon in your left hand if you're eating soup. It's considered good hospitality for a host to offer you food, fill your glass and place the best pieces of a dish in your bowl. If you want to stop eating, politely repeat that you're very full
.

#### **In temples**

Remove your shoes before entering temple buildings. Speak quietly or stay silent in sacred areas. Walk slowly and observe what the locals do
.

Cover your shoulders and knees in areas of religious or cultural significance, such as temples and people's homes, and keep any underwear out of sight
.

#### **Public behaviour**

No public displays of affection: holding hands is fine, but kissing or hugging in public is frowned upon. Avoid loud or aggressive behaviour: stay calm and courteous, even in stressful situations
.

Avoid touching people's heads. Don't point your finger at someone. Instead, use your whole hand or nod your head
.

#### **Tipping and money**

At most social gatherings, the person who issued the invitation is the one who pays the bill
. Tips aren't mandatory but are appreciated in restaurants (5-10% of the total).

### Useful Words

Here are 15 essential phrases in Vietnamese with pronunciation:

1. **Xin chào** (sin chow) - Hello/Hi
2. **Cảm ơn** (gam-un) - Thank you
3. **Xin lỗi** (sin loy) - Sorry/Excuse me
4. **Không có gì** (khong ko zee) - You're welcome/No problem
5. **Có** (ko) - Yes
6. **Không** (khong) - No
7. **Bao nhiêu tiền?** (bow new tien) - How much does it cost?
8. **Tôi là** (thoy la) - My name is
9. **Ở đâu?** (uh dow) - Where is it?
10. **Nhà vệ sinh** (nya ve shin) - Bathroom/Toilet
11. **Tôi không hiểu** (thoy khong hee-oo) - I don't understand
12. **Nước** (neu-uk) - Water
13. **Cơm** (kom) - Rice
14. **Thịt** (theet) - Meat
15. **Chay** (chai) - Vegetarian

**Bonus for food**:
- **Ngon** (ngon) - Delicious!
- **Cay** (kai) - Spicy
- **Không cay** (khong kai) - Not spicy

Making the effort to speak even a few words of Vietnamese goes a long way: it shows respect for the local culture and a willingness to meet people on their own terms, and it tends to earn you warmer welcomes and richer encounters
.

Remember:
if there's a universal language in Vietnam, it's a smile. Even if your pronunciation isn't perfect, a friendly smile will bridge any gap in communication and make your trip much more enjoyable
!
