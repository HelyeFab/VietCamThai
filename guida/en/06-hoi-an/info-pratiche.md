## Practical Info — Hoi An

### Getting There

**Ho Chi Minh City → Da Nang → Hoi An (Flight + transfer)**


From Saigon you reach Hoi An by flying to Da Nang, the nearest airport. The direct flight takes 1 hour and 20 minutes, and there are around 50 flights a day
(VietJet Air, Vietnam Airlines, Vietravel Airlines).
From Da Nang airport, Hoi An is about 30 km away: the transfer by private car or taxi takes about 45 minutes
.

**Options from Da Nang airport:**
- **Private car / airport taxi**:
~45 minutes, about 350,000-450,000 VND (~€14-18) for the whole car

- **Grab from Da Nang**:
a cheaper alternative, subject to availability

- **Hotel shuttle**:
many Hoi An accommodations offer airport transfers on request


**Useful sites:**
- [Vietnam Airlines](https://www.vietnamairlines.com)
- [VietJet Air](https://www.vietjetair.com)
- [Skyscanner](https://www.skyscanner.com) to compare prices

### Moving On

**Hoi An → Hué via Hai Van Pass**


The transfer from Hoi An to Hué via the Hai Van Pass is one of the most scenic routes in Vietnam
. A private car via the Hai Van Pass (~€25/pp) is the perfect choice —
it offers door-to-door service with a professional driver and the option to choose the scenic route
.

**Transport options:**
- **Private car (recommended)**:
About 3 hours of travel through the scenic Hai Van Pass, one of the most beautiful routes in Vietnam

- **Tourist bus**:
3.5-4.5 hours, cost $6-15/pp

- **Train + bus via Da Nang**:
5 hours total, cost $10-13/pp


**Reliable companies:**
- **Hoi An Transport Service**: [hoi](https://hoiantransportservice.com/) -
Specialising in private transport since 2014, modern and clean cars, punctual and professional drivers

- **GetYourGuide**: [Link](https://www.getyourguide.com/da-nang-l939/hoi-an-to-hue-by-private-car-via-hai-van-pass-golden-bridge-t475297/) -
Door-to-door service with a professional driver, the option to choose the route


**Stops along the way:**
-
Lang Co Bay - recognised in 2009 as one of the 30 most beautiful bays in the world

-
Hai Van Pass - one of the most spectacular coastal roads in Vietnam with panoramic ocean views

-
Marble Mountains - limestone formations with natural caves, ancient pagodas and hidden tunnels


### Getting Around

Hoi An is perfect to explore on foot or by bike —
it's a paradise for cyclists with its compact size and flat terrain
.

**Local transport with costs (2025-2026 prices):**

**Grab (the most-used app):**
-
GrabBike from Old Town to An Bang Beach: 28,000 VND (~€1.10)

-
GrabBike: 10,000 VND base + 3,000 VND/km; GrabCar: 25,000 VND base + 7,000 VND/km

-
Grab and FastGo (the Vietnamese equivalent) available and cheaper than traditional taxis


**Taxi:**
-
Mai Linh (green): +84 235 3914 914; Faifoo (yellow): +84 235 3919 191

-
English taxi for tourists: +84 235 3 929 929


**Xe om (motorbike-taxi):**
-
Short trips: max 30,000 VND (~€1.20); to An Bang/Cua Dai beaches: 40,000 VND (~€1.60)


**Bicycles (ideal!):**
-
20,000-150,000 VND/day (~€0.80-6) depending on the type; rental at hotels or shops on Tran Hung Dao/Hai Ba Trung

-
Often free at hotels; rental 30,000 VND/day (~€1.30)


**Cyclo (rickshaw):**
-
15-20 minute Old Town tour: 150,000-200,000 VND (~€6-8)


**Essential apps:**
- **Grab** (main)
- **FastGo** (local alternative)
- **Xanh SM** (eco-friendly electric taxis)

### Safety

**General level:**
Central Vietnam (Hue, Da Nang, Hoi An) extremely safe for tourists with excellent infrastructure
.

**Specific risks:**

**Crime:**
-
Hoi An classified among Vietnam's safe destinations; violent crimes against foreigners rare, most trips are trouble-free

-
Main risks: pickpocketing and bag-snatching in crowded areas and tourist markets


**Common scams to avoid:**

**1. Cyclo/xe om scam:**
-
They quote a low price (50,000 VND) but ask for more (500,000 VND) after the ride; unexpected detours with demands for extra fares

- **Prevention**:
Agree the fare clearly beforehand, use Grab for motorbike-taxis


**2. "Free" photo scam:**
-
People with water buffalo or traditional costumes who encourage photos then ask for payment

- **Prevention**: Always ask the price beforehand

**3. Taxi scam:**
-
Rigged meters; use reliable companies like Vinasun, Mai Linh or the Grab app


**General precautions:**
-
Don't use your phone near the roadside; step into a shop or turn towards a wall

-
Avoid shoulder bags; use cross-body bags held in front

-
Keep valuables in hidden belts or the hotel safe; avoid large sums of cash


**Seasonal flooding:**
-
Hoi An Ancient Town floods periodically during the rainy season (October-November); the People's Committee provides real-time updates; 24/7 tourist assistance centre at 47 Tran Phu Street
