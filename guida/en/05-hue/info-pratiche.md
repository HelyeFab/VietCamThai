## Practical Info — Hue

### Getting There

**Hoi An / Da Nang → Hue via Hai Van Pass**


The transfer from Hoi An to Hue through the Hai Van Pass takes about 4 hours and is one of the most scenic routes in Vietnam
. The road climbs along the coast offering breathtaking ocean views before descending toward the imperial city.

**Transport options:**
- **Private car (recommended)**: 
about 4 hours with scenic stops, ~€25/pp; door-to-door service with a professional driver

- **Tourist bus**: 
3.5-4.5 hours, cost $6-15/pp

- **Train via Da Nang**: 
the spectacular Da Nang–Hue rail route hugs the sea, about 2.5-3 hours, $10-13/pp


**Stops along the way:**
- 
Lang Co Bay - recognized in 2009 as one of the 30 most beautiful bays in the world

- 
Hai Van Pass - one of the most spectacular coastal roads in Vietnam with panoramic ocean views

- 
Marble Mountains - limestone formations with natural caves, near Da Nang


**Reliable companies:**
- **Hoi An Transport Service**: [Link](https://hoiantransportservice.com/) - private transport since 2014, modern and clean cars, punctual and professional drivers
- **GetYourGuide**: door-to-door service with driver, option to choose the scenic route

### Moving On

**Hue → Phong Nha (Bus / Train)**


The direct bus from Hue to Phong Nha takes 3.5-4 hours and costs about 207,000 VND (~€8)
. Several companies operate on the route:

- **Queen Cafe Bus**: 
sleeper service with prices starting from 350,000 VND (~€14)

- **Hung Thanh Bus**: 
morning departures, arrival in Phong Nha in about 4 hours

- **DMZ Bus (with tour)**: 
600,000 VND (~€23) includes stops at the Vinh Moc Tunnels and the Ben Hai River


Alternatively, the Hue → Dong Hoi train (~2.5 hours) followed by a short transfer to Phong Nha is a solid scenic option.

**Bookings**: 
- Queen Cafe: +84 906413468
- **Online**: 12Go Asia to compare prices and schedules

### Safety

**General situation**: 
Hue is extremely safe for tourists with excellent infrastructure
 • 
A safe area confirmed in the official guides


**Common scams to avoid**:

1. **Taxi scam**: 
Tampered meters • Use Grab or reliable companies like Mai Linh and Vinasun


2. **Pickpocketing**: 
Common in markets and tourist attractions • Thieves work in groups

   - 
Don't use your phone near the roadside • Step into a shop or turn your back


3. **Motorbike snatch-thefts**: 
People on scooters snatching valuables

   - 
Use cross-body bags worn in front of your body


4. **Currency exchange**: 
Counterfeit banknotes from street money changers


**Precautions**:
- 
Keep copies of your documents separate from the originals • Use the hotel safe

- 
Always maintain awareness of your surroundings

- 
The key is to be aware of what's happening around you


**Emergency numbers**:
- 
Police: 113 • Ambulance: 115 • Fire brigade: 114

-
