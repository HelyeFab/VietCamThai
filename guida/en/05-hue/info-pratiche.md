## Practical Info — Hue

### Getting There

**Hoi An / Da Nang → Hue via Hai Van Pass**


The transfer from Hoi An to Hue over the Hai Van Pass takes about 4 hours and is one of the most scenic routes in Vietnam. The road climbs along the coast, serving up breathtaking ocean views before dropping down towards the imperial city.

**Transport options:**
- **Private car (recommended)**: about 4 hours with scenic stops, ~€25/pp; door-to-door with a professional driver
- **Tourist bus**: 3.5-4.5 hours, $6-15/pp
- **Train via Da Nang**: the spectacular Da Nang–Hue line hugs the sea, about 2.5-3 hours, $10-13/pp

**Stops along the way:**
- Lang Co Bay - named in 2009 as one of the 30 most beautiful bays in the world
- Hai Van Pass - one of Vietnam's most spectacular coastal roads, with sweeping ocean views
- Marble Mountains - limestone formations riddled with natural caves, near Da Nang

**Reliable companies:**
- **Hoi An Transport Service**: [Link](https://hoiantransportservice.com/) - private transport since 2014, clean and modern cars, punctual, professional drivers
- **GetYourGuide**: door-to-door with a driver, and the option to take the scenic route

### Moving On

**Hue → Phong Nha (Bus / Train)**


The direct bus from Hue to Phong Nha takes 3.5-4 hours and costs about 207,000 VND (~€8). Several companies work the route:

- **Queen Cafe Bus**: sleeper service, from 350,000 VND (~€14)
- **Hung Thanh Bus**: morning departures, reaching Phong Nha in about 4 hours
- **DMZ Bus (with tour)**: 600,000 VND (~€23), including stops at the Vinh Moc Tunnels and the Ben Hai River

Alternatively, the Hue → Dong Hoi train (~2.5 hours), followed by a short transfer to Phong Nha, is a solid scenic option.

**Bookings**: 
- Queen Cafe: +84 906413468
- **Online**: 12Go Asia to compare prices and schedules

### Safety

**General situation**: Hue is extremely safe for tourists, with excellent infrastructure • the official guides confirm it as a safe area

**Common scams to avoid**:

1. **Taxi scam**: rigged meters • use Grab or trusted firms like Mai Linh and Vinasun

2. **Pickpocketing**: common in markets and at tourist sites • thieves work in groups
   - Don't use your phone by the roadside • duck into a shop or turn your back first

3. **Motorbike snatch-thefts**: scooter riders grabbing valuables
   - Wear a cross-body bag on the front of your body

4. **Currency exchange**: fake banknotes from street money changers

**Precautions**:
- Keep copies of your documents apart from the originals • use the hotel safe
- Stay aware of your surroundings at all times
- The key is simply to keep an eye on what's going on around you

**Emergency numbers**:
- Police: 113 • Ambulance: 115 • Fire brigade: 114
- Hanoi Tourist Police Office: 28 Hang Dau Street (8:00-22:00), for reference

**For women travellers**: Vietnam ranked 6th in the 2024 list of the safest destinations for women travelling alone
