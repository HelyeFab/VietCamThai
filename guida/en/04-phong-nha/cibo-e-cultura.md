# FOOD & CULTURE - Phong Nha

## Where and What to Eat in Phong Nha

![Vietnamese street food stall](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Dried_food_market_Saigon.JPG/1280px-Dried_food_market_Saigon.JPG)

### Must-Try Local Dishes

**Cháo Canh** *(Porridge soup)*

The most popular dish in Quang Binh, similar to pho but with a unique twist. It's a lovely soup with a slightly yellowish broth, served with rice vermicelli, fish, prawns, pork and spring rolls
. 
It's the locals' favourite breakfast
. **Price**: 30,000-50,000 VND (€1.25-2)

**Grilled Chicken with Cheo Salt**

The free-range, naturally raised chicken has a chewy texture and a rich, sweet flavour
. 
It's often eaten with Cheo salt, a Phong Nha speciality made of chilli, lime leaves, coriander and Doi seeds
. **Price**: 150,000-200,000 VND (€6.30-8.40)

**Grilled Snakehead Fish**

One of the most distinctive dishes of the region, the snakehead fish is a freshwater predator known for its firm white meat with a delicate flavour, caught fresh from the region's rivers and lakes
. **Price**: 100,000-150,000 VND (€4.20-6.30)

**Snails Sautéed with La Lot Leaves**

The snails are gathered around Phong Nha, while the la lot leaves are selected for their freshness and earthy aroma. When the frying begins, the aroma of shallots, garlic and chillies fills the air
. **Price**: 80,000-120,000 VND (€3.35-5)

**Bánh Lọc** *(Tapioca cake)*

A speciality originally from Hué but prepared slightly differently in Quang Binh. It has a tapioca crust with a filling of prawns, pork and wood ear mushrooms. Many tourists go for the traditional tapioca cake wrapped in banana leaves
. **Price**: 10,000-15,000 VND (€0.40-0.65) per piece

**Local Street Food**

There are a few local eateries in the centre of Phong Nha. The most local are the small stalls right as you come off the highway. They set up every afternoon. It's not a bad spot for a super-local beer and a meal at the end of the day
.
💰 **Range**: Ultra-budget (20,000-40,000 VND / €0.85-1.70)

### Markets

There are no large central markets in Phong Nha like in the bigger cities. 
The areas near the markets are prime spots for the morning vendors who feed schoolchildren, market sellers and locals heading to work
.

**Best times for street food**:
- 
Breakfast: right after sunrise for unforgettable bowls of hot noodles, hearty rice porridge or glutinous rice cakes

- 
Lunch: from 11:30 to 13:00

- 
Dinner: from 17:00, with the period between 18:00 and 20:00 perfect for flaming hotpots, grilled seafood and barbecue sessions in the warm evening air

### Etiquette and Culture

**At the Table**:
- 
It's polite to avoid talking loudly, interrupting others or speaking with food in your mouth

- 
Asking about the food, how it was prepared, reflects interest and shows respect for the cook

- 
Shared condiments are commonly placed on every table. They're meant to be used! You'll usually find chilli sauce, fish sauce and maybe soy sauce

**With People**:
- 
Vietnamese people are very proud of their culture and cuisine, and love sharing it with foreigners. If you look lost, help is given freely

- 
Don't be afraid to laugh at yourself. You're likely to make a few mistakes. If you do, share a laugh with the vendor and remember for next time

- 
Bargaining is part of the culture, but always with a smile! Respect the vendors: avoid aggressive haggling. Dispose of your rubbish properly. Be patient: the crowds are part of the charm

**Practical Notes**:
- 
Each table usually has its own bin where you can put rubbish. Cutlery is offered in a shared container. Order first and pay afterwards. When you're ready to leave, you'll need to wave someone over to pay

- 
The busier the stall, the more likely it's worth the wait

### Useful Words

**Basic Food**:
- **Ăn** (ahn) - to eat
- **Uống** (oong) - to drink  
- **Cơm** (gom) - rice
- **Phở** (fuh) - noodle soup
- **Nước** (nook) - water

**At the Restaurant**:
- **Thực đơn** (tuk dohn) - menu
- **Tôi muốn** (toy moo-ohn) - I would like
- **Bao nhiêu tiền?** (bow nyew tyen) - how much does it cost?
- **Không cay** (kong kay) - not spicy
- **Ngon** (nawn) - delicious

**Useful**:
- **Xin chào** (sin chow) - hello
- **Cảm ơn** (gam ohn) - thank you
- **Xin lỗi** (sin loy) - excuse me
- **Không hiểu** (kong hyew) - I don't understand
- **Tiếng Anh** (tyeng anh) - English
