# FOOD & CULTURE - Phong Nha

## Where and What to Eat in Phong Nha

![Vietnamese street food stall](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Dried_food_market_Saigon.JPG/1280px-Dried_food_market_Saigon.JPG)

### Must-Try Local Dishes

**Cháo Canh** *(Porridge soup)*

The most popular dish in Quang Binh — think pho, but with a twist all its own. It's a lovely soup with a faintly golden broth, served with rice vermicelli, fish, prawns, pork and spring rolls, and it's the local breakfast of choice. **Price**: 30,000-50,000 VND (€1.25-2)

**Grilled Chicken with Cheo Salt**

This free-range chicken has a satisfyingly firm bite and a rich, sweet flavour, and it's usually eaten with Cheo salt — a Phong Nha speciality of chilli, lime leaves, coriander and Doi seeds. **Price**: 150,000-200,000 VND (€6.30-8.40)

**Grilled Snakehead Fish**

One of the region's signature dishes. The snakehead is a freshwater predator prized for its firm white flesh and delicate flavour, caught fresh from local rivers and lakes. **Price**: 100,000-150,000 VND (€4.20-6.30)

**Snails Sautéed with La Lot Leaves**

The snails are gathered around Phong Nha and the la lot leaves picked for their freshness and earthy scent. Once they hit the pan, the smell of shallots, garlic and chilli fills the air. **Price**: 80,000-120,000 VND (€3.35-5)

**Bánh Lọc** *(Tapioca cake)*

A speciality that started life in Hué but gets a slightly different treatment in Quang Binh: a chewy tapioca casing filled with prawns, pork and wood-ear mushrooms. Most visitors go for the traditional version, wrapped in banana leaves. **Price**: 10,000-15,000 VND (€0.40-0.65) per piece

### Our Recommended Restaurants and Street Food

**Phong Nha Coffee Station**

Ranked #1 on TripAdvisor with 5.0/5 stars across 250 reviews. The owner, Huynh, pulls his espresso from Dalat beans — the best coffee in Phong Nha!
📍 **Area**: Town centre
💰 **Range**: Budget (30,000-80,000 VND / €1.25-3.35)

**Thang Nhung BBQ**

Some guests come back every single evening of their stay — the spit-roast pork is that good (and the garlic water spinach is a treat too). The kitchen specialises in premium grilled dishes, from tender beef and juicy lamb to fresh, locally sourced seafood, with the meats marinated in a distinctive blend of spices and aromatic herbs.
📍 **Address**: 
Phong Nha National Park, Son Trach, Bo Trach, Quang Binh

💰 **Range**: Mid (100,000-200,000 VND / €4.20-8.40)

**Phong Nha Family Restaurant**

Ranked #3 on TripAdvisor with 4.9/5 stars across 82 reviews. Fresh food and drink come first here, with inventive dishes for Western, local, vegetarian and vegan diners, served from breakfast through brunch, lunch and dinner. There's also a lovely garden with a pool.
💰 **Range**: Mid (80,000-150,000 VND / €3.35-6.30)

**Mountain View Restaurant**

Ranked #8 on TripAdvisor with a perfect 5.0/5. The food is fantastic, and the mountain views are gorgeous.
💰 **Range**: Mid-high (120,000-250,000 VND / €5-10.50)

**Moi Moi (Bong Lai Valley)**

The Quang Binh-style barbecue chicken lunch was the best meal of our whole stay in Phong Nha — exceptional!

📍 **Area**: Bong Lai Valley (8km from the centre)
💰 **Range**: Budget (60,000-120,000 VND / €2.50-5)

**The Villas Restaurant**

For refined, beautifully cooked food, nowhere in Phong Nha beats The Villas — and its immaculate setting beside the Son River is a big part of the draw.
💰 **Range**: High (200,000-400,000 VND / €8.40-16.80)

**Capture Cafe**

The one place in town with a proper international café feel, serving pizza, pasta, sandwiches and coffee. The house coffee comes from nearby Khe Sanh, and it's a popular bolt-hole for travellers craving a taste of home.
💰 **Range**: Mid (100,000-180,000 VND / €4.20-7.55)

**Local Street Food**

There are a handful of local eateries in the centre of Phong Nha, but the most authentic are the little stalls right where you come off the highway, which set up every afternoon. It's a fine spot for an ice-cold local beer and a meal at the end of the day.
💰 **Range**: Ultra-budget (20,000-40,000 VND / €0.85-1.70)

### Markets

Phong Nha has no big central markets like the larger cities do. The streets around the smaller markets, though, are prime territory for the morning vendors who feed schoolchildren, stallholders and locals on their way to work.

**Best times for street food**:
- 
Breakfast: just after sunrise, for unforgettable bowls of hot noodles, hearty rice porridge or glutinous rice cakes

- 
Lunch: from 11:30 to 13:00

- 
Dinner: from 17:00, with 18:00 to 20:00 the sweet spot for sizzling hotpots, grilled seafood and barbecue in the warm evening air

### Etiquette and Culture

**At the Table**:
- 
It's polite not to talk loudly, interrupt others or speak with your mouth full

- 
Asking about a dish and how it was made shows genuine interest and respect for the cook

- 
Shared condiments sit on every table and are there to be used — usually chilli sauce, fish sauce and sometimes soy sauce

**With People**:
- 
Vietnamese people are hugely proud of their culture and cuisine and love sharing it with foreigners — if you look lost, someone will usually step in to help

- 
Don't be afraid to laugh at yourself. You'll make a few mistakes; when you do, share the laugh with the vendor and remember for next time

- 
Bargaining is part of the culture, but always with a smile. Respect the vendors and skip the aggressive haggling, dispose of your rubbish properly, and be patient — the crowds are part of the charm

**Practical Notes**:
- 
Each table usually has its own bin for rubbish, and cutlery comes in a shared container. You order first and pay afterwards, so when you're ready to leave, just wave someone over to settle up

- 
The busier the stall, the more likely it's worth the wait

### Useful Words

**Basic Food**:
- **Ăn** (ahn) - to eat
- **Uống** (oong) - to drink  
- **Cơm** (gom) - rice
- **Phở** (fuh) - noodle soup
- **Nước** (nook) - water

**At the Restaurant**:
- **Thực đơn** (tuk dohn) - menu
- **Tôi muốn** (toy moo-ohn) - I would like
- **Bao nhiêu tiền?** (bow nyew tyen) - how much does it cost?
- **Không cay** (kong kay) - not spicy
- **Ngon** (nawn) - delicious

**Useful**:
- **Xin chào** (sin chow) - hello
- **Cảm ơn** (gam ohn) - thank you
- **Xin lỗi** (sin loy) - excuse me
- **Không hiểu** (kong hyew) - I don't understand
- **Tiếng Anh** (tyeng anh) - English
