## Day 23 — October 23, 2026 — Arriving in the kingdom of caves

### Morning

![Paradise Cave](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/ThienDuongCave1.jpg/1280px-ThienDuongCave1.jpg)

Arriving in Phong Nha from Hué (train to Dong Hoi plus a transfer, or a direct bus, about 4 hours), you'll have roughly 2-3 hours to settle in before starting the adventure. 
The boat service to the Phong Nha Cave opens at 8:00 in the morning and runs until mid-afternoon (the last boats leave around 15:30-16:00)
.

**Phong Nha Cave by boat (9:30-12:30)**

The entrance ticket costs 150,000 VND (about $6-7) per adult
, while 
the boat costs about 700,000 VND per vessel (up to 12 people)
. In practice, 
groups often share the boat and split the cost
 — perfect for the four of you!

The experience begins with 
a peaceful 25-30 minute cruise through the countryside and rice paddies on the Son River, with towering karst peaks on the horizon
. 
Once you reach the mouth of the Phong Nha Cave, the boat passes under a low rocky vault and enters the dark interior. The cool, damp cave air is a welcome relief from the midday heat
.

**Official site**: [Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park](https://phongnhakebang.vn/)

### Afternoon

**Relaxing by the river (13:00-15:00)**

![The Son River valley at Phong Nha](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Stories_and_legends_of_Annam_%28IA_cu31924023423142%29.pdf/page1-500px-Stories_and_legends_of_Annam_%28IA_cu31924023423142%29.pdf.jpg)

After the cave, enjoy a relaxed lunch at one of the riverside restaurants. 
Try the grilled Son River carp (Cá Trắm) and grilled eel
 at the **Son River Floating Restaurant**.

The afternoon is perfect for relaxing along the banks of the Son River, taking a few scenic photos, or exploring the small centre of Phong Nha on foot.

**Adventurous alternative: Duck Stop**

If you prefer a more original (and fun) experience, rent bikes or a motorbike and head to the famous **Duck Stop** in the Bong Lai valley. 
It costs about 150,000 VND ($6) for the full experience with ducks, a buffalo ride, and snacks. The buffalo ride costs 50,000 VND extra
.

The Duck Stop is a family farm set in the lush, scenic Bong Lai valley, about 10-15 minutes from the centre of Phong Nha
. 
Rent a motorbike or a bike in Phong Nha town and enjoy the scenic 11 km ride through the Bong Lai valley. It's an adventure in itself!

### Evening

**Where to dine**

- **Thang Nhung**: 
The best pork and noodle shop in the world (probably) — the name says it all

- **Phong Nha Underground Restaurant**: 
A refined restaurant that is genuinely a cut above many/all the other restaurants in Phong Nha. We ate at about seven different places in town, and we found Underground to be the best. In fact, we ate there twice

- **Capture Cafe**: 
The only place in town with an international café atmosphere, serving pizza, pasta, sandwiches and coffee

The best hours to visit: 8:30-10:30 or 15:30-17:00 for the cooler weather and the best light for photos
.

### Tips of the Day

- 
Arriving early at the boat station helps you avoid the later crowds. Many travellers recommend going first thing in the morning if possible

- 
Bring cash: there are no ATMs at the station, so bring Vietnamese dong for tickets and tips

- 
Motorbike rental should cost about 150k VND per day excluding petrol. You can haggle down to 120k VND depending on your skills!

- If it rains, 
the rains from September to November are heavy enough to make the rivers overflow and can cause flooding; so sometimes it is closed

## Day 24 — October 24, 2026 — The largest underground palace in the world

### Morning

**Paradise Cave (8:30-12:00)**

This is 
a magnificent dry cave about 25 km away; it is often paired with a boat trip here
. 
Adults: 250,000 VND (~$10). Children (1.1m – 1.3m): 125,000 VND. Children (Under 1.1m): Free
.

Electric buggy (Optional): 15,000 VND one way or 25,000 VND return. This saves you a 1.6 km walk from the ticket office to the foot of the cave's mountain
.

Paradise Cave will leave you speechless: 
The cave's scale reaches 72 m high and 150 m wide — it's breathtaking
. 
A standard visit along the 1 km wooden walkway typically takes 1 hour
.

**Pro-tip**: 
To avoid the large tour groups that arrive around 10:30, aim to be at the entrance of Paradise Cave by 8:00. You'll have the walkway almost entirely to yourselves for photographs
.

**Official site**: [Paradise Cave](https://phongnhakebang.vn/en/paradise-cave)

### Afternoon

**Adventurous alternative: Dark Cave (13:30-16:30)**

If you prefer adrenaline to contemplation, the Dark Cave offers a completely different experience. 
There are two different packages available: 450,000 VND (about $19) for adults and 150,000 VND (about $6) for children (1m-1.3m tall). Or 250,000 VND (about $11)
.

Add in the longest zipline in Vietnam (400 metres across the stunning Chay River), kayaking and swimming, and you'll have one of the most thrilling destinations in central Vietnam
.

**The experience includes**:
- 
Launch yourself from a platform and zip 50 metres straight over the jade-green Chay River at speeds up to 45 km/h — the longest double-cable zipline in Vietnam — before plunging into the water to swim the final stretch to the cave entrance

- 
Explore its fascinating stalactite and stalagmite formations and enjoy a natural mud bath inside the cave

- 
The full Dark Cave experience takes about 2.5-3 hours from start to finish, including zipline, kayaking, cave exploration, mud bath, swimming and the return trip

### Evening

**Dinner with a view**

- **East Hill Phong Nha**: 
Hilltop dining with panoramic views over rice paddies and limestone mountains. To try: scenic breakfasts and local delicacies

- **Phong Nha Family Restaurant**: 
A lovely restaurant for enjoying good home-style Vietnamese food on a rainy day. The beef Pho was unreal — I asked for it without coriander and they were very accommodating. I'd definitely go back and highly recommend it

### Tips of the Day

- **For Paradise Cave**: 
Wear comfortable walking shoes or sneakers. Even though it's 18°C inside, you'll sweat on the climb up the mountain. A light jacket is recommended if you get cold easily

- **For Dark Cave**: 
Swimwear/quick-drying clothes and water-appropriate shoes (no flip-flops that slip off). They'll turn you away at the entrance if you don't bring proper swimwear, and you could end up buying swimwear at inflated prices from the shop inside!

- 
Children under 1.4 metres are not allowed to do the zipline. Women more than three months pregnant will not be able to take part in this tour for your safety

- 
During the rainy season (October-February), the cave sometimes closes early if water levels rise too high — always call ahead during the rainy season to confirm the cave is open for all activities

**Estimated daily budget for 4 people**: 
- Paradise Cave: ~€40 (tickets + transport)
- Dark Cave: ~€80 (tickets + transport)
- Meals: ~€30
- Accommodation: ~€20
