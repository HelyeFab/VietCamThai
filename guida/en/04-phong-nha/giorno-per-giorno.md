## Day 23 — October 23, 2026 — Arriving in the kingdom of caves

### Morning

![Paradise Cave](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/ThienDuongCave1.jpg/1280px-ThienDuongCave1.jpg)

Arriving in Phong Nha from Hué (train to Dong Hoi plus a transfer, or a direct bus, about 4 hours), you'll have a good 2-3 hours to settle in before the adventure begins. The boat service to the Phong Nha Cave opens at 8:00 and runs until mid-afternoon, with the last boats leaving around 15:30-16:00.

**Phong Nha Cave by boat (9:30-12:30)**

The entrance ticket is 150,000 VND (about $6-7) per adult, and the boat costs around 700,000 VND per vessel (up to 12 people). In practice, groups often share a boat and split the cost — perfect for the four of you!

It begins with a peaceful 25-30 minute cruise along the Son River, past countryside and rice paddies, with towering karst peaks on the horizon. At the mouth of the Phong Nha Cave, the boat slips under a low rocky arch into the darkness inside, where the cool, damp air comes as a welcome relief from the midday heat.

**Official site**: [Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park](https://phongnhakebang.vn/)

### Afternoon

**Relaxing by the river (13:00-15:00)**

![The Son River valley at Phong Nha](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Stories_and_legends_of_Annam_%28IA_cu31924023423142%29.pdf/page1-500px-Stories_and_legends_of_Annam_%28IA_cu31924023423142%29.pdf.jpg)

After the cave, settle in for a relaxed lunch at one of the riverside restaurants. Try the grilled Son River carp (Cá Trắm) and grilled eel at the **Son River Floating Restaurant**.

The afternoon is made for lingering on the banks of the Son River, taking a few scenic photos, or exploring Phong Nha's small centre on foot.

**Adventurous alternative: Duck Stop**

For something more offbeat (and a lot of fun), rent bikes or a motorbike and head for the famous **Duck Stop** in the Bong Lai valley. The full experience — ducks, a buffalo ride and snacks — costs about 150,000 VND ($6), with the buffalo ride 50,000 VND on top.

The Duck Stop is a family farm tucked into the lush, scenic Bong Lai valley, about 10-15 minutes from central Phong Nha. Rent a motorbike or bicycle in town and enjoy the scenic 11 km ride out through the valley — an adventure in itself!

### Evening

**Where to dine**

- **Thang Nhung**: 
The best pork-and-noodle shop in the world, probably — the name says it all

- **Phong Nha Underground Restaurant**: 
A refined spot that's genuinely a cut above the rest in Phong Nha. We ate at about seven different places in town and rated Underground the best — in fact, we went back a second time

- **Capture Cafe**: 
The one place in town with an international café feel, serving pizza, pasta, sandwiches and coffee

Best times to go: 8:30-10:30 or 15:30-17:00, for the cooler air and the best light for photos.

### Tips of the Day

- 
Get to the boat station early to beat the later crowds — many travellers swear by going first thing in the morning if you can

- 
Bring cash: there are no ATMs at the station, so carry Vietnamese dong for tickets and tips

- 
Motorbike rental runs about 150k VND a day, petrol excluded, and you can usually haggle down to 120k VND depending on how good you are!

- If it rains: the September-to-November rains are heavy enough to make the rivers overflow and flood, so the cave sometimes closes

## Day 24 — October 24, 2026 — The largest underground palace in the world

### Morning

**Paradise Cave (8:30-12:00)**

A magnificent dry cave about 25 km away, often combined with a boat trip out here. Adults: 250,000 VND (~$10). Children (1.1m – 1.3m): 125,000 VND. Children (under 1.1m): free.

Electric buggy (optional): 15,000 VND one way or 25,000 VND return, saving you a 1.6 km walk from the ticket office to the foot of the cave's mountain.

Paradise Cave will leave you speechless: it soars to 72 m high and 150 m wide — utterly breathtaking. A standard visit along the 1 km wooden walkway takes about an hour.

**Pro-tip**: 
To beat the big tour groups that roll in around 10:30, aim to be at the Paradise Cave entrance by 8:00. You'll have the walkway almost to yourselves for photos.

**Official site**: [Paradise Cave](https://phongnhakebang.vn/en/paradise-cave)

### Afternoon

**Adventurous alternative: Dark Cave (13:30-16:30)**

If you'd rather have adrenaline than quiet contemplation, the Dark Cave is a completely different beast. There are two packages: 450,000 VND (about $19) for adults and 150,000 VND (about $6) for children (1m-1.3m tall), or 250,000 VND (about $11).

Throw in the longest zipline in Vietnam (400 metres across the stunning Chay River), plus kayaking and swimming, and you've got one of the most thrilling spots in central Vietnam.

**The experience includes**:
- 
Launch off a platform and zip 50 metres straight over the jade-green Chay River at up to 45 km/h — Vietnam's longest double-cable zipline — before plunging in to swim the final stretch to the cave entrance

- 
Explore the cave's fascinating stalactites and stalagmites and wallow in a natural mud bath inside

- 
The full Dark Cave experience takes about 2.5-3 hours from start to finish — zipline, kayaking, cave exploration, mud bath, swimming and the trip back

### Evening

**Dinner with a view**

- **East Hill Phong Nha**: 
Hilltop dining with sweeping views over rice paddies and limestone mountains. Worth trying: the scenic breakfasts and local delicacies

- **Phong Nha Family Restaurant**: 
A lovely place for good home-style Vietnamese food on a rainy day. The beef pho was unreal — I asked for it without coriander and they happily obliged. I'd go back in a heartbeat and can't recommend it enough

### Tips of the Day

- **For Paradise Cave**: 
Wear comfortable walking shoes or trainers. It's 18°C inside, but you'll work up a sweat on the climb up the mountain. Pack a light jacket if you feel the cold

- **For Dark Cave**: 
Bring swimwear or quick-drying clothes and shoes that stay on in the water (no flip-flops). Turn up without proper swimwear and they'll send you back from the entrance — or you'll end up paying over the odds for it at the shop inside!

- 
Children under 1.4 metres can't do the zipline, and women more than three months pregnant can't take part in this tour, for their own safety

- 
During the rainy season (October-February), the cave sometimes closes early if the water rises too high — always call ahead in that period to confirm it's open for all activities

**Estimated daily budget for 4 people**: 
- Paradise Cave: ~€40 (tickets + transport)
- Dark Cave: ~€80 (tickets + transport)
- Meals: ~€30
- Accommodation: ~€20
