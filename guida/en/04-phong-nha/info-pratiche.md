## Practical Info — Phong Nha

### Getting There

**From Hué to Phong Nha (main option, ~4 hours)**

Phong Nha is easily reached from Hué, your previous stop. There are two options and they are equivalent in total duration (about 4 hours).

**By train + transfer (more scenic):**
- 
Take the train from Hué to Dong Hoi (about 2.5-3 hours), the most scenic stretch of the North-South railway along the coast
- 
From Dong Hoi (45 km from Phong Nha) take a transfer to the village: a private transfer costs about 650,000 VND (about €25) for a 7-seat car, or a shared shuttle is cheaper
- 
Train tickets can be bought online through [dsvn.vn](https://dsvn.vn) or apps like Vexere/12go.asia

**By direct bus/limousine (simpler):**
- 
Numerous limousines and tourist buses connect Hué directly to Phong Nha in about 4 hours, with hotel pickup
- 
Indicative price 200,000-300,000 VND (€8-12) per person
- 
Ask your accommodation to book for you: they often get better last-minute prices than third-party sites

**Practical tips:**
- 
Bring snacks and water for the trip
- 
Arrive at the departure point at least 30 minutes before departure
- 
Book ahead in high season: seats on the direct limousines sell out

**Departing — towards Ninh Binh:**

From Phong Nha the next stop is Ninh Binh, reachable by overnight sleeper buses (about €12-15 per person, 8-10 hours). Reliable companies like Queen Cafe and Full Moon leave in the evening (typically 19:30-21:00) arriving at dawn; you can book through the Vexere app or ask your accommodation.

### Getting Around

**Motorbikes (the best way)**
- 
Rental: about 120,000 VND (€4) per day, you can haggle if they ask for 150,000 VND

- 
Legally you need an International Driving Permit (IDP) for motorbikes over 50cc. However, most rental places don't check licences and police presence in the national park is minimal

- 
Paradise Cave is 25 km away, Dark Cave 5 km, and the Phong Nha Cave boat station is in town

- 
In town you'll find plenty of motorbike rentals and they can give you a map to explore


**Bicycles**
- 
Many people rent bicycles for the nearby attractions

- 
Some hostels and guesthouses offer bicycle rental


**Grab and ride-hailing apps**
- 
You can use the Grab, Be and Xanh SM apps with an internet connection

- 
Cheaper prices than traditional taxis, with clear routes and prices before you book


**Xe Om (motorbike taxi) and local transport**
- 
You can book a xe om or ask your hotel to arrange transport

- 
Perfect for those who can't walk far or cycle, travelling on almost any road and meeting the locals

### Connectivity & SIM

**Network coverage in Phong Nha**

If you're travelling to Phong Nha, Viettel is essential. Phong Nha is surrounded by dense forests and karst mountains. Viettel has the strongest coverage near the national park and along the Ho Chi Minh road. Mobifone and Vinaphone drop out frequently outside town
.

**Where to buy a SIM**
- 
At the airport: you'll find local provider kiosks at the airports in Hanoi, Nha Trang and Ho Chi Minh, so you can connect as soon as you land

- 
In Vietnam: if you left the airport without a Vietnamese SIM, you can get one at official stores throughout the country, as well as at convenience stores like Circle K, 7-Eleven and Minimart

- 
You can also buy a SIM in Ho Chi Minh City, Hai Phong, Can Tho and other cities by visiting a post office. There are mobile provider stores in every city, including Viettel, Mobifone and Vinaphone. These stores often have English-speaking staff


**Prices and plans**
- 
All Viettel plans offer both data and call minutes, with prices from 60,000 VND (~€2) to 200,000 VND (~€6.50). Validity periods range from 15 to 30 days

- 
Tourist SIM packages cost 100,000-200,000 VND (€4-8) for 30 days with generous data. 30 GB of 4G data for €4. Vietnam's data prices are among the lowest in the world

- 
SIM registration is mandatory in Vietnam for both locals and foreigners


**WiFi**

There is WiFi EVERYWHERE in Vietnam! It's available in many hotels, restaurants, cafés and public spaces. However, having a local SIM guarantees internet access wherever you go, even in areas without Wi-Fi coverage
.

**eSIM (a modern alternative)**
- 
You can get an international eSIM instead of a physical SIM and enjoy instant connectivity. It only takes a couple of minutes to buy, set up and activate your eSIM

- Recommended providers: Airalo, Saily (Viettel network for better rural coverage)

### Safety

**Emergency numbers**
- 
Police: 113, Ambulance: 115, Fire brigade: 114

- 
Tourist police: 1800 1091

- 
English-language support is limited, especially outside the main cities. Response times vary and communication barriers can slow things down


**General safety in Phong Nha**
Phong Nha is generally very safe for tourists. It's a quiet rural area with minimal crime. The main risks are related to outdoor activities and transport.

**Specific precautions**
- **Motorbikes:** 
Riding without a proper licence can complicate insurance claims in the event of accidents

- **Caves and trekking:** Always tell someone your plans, especially for advanced caves
- **Weather:** 
Heavy rains (especially October-December) can flood the cave entrances and force closures. For reliable conditions, visit in the dry season (roughly November-April)

- **Nature:** Respect the local wildlife and stay on marked trails

**Documents**
- 
Hotels must register every guest with the local police, which is why your passport is required at check-in. If you stay in a private house, you must register your stay with the local authorities within 24 hours


**Travel insurance**

Travel insurance is a smart move in Vietnam. Road accidents, motorbike mishaps, food poisoning and tropical illnesses are common pitfalls for travellers. Good coverage should include medical treatment, evacuation and trip interruption
.
