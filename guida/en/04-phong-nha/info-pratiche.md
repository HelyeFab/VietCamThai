## Practical Info — Phong Nha

### Getting There

**From Hué to Phong Nha (main option, ~4 hours)**

Phong Nha is an easy hop from Hué, your previous stop. There are two options, and they take much the same time overall (about 4 hours).

**By train + transfer (more scenic):**
- 
Take the train from Hué to Dong Hoi (about 2.5-3 hours) — the most scenic stretch of the North-South railway, hugging the coast
- 
From Dong Hoi (45 km from Phong Nha), take a transfer to the village: a private car (7 seats) costs about 650,000 VND (about €25), or a shared shuttle for less
- 
Buy train tickets online at [dsvn.vn](https://dsvn.vn) or through apps like Vexere/12go.asia

**By direct bus/limousine (simpler):**
- 
Plenty of limousines and tourist buses run direct from Hué to Phong Nha in about 4 hours, with hotel pickup
- 
Roughly 200,000-300,000 VND (€8-12) per person
- 
Ask your accommodation to book it for you — they often land better last-minute prices than the third-party sites

**Practical tips:**
- 
Pack snacks and water for the journey
- 
Get to the departure point at least 30 minutes early
- 
Book ahead in high season — seats on the direct limousines sell out

**Departing — towards Ninh Binh:**

From Phong Nha, the next stop is Ninh Binh, reached by overnight sleeper bus (about €12-15 per person, 8-10 hours). Reliable operators like Queen Cafe and Full Moon leave in the evening (typically 19:30-21:00) and arrive at dawn; book through the Vexere app or ask your accommodation.

### Getting Around

**Motorbikes (the best way)**
- 
Rental: about 120,000 VND (€4) a day — haggle if they quote 150,000 VND

- 
Legally, motorbikes over 50cc require an International Driving Permit (IDP). In practice, most rental places don't check licences and there's barely any police presence in the national park

- 
Paradise Cave is 25 km away, Dark Cave 5 km, and the Phong Nha Cave boat station is right in town

- 
There are plenty of motorbike rentals in town, and they can give you a map to explore with


**Bicycles**
- 
Plenty of visitors rent bicycles for the nearby attractions

- 
Some hostels and guesthouses rent bicycles too


**Grab and ride-hailing apps**
- 
The Grab, Be and Xanh SM apps all work as long as you have an internet connection

- 
Cheaper than traditional taxis, with the route and price shown up front before you book


**Xe Om (motorbike taxi) and local transport**
- 
Book a xe om yourself, or ask your hotel to arrange transport

- 
Ideal if you can't walk far or cycle — they'll take almost any road, and you'll meet a few locals along the way

### Connectivity & SIM

**Network coverage in Phong Nha**

If you're heading to Phong Nha, a Viettel SIM is essential. The area is hemmed in by dense forest and karst mountains, and Viettel has the strongest coverage near the national park and along the Ho Chi Minh road. Mobifone and Vinaphone drop out often once you leave town.

**Where to buy a SIM**
- 
At the airport: look for the provider kiosks at Hanoi, Nha Trang and Ho Chi Minh airports, so you can get online the moment you land

- 
Around the country: if you left the airport without a Vietnamese SIM, you can pick one up at official stores nationwide, or at convenience stores like Circle K, 7-Eleven and Minimart

- 
You can also buy a SIM at a post office in Ho Chi Minh City, Hai Phong, Can Tho and other cities. Every city has Viettel, Mobifone and Vinaphone stores, and their staff often speak some English


**Prices and plans**
- 
Every Viettel plan bundles data and call minutes, priced from 60,000 VND (~€2) to 200,000 VND (~€6.50), valid for 15 to 30 days

- 
Tourist SIM packages run 100,000-200,000 VND (€4-8) for 30 days with generous data — 30 GB of 4G for €4. Vietnam's data prices are among the lowest in the world

- 
SIM registration is compulsory in Vietnam, for locals and foreigners alike


**WiFi**

There's WiFi EVERYWHERE in Vietnam — in most hotels, restaurants, cafés and public spaces. Even so, a local SIM guarantees you're online wherever you go, including spots with no WiFi at all.

**eSIM (a modern alternative)**
- 
Skip the physical SIM and go for an international eSIM instead, for instant connectivity — it takes just a couple of minutes to buy, set up and activate

- Recommended providers: Airalo, Saily (Viettel network for better rural coverage)

### Safety

**Emergency numbers**
- 
Police: 113, Ambulance: 115, Fire brigade: 114

- 
Tourist police: 1800 1091

- 
English-language support is limited, especially outside the main cities. Response times vary, and the language barrier can slow things down


**General safety in Phong Nha**
Phong Nha is generally very safe for tourists. It's a quiet rural area with minimal crime. The main risks are related to outdoor activities and transport.

**Specific precautions**
- **Motorbikes:** 
Riding without a proper licence can complicate any insurance claim if you have an accident

- **Caves and trekking:** Always tell someone your plans, especially for the more advanced caves
- **Weather:** 
Heavy rain (especially October-December) can flood the cave entrances and force closures. For dependable conditions, come in the dry season (roughly November-April)

- **Nature:** Respect the local wildlife and stay on marked trails

**Documents**
- 
Hotels have to register every guest with the local police, which is why they ask for your passport at check-in. If you stay in a private home, you must register your stay with the local authorities within 24 hours


**Travel insurance**

Travel insurance is a smart move in Vietnam. Road accidents, motorbike spills, food poisoning and tropical illness are all common pitfalls for travellers. Good cover should include medical treatment, evacuation and trip interruption.
