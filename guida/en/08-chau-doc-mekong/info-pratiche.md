## Practical Info — Chau Doc & Mekong

### Getting There

**Speedboat from Phnom Penh (Cambodia)**


You reach Chau Doc by heading down the Mekong by speedboat from Phnom Penh: the journey takes about 5-6 hours (including the procedures at the Cambodia-Vietnam river border) and costs between $30-65 per person. It's one of the most striking crossings of the entire itinerary.

**Reliable companies:**
- **Hang Chau Tourist** (www.hangchautourist.vn) — the main one on this route, departing in the morning from Phnom Penh and arriving in Chau Doc in the early afternoon
- **Blue Cruiser** — a reliable alternative

**Practical details:**
- Vietnamese visa: prepare the e-visa before departure; the procedures take place at the river border (1-2 hours)
- Boarding: International Floating Port, Phnom Penh | Arrival: Sunrise Palace, 03A Le Loi Street, Chau Doc


### Moving On — Bus to Ho Chi Minh City


From Chau Doc you continue on to Ho Chi Minh City by bus: about 5-6 hours, with departures every 2 hours from 5:00 to 23:00 and tickets starting at 8 dollars (about €7.5).

**Recommended companies:**
- **Phuong Trang (Futa Bus)** - The absolute first choice
  -
Uses double-decker sleeper buses with fixed berths for 40-44 passengers

  -
Departs from Chau Doc station, arrives at Mien Tay Bus Station HCMC, duration 6h 15min, price about €10

  - Online booking: [Baolau](https://booking.baolau.com/en/s/ho-chi-minh/chau-doc) or [12Go](https://12go.asia)

- **Hue Nghia** - Budget alternative
  -
Economy tickets from 7 dollars (€6.5), duration 8h 46min


**Stations:**
- **Departure**:
Chau Doc Bus Station (Ton Duc Thang Street, Vinh My Ward)

- **Arrival**:
Mien Tay Bus Station (395 Kinh Duong Vuong, An Lac Ward, Binh Tan District)


**💡 Insider trick**:
Arrive at the station 30-60 minutes before departure
 and keep your phone charged for communicating with the driver via WhatsApp.

### Getting Around

**Local transport available:**

**🚗 Grab & Apps**
-
Grab is the most widely used app in Vietnam, along with Be and XanhSM

-
A Grab-bike costs 1-2 dollars per ride (about €1-2)

-
A Vietnamese SIM is needed to communicate with the driver


**🚕 Traditional taxis**
-
Fares: about 10,000 VND for the first km, 15,000 VND for the following 30 km

-
Reliable companies: Mai Linh (green) and Vinasun


**🛵 Xe Om (moto-taxi)**
-
Cheap and fast in traffic, called "xe om"

- Always negotiate the price first

**🚲 Distinctive rides**
-
Xe Loi (rickshaw): specific to Chau Doc, a trailer pulled by a bicycle for 2 people

-
Cyclo: about 100,000-150,000 VND per hour per person (€4-6)

### Connectivity & SIM

**📱 Mobile network in the Mekong Delta**

**Coverage:**
-
Viettel offers the strongest and most reliable 4G/LTE coverage in the Mekong Delta, especially in rural areas

-
Vinaphone is strong in urban centers and in the Mekong Delta region

-
The signal thins out in rural areas and along the winding river roads


**SIM options:**
- **Local physical SIM**:
100,000-200,000 VND (€4-8) with plenty of data

- **eSIM**:
Recommended for first arrivals, immediate activation at the airport

-
Tourist packages of 30-60GB for around 10-15 USD from Viettel, Vinaphone, Mobifone


**Where to buy:**
-
Tan Son Nhat Airport, shops in Districts 1 and 3, Circle K, Vinmart

-
Passport required by Vietnamese law


**📶 WiFi:**
- Most hotels offer free WiFi
-
Public WiFi in remote areas is often slow or unstable


### Safety

**🛡️ General safety level**


Vietnam is one of the safest destinations in Southeast Asia in 2025-2026, with low rates of violent crime and improving infrastructure
.
It is considered safer than Cambodia or the Philippines, with violent crimes against foreigners rare and the main risks limited to petty theft, road accidents and tourist scams
.

**⚠️ Common scams to avoid:**

1. **Transport scams**
   -
Taxis without a meter or with rigged meters - use Grab or a hotel taxi

   -
Motorbike-rental scam: fake damage on return - always photograph the condition beforehand


2. **Restaurant scams**
   -
Never sit down without prices clearly displayed on the menu

   -
"Free" snacks and wet wipes often carry a small charge - it's a local custom


3. **Digital scams 2025**
   -
Tampered QR codes at tourist sites - only scan from official sources

   -
Fake booking sites - always verify and book through official channels


**📞 Emergency numbers:**
- **Ambulance**:
115

- **Police**: 113
- **Fire brigade**: 114
- **Hanoi tourist police**:
28 Hang Dau Street (8:00-22:00, assistance in English)


**🚨 Specific precautions:**
-
Use zip-up shoulder bags, keep valuables hidden

-
Only use ATMs inside banks or secure shopping centers

-
Never drink tap water, only use sealed bottled water

-
Learn emergency phrases: "Tôi cần bác sĩ" (I need a doctor), "Giúp tôi với" (help me)


**👥 Specific safety:**
-
Vietnam is very safe for solo female travelers thanks to safe cities and active traveler communities

-
Excellent LGBTQ+ welcome, second place in Southeast Asia after Thailand


**💡 Final tip**:
The vast majority of Vietnamese people are welcoming and honest, especially if you share a common language. In tourist centers, maintain a basic level of vigilance
.
