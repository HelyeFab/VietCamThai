## Chau Doc & Mekong — Overview

Welcome to the beating heart of the Mekong Delta, where rivers weave together like silver veins beneath endless skies and the sun sets behind sugar palms swaying in the wind.
Chau Doc is a border town near Cambodia with a completely different atmosphere
, a crossroads of cultures where
Vietnamese, Chinese (Hoa), Cham and Khmer live together in harmony in the town and its surroundings, keeping their own culture and traditions visible through their looks and dress and in the buildings
. This is the land where sacred Sam Mountain rises from the endless rice paddies like an island of spirituality, and where
it is considered the spiritual heart of the Mekong Delta, and people from all over Vietnam come to pay their respects
.

![The Mekong Delta](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Can_Tho%2C_Vietnam%2C_Floating_Market%2C_Rawing.jpg/1280px-Can_Tho%2C_Vietnam%2C_Floating_Market%2C_Rawing.jpg)

It is also one of the best places in the region to see a more local and less commercial side of the Delta
. Here you can navigate through
the Tra Su Cajuput Forest, a beautiful flooded forest with boat trips along green waterways
, and lose yourself in the floating villages where life flows to the age-old rhythm of the great river.

### History & Culture

The story of Chau Doc is a fascinating tale of cultural fusion rooted in ancient Cambodia.
Chau Doc belonged to the territory of the various kingdoms that dominated the Mekong Delta area, such as the Kingdom of Funan, the Kingdom of Chenla and the Khmer Empire. The territory became Vietnamese about 300 years ago, when Mac Cuu, a Chinese general, began to expand Vietnamese and Chinese settlements deeper into the Khmer Empire
.

![Sam Mountain in Chau Doc](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a1/Mountain_Nui_Sam%2C_Chau_Doc.jpg)

In 1815, King Gia Long (Nguyen Anh) of the Nguyen dynasty ordered the construction of the Chau Doc Fort on the western bank of the river. A Chinese mandarin named Diep Hoi was assigned as head of Chau Doc, and to encourage people to settle and work in this area, loans would be granted to immigrants to reclaim land or start businesses in these wild, marshy territories
.

But it is today's cultural richness that makes Chau Doc truly special.
The Cham around Chau Doc practice a rustic form of Sunni Islam, most likely introduced by seafaring Muslim traders in the late 10th and early 11th centuries and reinforced by the arrival of Muslim Cham from Cambodia into the Mekong Delta in the mid-19th century. Thanks to its close proximity to the Cambodian border, Chau Doc is home to several ethnic and religious communities where Cambodian Buddhists, Vietnamese Buddhists, ethnic Chinese and Muslim Cham live peacefully side by side
.

A fascinating fact:
Children here study Vietnamese at school and Arabic in the evening at the local mosque, built with the help of Muslim brothers from overseas. While the community is exclusive (those who wish to marry must convert to Islam), they mix freely with their Vietnamese and Khmer neighbors
.

### When to Go

October is one of the best times to visit the Mekong Delta!
The flood season, running from September to December, is the best time to visit the Mekong Delta. During this period, the Mekong Delta enjoys a pleasant climate with very little rain
.

**What weather to expect in mid-October:**
- **Temperatures**:
During the day the temperature is very hot in Chau Doc at 33°C. When night falls, temperatures generally drop to 25°C in Chau Doc

- **Rainfall**:
Chau Doc can be quite rainy during October, receiving around 300 mm of precipitation over 18 rainy days
, but
the summer monsoon rains gradually ease off in the South, promising the return of summer

- **General conditions**:
Between September and November, you'll see them in the early morning or late afternoon, when they are most active and the forest floods
 - perfect for your visit to the Tra Su Forest!

**The upside**: it is the perfect season to see the lotus flowers in full bloom and the birdlife in the flooded forest.

### Orientation

Chau Doc is a compact, easy-to-navigate town, built along the banks of the Hau Giang River (Bassac River).
The town sits between the Hau Giang River, a branch of the Mekong River, and the man-made Vinh Te canal
.

**How it's laid out:**

- **Town center**: Along the riverfront, where you'll find the local market, hotels and restaurants. This is where
the Vietnamese and Chinese generally live, in the center

- **River area**:
The Cham live along the Hau River
 - here you'll find the floating villages and traditional stilt houses
- **Border area**:
The Khmer at the edge of Chau Doc, near Cambodia

- **Sam Mountain**:
Sam Mountain is 6 km from Chau Doc and from here you get an excellent view of the flat plains surrounding the mountain

**Mental map to get your bearings:**
- The river flows from northwest to southeast
- The town center is on the eastern bank
- The Cham villages are reachable by boat across the river
- Sam Mountain is west of town (6 km)
- The Tra Su Forest is to the south (30 km)
- The Cambodian border is very close to the southwest

The beauty of Chau Doc lies in its human scale: everything is reachable on foot, by bicycle or with a short moto-taxi ride, and the river is always your reference point for finding your way.
