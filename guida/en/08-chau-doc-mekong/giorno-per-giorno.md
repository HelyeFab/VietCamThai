# DAY BY DAY — Chau Doc & Mekong

## Day 13 — October 13, 2026 — Among the lotus flowers of the enchanted forest

### Morning

![The Tra Su Forest](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/R%E1%BB%ABng_Tr%C3%A0_S%C6%B0%2C_van_giao%2CTinhBi%C3%AAn_%2C_chau_doc%2CAn_Giang_vietnam%2C05-10-2013-Dyt_-_panoramio.jpg/1280px-R%E1%BB%ABng_Tr%C3%A0_S%C6%B0%2C_van_giao%2CTinhBi%C3%AAn_%2C_chau_doc%2CAn_Giang_vietnam%2C05-10-2013-Dyt_-_panoramio.jpg)

Your morning in charming Chau Doc begins with one of the most magical experiences in the Mekong Delta: a visit to the Tra Su Forest, at its loveliest in the rainy season from September to November, when the water is carpeted end to end in green duckweed.

**Departure and arrival (8:00-9:30)**

The forest is about 20km from Chau Doc. You can book an organised tour straight from town or hire a motorbike and make your own way there. Organised tours leave at 8:00 or 14:00 and include transport, guide and entrance ticket.

**The boat experience (9:30-12:30)**

The entrance price is around 100,000-190,000 VND per person (€4-8)
.
First you board a motorboat for a 20-minute run through the flooded forest, then switch to a traditional rowed sampan to explore the narrower, more intimate canals.

The forest is home to over 140 plant species and 70 bird species, including storks, herons and cormorants. After the boat trip, climb the 25-metre observation tower for a panoramic view over the forest, dotted with hundreds of storks.

**Useful link**: [Official Tra Su tours website](https://vemekong.com/tra-su-forest-tour-from-chau-doc/)

### Afternoon

**Option A: Relaxation and local exploration**

![Crossing the Mekong](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a5/Seasonal_flooding_in_Thailand_and_Cambodia_2002_October_9.jpg)

The forest tours last about 3-4 hours, so you'll be back in Chau Doc by early afternoon. Use the time to explore the town centre on foot or by bicycle, taking in the local market and the streets along the river.

**Option B (Recommended alternative): Sam Mountain at sunset**

From the centre of Chau Doc, a taxi takes about 15 minutes (around 50,000-100,000 VND) to the base of Sam Mountain, which stands 284 metres high, some 6 km from the town centre.

**Temples and cable car (15:00-17:30)**

Visit the Ba Chua Xu Temple at the base, famous for its lotus-flower architecture — three storeys topped with blue-tiled roofs. The cable car costs 90,000 VND return for adults (60,000 VND one way).

### Evening

**Sunset from Sam Mountain (17:30-18:30)**

Nothing beats the sunset from the top of Sam Mountain: peaceful Chau Doc spread out below as the sun sinks slowly like a ball of fire. As the highest peak in the Mekong Delta, it commands an unrivalled view over endless rice paddies and winding rivers.

**Authentic dinner (19:00-21:00)**
For dinner, head to one of these highly recommended restaurants:

- **Quán Mekong** (Le Loi Street):
A popular spot in a quiet courtyard opposite an old colonial villa. The chairs and tables are plain plastic, but the atmosphere is warm and welcoming. Specialities: braised fish and sweet-and-sour soup.

- **Bassac Restaurant** (Victoria Chau Doc Hotel):
An elegant restaurant with a river-view terrace, perfect for a sunset aperitif, serving both Vietnamese and international dishes.

- **Hội Quán Châu Đốc**:
Near the Sam Mountain temples, known for its terracotta-pot dishes and affordable prices. Don't miss the sweet-and-sour fish soup (canh ca chua) and the braised fish.

### Tips of the Day

**🚌 Transport**:
The bus journey from Ho Chi Minh City to Chau Doc costs $7-10 and takes 5-6 hours. Reliable companies: Phuong Trang and Futa Bus
. Book in advance for the best seats!

**🏨 Accommodation**:
Budget hotels from $11-25/night: Paris Hotel ($25), Little Saigon Hotel ($15), The Luxe Hotel Chau Doc ($21)
. For maximum comfort: Victoria Chau Doc Hotel ($93).

**💡 Insider tips**:
-
Tra Su is at its most splendid from October 2025 onwards
 — you're in the perfect period!
-
Dress appropriately for the temples: avoid shorts and sleeveless shirts

-
Bring insect repellent if you visit during the rainy months

-
The best time for Sam Mountain is the dry season (December-April), but October is fine. Avoid weekends for smaller crowds

**🍜 Dishes to try**:
Chau Doc is famous for its seafood — you can't go wrong with fish in a terracotta pot, though if you're not keen on fish sauce, go for the pork version instead. Also try Chau Doc's bún cá, made with snakehead fish, ngai herbs (similar to turmeric) and shrimp paste.

Your first day in the magical Mekong Delta will have given you an unforgettable spectacle of nature, spirituality and authentic flavors!
