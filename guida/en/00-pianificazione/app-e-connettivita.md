## Essential Apps & Connectivity

### Apps to Install Before You Leave

Here are the must-have apps for travelling in Southeast Asia — install them all BEFORE your trip so you set off ready:

#### 🚗 Transport & Getting Around

**Grab** — The undisputed king of Southeast Asia. It's the local Uber that works in Vietnam, Cambodia and Thailand
.
Essential in Southeast Asia, combining ride-sharing, food delivery and payments in a single app
.
- **Download:** [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/grab-taxi-food-delivery/id647268330) | [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.grabtaxi.passenger)
- **Setup:** Link your credit card before you go (2-4% fees), or pay with cash
- 
It automatically adapts to the currency and location when you change country


**PassApp** — 
Only some cities offer one or the other, so it's worth having both on hand in case you need a ride
. Specific to tuk-tuks in Cambodia (Phnom Penh and Siem Reap).
- **Download:** Local Cambodian app store
- **Pro tip:** Use it as a backup if Grab has no tuk-tuks available

#### 🗺️ Maps & Navigation

**Google Maps** — Still the backbone of trip planning in 2026 — and far more powerful than most people realise
.
- **Download:** Pre-installed on Android, [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/maps/id585027354) for iOS
- 
You can also download offline city maps for when you haven't got a SIM card yet and need to navigate to your hostel

- **Trick:** Save places by category (food, sights, cafés) for a lightweight itinerary

**Maps.me** — 
Navigating unfamiliar roads and remote areas in Asia can be daunting, but with Maps.me you can download offline maps of entire countries or regions
.
- **Download:** [Maps.me](https://maps.me/)
- **Perfect for:** Rural areas like Ninh Binh, Phong Nha, the Mekong Delta

#### 💱 Money & Conversions

**XE Currency** — My go-to for working out a travel budget in a place with a different currency. It's reliable, free, up to date and easy to use
.
- **Download:** [XE Currency](https://www.xe.com/apps/)
- **Use:** Always before changing cash or haggling over prices

**Wise** — 
One of the most useful travel apps you can have in 2026. Wise makes spending abroad simple and transparent. You get real exchange rates, low fees and the ability to hold and spend multiple currencies from one account
.
- **Download:** [Wise](https://wise.com/)
- **Setup:** Set up the account BEFORE the trip so you receive the physical card

#### 🗣️ Translation & Communication

**Google Translate** — I lean heavily on Google Translate to bridge the communication gap between me and a local who doesn't speak much English. Google Translate also has a photo option where you can take pictures of signs (or food packaging) and get a translation of what's there
.
- **Download:** [Google Translate](https://translate.google.com/intl/en/about/apps/)
- 
Download the offline packs: Vietnamese, Khmer, Thai



**Papago** — For conversations Papago is far better. Unfortunately it only has a few languages available at the moment, but it DOES include Vietnamese, Thai, Indonesian and Chinese
.
- **Download:** [Papago](https://papago.naver.com/)

#### 🏨 Bookings & Tours

**Agoda** — It's the most popular hotel-booking site across all of Southeast Asia. Prices are often lower than any other app because they specialise in Southeast Asia
.
- **Download:** [Agoda](https://www.agoda.com/mobile)


**Klook** — It's a website created in Singapore where you can get entrance tickets, tours, transport and SIM cards for all of Southeast Asia
.
- **Download:** [Klook](https://www.klook.com/en-US/mobile/)
- **Alternatives:** GetYourGuide for some activities

### SIM & Internet

#### Local SIM Cards (Cheapest)
**Vietnam:**
- 
**Carriers:** Viettel (best coverage), Vinaphone, Mobifone

- 
**Where to buy:** Noi Bai (HAN), Tan Son Nhat (SGN) and Da Nang (DAD) airports; official shops in Hanoi, Hoi An, Ho Chi Minh City

- **Cost:** 
Expect to pay 50,000 – 60,000 VND (about 2-3 USD) for a Vietnamese prepaid SIM

- **Pro tip:** 
A huge bonus with Viettel is that you can use their SIM card in Laos and Cambodia at no extra cost


**Cambodia:**
- 
**Carriers:** Smart Axiata offers the best balance of coverage, speed and reliability in Cambodia's urban and tourist areas. Metfone provides superior coverage in remote regions, while Cellcard offers competitive prices

- 
**Where to buy:** The easiest option for most travellers is to buy a SIM card right on arrival at Phnom Penh International Airport

- **Cost:** 
SIM card prices in Cambodia are quite affordable


**Thailand:**
- 
**Carriers:** AIS, dtac and TrueMove H offer tourist SIMs with the best roaming options—in particular AIS SIM2Fly, which works in all three countries

- 
**Where to buy:** Suvarnabhumi (BKK), Don Mueang (DMK), Phuket (HKT) and Chiang Mai (CNX) airports; 7-Eleven, FamilyMart

### Offline Maps

#### How to Download (Google Maps)

Google Maps remains the default for Southeast Asia. The offline maps feature is criminally underrated—download entire cities or regions in advance so you're covered on ferries, islands, rural highways, or when your eSIM hiccups. I regularly download Bangkok, Chiang Mai, Hanoi, HCMC, Bali and Singapore ahead of time
.

**Procedure:**
1. Open Google Maps on WiFi
2. Search for the destination (e.g. "Bangkok")  
3. Tap the city name at the bottom
4. Tap "Download" 
5. Select the area to download
6. Confirm the download

**Space required:**
- **Bangkok:** ~150-200 MB
- **Hanoi:** ~100-150 MB  
- **Ho Chi Minh City:** ~120-180 MB
- **Phnom Penh:** ~80-120 MB
- **Siem Reap:** ~60-100 MB


**Limitations:** While Google Maps is the gold standard, there's a bit of work involved in getting the offline maps. They certainly don't make it easy and you can only download bits and pieces rather than a whole country, and the interface isn't intuitive
.

#### Alternatives for Rural Areas

**Maps.me** — Navigating unfamiliar roads and remote areas in Asia can be daunting, but with Maps.me you can download offline maps of entire countries or regions. This means you can access detailed maps and navigation without needing an internet connection
.

**iOverlander** — Great for points of interest in rural areas and campsites, perfect for zones like:
- Ninh Binh (Vietnam)
- Phong Nha-Ke Bang (Vietnam)  
- Mekong Delta (Vietnam/Cambodia)
- Mondulkiri (Cambodia)

**Pro Tips:**
- 
Keep both Grab and Bolt installed and download the offline maps before you land

- Download the maps while you're on the hotel WiFi, not on mobile data
- 
The maps have a limited lifespan and can be deleted or updated when the internet is available

- For very remote areas, carry a paper map as backup
