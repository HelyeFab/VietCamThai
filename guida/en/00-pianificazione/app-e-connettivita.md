## Essential Apps & Connectivity

### Apps to Install Before You Leave

Here are the must-have apps for travelling in Southeast Asia — install the lot BEFORE you go so you set off ready:

#### 🚗 Transport & Getting Around

**Grab** — The undisputed king of Southeast Asia, and the local answer to Uber across Vietnam, Cambodia and Thailand. It rolls ride-hailing, food delivery and payments into a single app.
- **Download:** [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/grab-taxi-food-delivery/id647268330) | [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.grabtaxi.passenger)
- **Setup:** Link your credit card before you go (2-4% fees), or pay with cash
- It switches currency and location automatically the moment you cross a border


**PassApp** — The go-to app for tuk-tuks in Cambodia (Phnom Penh and Siem Reap). Coverage varies from city to city, so it's worth keeping both this and Grab on hand in case one comes up short.
- **Download:** Local Cambodian app store
- **Pro tip:** Use it as a backup if Grab has no tuk-tuks available

#### 🗺️ Maps & Navigation

**Google Maps** — Still the backbone of trip planning in 2026 — and far more powerful than most people realise
.
- **Download:** Pre-installed on Android, [App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/maps/id585027354) for iOS
- You can also download offline city maps — a lifesaver for finding your way to the hostel before you've sorted a SIM card

- **Trick:** Save places by category (food, sights, cafés) for a lightweight itinerary

**Maps.me** — Unfamiliar roads and remote corners of Asia can be daunting, but Maps.me lets you download offline maps of entire countries or regions.
- **Download:** [Maps.me](https://maps.me/)
- **Perfect for:** Rural areas like Ninh Binh, Phong Nha, the Mekong Delta

#### 💱 Money & Conversions

**XE Currency** — My go-to for working out a budget in an unfamiliar currency. Reliable, free, up to date and dead easy to use.
- **Download:** [XE Currency](https://www.xe.com/apps/)
- **Use:** Always before changing cash or haggling over prices

**Wise** — One of the most useful travel apps going in 2026. It makes spending abroad simple and transparent: real exchange rates, low fees, and the ability to hold and spend several currencies from one account.
- **Download:** [Wise](https://wise.com/)
- **Setup:** Set up the account BEFORE the trip so you receive the physical card

#### 🗣️ Translation & Communication

**Google Translate** — I lean on it constantly to bridge the gap with locals who don't speak much English. It also has a camera mode: point it at a sign (or a packet of food) and it translates what's there on the spot.
- **Download:** [Google Translate](https://translate.google.com/intl/en/about/apps/)
- 
Download the offline packs: Vietnamese, Khmer, Thai



**Papago** — For back-and-forth conversation it's far better. It only covers a handful of languages so far, but crucially those include Vietnamese, Thai, Indonesian and Chinese.
- **Download:** [Papago](https://papago.naver.com/)

#### 🏨 Bookings & Tours

**Agoda** — The most popular hotel-booking site across Southeast Asia. Because the region is its speciality, its prices often undercut the other apps.
- **Download:** [Agoda](https://www.agoda.com/mobile)


**Klook** — A Singapore-born platform for booking entrance tickets, tours, transport and SIM cards right across Southeast Asia.
- **Download:** [Klook](https://www.klook.com/en-US/mobile/)
- **Alternatives:** GetYourGuide for some activities

### SIM & Internet

#### Local SIM Cards (Cheapest)
**Vietnam:**
- 
**Carriers:** Viettel (best coverage), Vinaphone, Mobifone

- 
**Where to buy:** Noi Bai (HAN), Tan Son Nhat (SGN) and Da Nang (DAD) airports; official shops in Hanoi, Hoi An, Ho Chi Minh City

- **Cost:** Expect to pay 50,000–60,000 VND (about 2-3 USD) for a Vietnamese prepaid SIM
- **Pro tip:** A big bonus with Viettel is that the same SIM works in Laos and Cambodia at no extra cost

**Cambodia:**
- **Carriers:** Smart Axiata strikes the best balance of coverage, speed and reliability in Cambodia's cities and tourist areas; Metfone reaches furthest into remote regions, while Cellcard tends to be the cheapest
- **Where to buy:** For most travellers the easiest option is to grab a SIM the moment you land at Phnom Penh International Airport
- **Cost:** SIM cards in Cambodia are very affordable

**Thailand:**
- **Carriers:** AIS, dtac and TrueMove H all offer tourist SIMs with the best roaming options — in particular AIS SIM2Fly, which works across all three countries
- **Where to buy:** Suvarnabhumi (BKK), Don Mueang (DMK), Phuket (HKT) and Chiang Mai (CNX) airports; 7-Eleven, FamilyMart

#### eSIM (Most Convenient)
eSIMs are perfect if you change country often and value convenience over cost.

### Offline Maps

#### How to Download (Google Maps)

Google Maps remains the default for Southeast Asia. Its offline maps feature is criminally underrated — download whole cities or regions in advance and you're covered on ferries, islands, rural highways, or whenever your eSIM plays up. I always download Bangkok, Chiang Mai, Hanoi, HCMC, Bali and Singapore before I go.

**Procedure:**
1. Open Google Maps on WiFi
2. Search for the destination (e.g. "Bangkok")  
3. Tap the city name at the bottom
4. Tap "Download" 
5. Select the area to download
6. Confirm the download

**Space required:**
- **Bangkok:** ~150-200 MB
- **Hanoi:** ~100-150 MB  
- **Ho Chi Minh City:** ~120-180 MB
- **Phnom Penh:** ~80-120 MB
- **Siem Reap:** ~60-100 MB


**Limitations:** Google Maps may be the gold standard, but getting the offline maps takes a bit of fiddling. It's not made easy: you can only download regions piece by piece rather than a whole country, and the interface isn't the most intuitive.

#### Alternatives for Rural Areas

**Maps.me** — Unfamiliar roads and remote areas can be daunting, but Maps.me lets you download offline maps of entire countries or regions — detailed maps and turn-by-turn navigation with no internet connection needed.

**iOverlander** — Great for points of interest in rural areas and campsites, perfect for zones like:
- Ninh Binh (Vietnam)
- Phong Nha-Ke Bang (Vietnam)  
- Mekong Delta (Vietnam/Cambodia)
- Mondulkiri (Cambodia)

**Pro Tips:**
- Keep both Grab and Bolt installed, and download your offline maps before you land
- Download them on the hotel WiFi, not on mobile data
- Offline maps have a limited lifespan — delete or refresh them whenever you're back online
- For very remote areas, carry a paper map as backup
