### How to Save

**💰 Accommodation (save up to 30%)**
- Guest houses and hostels run €20-40 per night — book at least 2 weeks ahead
- Share double rooms between couples instead of singles
- Consider homestays in rural areas (Ninh Binh, Phong Nha)
- In Siem Reap, stay outside the tourist centre to save €10-15/night

**🚌 Transport (save up to 40%)**
- Backpacker budget: $25-40/day — always take local buses rather than tours
- Book domestic flights 30+ days ahead (Da Nang→HCMC: €30 vs €60)
- Share airport taxis between 4: €15 each instead of €25
- Use Grab instead of tuk-tuks for city journeys

**🍜 Food (save up to 50%)**
- In Vietnam, budget travellers spend an average of €6-10/day on food
- Eat where the locals eat: pho €1.50 vs €5 in tourist restaurants
- Breakfast included with accommodation where possible
- Occasional self-catering at the markets

**🎫 Entrance Fees & Activities**
- Combine visits: 3-day Angkor pass vs a single day
- Group tours vs private ones: Paradise Cave €25 vs €45
- Avoid "official" guides at the entrances — often unnecessary

**💳 Fees & Exchange**
- Fee-free travel card (see below)
- The best ATMs in Vietnam are VPBank and ACB, which charge no withdrawal fees

### Where NOT to Save

**🚨 Never compromise on:**
- **Insurance:** €130 sounds like a lot, but from scooter accidents to sudden illness, it's your safety net
- **Angkor Wat pass:** €62 fixed UNESCO fee but worth every cent
- **Transport safety:** sleeper night buses vs regular seats
- **Safe accommodation:** better to pay €5 more for safe, central areas

3. **Wise** — Uses the mid-market exchange rate, with a transparent fee of about 0.33%

**💰 ATMs & Cash by Country:**

**Vietnam:**
- VPBank and ACB: zero withdrawal fees
- Military Bank and VPBank charge no fees but cap withdrawals lower
- Avoid TPBank, now the priciest at 3.3%
- Limit: 3-5 million VND (€120-200) per transaction

**Cambodia:**
- Best ATMs: PPCBank €5 fee, max $1000; Canadia €5, max $500
- Most ATMs charge $4-6 per transaction, with a $500 withdrawal cap
- Bring USD cash as backup — widely accepted

**Thailand:**
- AEON Bank ATMs: the lowest fees, at 150 THB (~€6) per withdrawal
- Withdraw the maximum 20,000 THB ($600) per transaction to keep fees down
- Find AEON in Big C and Tesco Lotus

**📱 Smart Tips:**
- **2-3 different cards** for safety (one can get blocked)
- **Notify your bank** before departure to avoid blocks
- **Large withdrawals** instead of frequent small ones to minimise fees
- **Always "no conversion"** at ATMs — always accept the local currency
- **Local Vietnam SIM:** €3-5 for emergencies and offline maps

The golden rule: $30/day is realistically doable in Vietnam and Cambodia, but a 20-30% buffer lets you enjoy the trip without fretting over the budget!
