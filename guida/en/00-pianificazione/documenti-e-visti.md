## Documents, Visas & Insurance

### Passport

#### Validity requirements
Your passport must have **at least 6 months' remaining validity** from the date of entry into the most "demanding" country on your itinerary. For your trip, this means that 
passports must be valid for at least 6 months beyond the arrival date
 in all three countries.

#### Pre-departure checklist
- ✅ Check your passport's expiry date (must be valid until at least March 2027)
- ✅ 
Make sure you have at least 2 blank pages for stamps

- ✅ Make a copy of your passport and keep it separate from the original
- ✅ Photograph the page with your details and save it on your phone (in case of loss)

### Vietnam — E-Visa

**Good news for you Italians!** 
Italian citizens can enter Vietnam without a visa for stays of up to 45 days
. Since your time in Vietnam (about 19 days, from 13 to 31 October) stays well under 45 days, **you won't need any visa** for Vietnam.

However, if you decide to extend the trip beyond 45 days, you'll need to apply for the e-visa:

#### E-Visa Procedure (if needed)
1. **Official site**: [evisa.gov.vn](https://evisa.gov.vn) or [evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn](https://evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn)
2. **Cost**: 
$25–$50 USD

3. **Timing**: 
5-7 business days for standard processing

4. **Documents required**:
   - 
Full scan of the passport's biographical page (passport valid for at least 6 months)

   - Digital passport-format photo
   - Credit card for payment

⚠️ **Common mistakes to avoid**:
- 
Always use the official government portals. Using unofficial sites can result in incorrect fees, invalid visas or financial loss

- 
Always check that the e-visa details match the passport exactly


### Cambodia — Visa on Arrival

For Cambodia you'll need a visa, since you'll be arriving by boat from Chau Doc.

#### Visa on Arrival (recommended for you)
- **Cost**: 
$30 USD (tourist visa)

- **Duration**: 
30 days, extendable once for another 30 days

- **What to bring**:
  - 
Passport valid for at least 6 months with 2 blank pages

  - **2 passport photos per person** (4x6 cm format)
  - **$30 USD per person in cash** (new, clean banknotes)
  - Onward/exit ticket out of the country

#### E-Visa Alternative
If you'd rather avoid the queues at the border, you can apply for the e-visa in advance:
- **Official site**: [evisa.gov.kh](https://evisa.gov.kh)
- **Cost**: 
$36 USD

- **Advantages**: No queues, faster process
- **Timing**: 
5 working hours to 3 business days


💡 **Insider tip**: 
The visa on arrival costs $30 USD, payable in cash (the exact amount is recommended)
. Bring new dollars: worn or stained banknotes are often refused.

### Thailand

**Fantastic news!** 
Italian citizens don't need a tourist visa to travel to Thailand in 2026. Italian passport holders can stay in Thailand for a short period, up to 60 days
.

#### Documents required on entry
- 
Italian passport valid for six months beyond the intended stay

- 
Proof of onward travel (confirmed ticket to leave Thailand within 60 days of the arrival date)


#### Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC)
⚠️ **Important new requirement for 2026**: 
It is now mandatory for all foreign nationals entering Thailand to complete the Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC) online before arrival. The form must be completed at most 3 days before arrival
.

The digital card has replaced the old paper TM6 form that Thailand had used for decades.

### Travel Insurance

Travel insurance is **not optional** for Southeast Asia. Here are some real-world examples of healthcare costs:

**Examples of medical costs (without insurance)**:
-

A stay at an international clinic can exceed 50 million VND (1,800 EUR) or more

- 
Medical evacuations from Asia back to your home country can cost over $100,000
