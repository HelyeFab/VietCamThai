## Documents, Visas & Insurance

### Passport

#### Validity requirements
Your passport must have **at least 6 months' remaining validity** from the date you enter the strictest country on your itinerary. In practice, that means every passport needs to be valid for at least 6 months beyond your arrival date in all three countries.

#### Pre-departure checklist
- ✅ Check your passport's expiry date (must be valid until at least March 2027)
- ✅ Make sure you have at least 2 blank pages for stamps
- ✅ Make a copy of your passport and keep it separate from the original
- ✅ Photograph the page with your details and save it on your phone (in case of loss)

### Vietnam — E-Visa

**Good news for you Italians!** Italian citizens can enter Vietnam visa-free for stays of up to 45 days. As your time in Vietnam (about 19 days, 13 to 31 October) comes in well under that, **you won't need a visa** for Vietnam at all.

That said, if you decide to stay beyond 45 days, you'll need to apply for the e-visa:

#### E-Visa Procedure (if needed)
1. **Official site**: [evisa.gov.vn](https://evisa.gov.vn) or [evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn](https://evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn)
2. **Cost**: 
$25–$50 USD

3. **Timing**: 
5-7 business days for standard processing

4. **Documents required**:
   - A full scan of your passport's photo page (passport valid for at least 6 months)
   - Digital passport-format photo
   - Credit card for payment

⚠️ **Common mistakes to avoid**:
- Always use the official government portals. Unofficial sites can land you with inflated fees, invalid visas or outright fraud
- Always double-check that the e-visa details match your passport exactly


### Cambodia — Visa on Arrival

You'll need a visa for Cambodia, as you're arriving by boat from Chau Doc.

#### Visa on Arrival (recommended for you)
- **Cost**: 
$30 USD (tourist visa)

- **Duration**: 30 days, extendable once for a further 30 days
- **What to bring**:
  - Passport valid for at least 6 months, with 2 blank pages
  - **2 passport photos per person** (4x6 cm format)
  - **$30 USD per person in cash** (new, clean banknotes)
  - Onward/exit ticket out of the country

#### E-Visa Alternative
If you'd rather skip the queues at the border, you can apply for the e-visa in advance:
- **Official site**: [evisa.gov.kh](https://evisa.gov.kh)
- **Cost**: 
$36 USD

- **Advantages**: No queues, faster process
- **Timing**: from 5 working hours to 3 business days

💡 **Insider tip**: The visa on arrival costs $30 USD, payable in cash — bring the exact amount if you can. And bring crisp dollars: worn or stained notes are often refused.

### Thailand

**Fantastic news!** Italian citizens don't need a tourist visa for Thailand in 2026. Italian passport holders can stay for up to 60 days.

#### Documents required on entry
- Italian passport valid for six months beyond your intended stay
- Proof of onward travel (a confirmed ticket out of Thailand within 60 days of arrival)


#### Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC)
⚠️ **Important new requirement for 2026**: all foreign nationals entering Thailand must now complete the Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC) online before arrival. Fill it in no more than 3 days before you land.

The digital card replaces the old paper TM6 form Thailand had used for decades.
