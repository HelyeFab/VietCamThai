## General Tips — The Golden Rules of Travel

### Before You Leave

**Strategic bookings**

Book accommodation on Booking.com or Hostelworld with free cancellation
 for the first few days and keep flexibility for the rest of the trip. 
For Angkor Wat, buy the passes in advance and consider hiring a guide for deeper historical context
.

⚡ **INSIDER TRICK**: Only lock in your bookings for the last 5 days — until then, keep everything changeable!

**Essential checklist**
- Visas: 
Thailand offers 60 days visa-free for EU citizens, but since June 2025 you need the ETA (Electronic Travel Authorization)

- 
Travel insurance is mandatory — we recommend Safety Wing for monthly payments with no binding contracts

- Credit cards with no foreign fees
- 
Local eSIMs (Airalo or Nomad) or physical SIM cards for connectivity


### On the Ground

**How to haggle (when to, when not to)**
- **YES**: Local markets, meterless tuk-tuks, souvenirs
- **NO**: 
Taxis with a working meter, restaurants with fixed-price menus

- 
Negotiate politely — prices are flexible, and avoid hotel referrals to sidestep commission markups


**Avoiding tourist traps**

Unfortunately there are plenty of people who try to take advantage of unsuspecting tourists. Use Google to check any service and look at recent posts on forums like Reddit for scams in specific places
.

**Common scams to avoid:**
- 
The "broken" meter or one that runs super fast — always ask for the meter or use Grab

- 
"Your attraction is closed" — always check the opening hours online or at the hotel reception

- 
The motorbike damage scam — always inspect the vehicle for damage before accepting it

- 
The "drug deal gone wrong" — avoid any substance offered on the street


### Dress Code for Temples and Sacred Places

**The universal rule**: 
Cover shoulders and knees — it's simple but fundamental
.

**What to bring:**
- 
A light sarong/pareo always in your backpack
 — super useful for the Grand Palace, Angkor Wat, My Son
- 
A short- or long-sleeved top to cover the shoulders, a skirt or dress that falls below the knees


**What to avoid:**
- 
Sleeveless tops, vests (even with a scarf over them), short shorts, leggings in the stricter temples


**Country-specific:**
- **Thailand**: 
Wat Phra Kaew has a very strict code — men: long trousers and a shirt with sleeves. Women: skirt/long trousers, no tight leggings

- **Cambodia**: 
Angkor Wat has no active temples but has strictly enforced the dress code since 2016 after incidents of inappropriate behaviour and nude photos



Always take off your shoes before entering, stay calm and quiet, and avoid sandals that come undone easily
.

### Photography: Rules and Common Sense


Ask permission before photographing monks or worshippers. Not everyone is comfortable being photographed. And above all, no selfies with the Buddha!



Avoid pointing your feet at the Buddha — it's very disrespectful, since feet are considered the least noble part of the body. Try kneeling or crossing your legs to the side if you're seated
.

### Shopping

**Hoi An Tailors: the tailoring paradise**

Hoi An can make high-quality bespoke pieces in a few days, for a fraction of what you'd pay almost anywhere in the world. It's not only recognised as the best place to have clothes made in Vietnam, it's known as the tailoring capital of the world
.

**Realistic prices 2025-2026:**
- 
Men's three-piece suit: $90-200 (2-5 million VND); women's short dress: $25-50, long: $40-150

- 
Standard two-piece suit: $120-250 depending on fabric and construction

- 
The era of the "good" $50 suit is over, but for $350-600 you can get a garment that rivals the tailoring houses of London or New York


**Process and timing:**
- 
Minimum 3 days for comparisons, initial measurements, fitting and final delivery. The first version based on the initial measurements takes 24-48 hours

- 
Most tailors do 1-3 fittings, each 12-24 hours apart


**⚡ INSIDER TRICK**: 
The tailoring here is dirt cheap, literally 80-95% cheaper than bespoke garments in most places, but "you get what you pay for" always holds. Tailors selling made-to-measure items for $5-10 probably aren't using decent materials
.

**Tips for success:**
- 
Before visiting a tailor, have an idea of what you want: type of garment, basic design, general colour. It helps to bring 1-3 images to communicate what you're after

- 
It's crucial to negotiate the price up front before making any deposit. A 50% deposit is usually required, with the balance on delivery


### Currency Exchange & Payments


GrabPay for Travel is a new payment method that accepts the region's national QR codes and works with the debit/credit cards linked to your Grab account. This reduces the need to fiddle with other banking apps, foreign e-wallets, and one-off situations where the vendor doesn't take card payments
.

**Golden rule**: Avoid currency exchanges at travel agencies — always use an official bank ATM or pay by card.

### Lan Ha vs Ha Long Bay

⚡ **INSIDER TRICK**: 
Go for Lan Ha — the same beauty as Ha Long but less crowded. A 2-day/1-night boat trip > a 1-day tour
.

### Angkor Wat: The Unmissable Sunrise


Leave at 5:00 from your hotel in Siem Reap — bring a torch!
 Sunrise at Angkor Wat is a life-changing experience, but it takes an early-morning sacrifice.

### General Safety


Violent crime is generally unusual and tens of millions visit without any problems every year, making Southeast Asia one of the safest places in the world to travel. Most of the people you meet will be kind, thoughtful and honest
.


Lock away your valuables — it's best not to bring many precious items on the trip. For everything you do bring, it's smart to have a small bag or safe with a lock, or leave them locked up in the hotel/hostel. Back pockets are magnets for pickpockets
.

### Remember


Sometimes, no matter how much you prepare, you'll fall victim to a scam. It might be for a few dollars or more. At the end of the day, as long as you're safe, that's what matters most. Don't put yourself at risk trying to get your money back or avoid being scammed!


Southeast Asia will give you unforgettable memories — follow these tips and enjoy every moment of this extraordinary adventure! 🌴✨
