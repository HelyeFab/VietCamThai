## Practical Info — Lan Ha Bay

### Getting There

From Ninh Binh, your previous stop, you reach Cat Ba (the gateway to Lan Ha Bay) by bus plus ferry/speedboat in about 3.5-4 hours. The route often runs through Hai Phong, where the ferries to the island depart. Once aboard the cruise, you spend the night in a cabin out on the bay.

**Recommended Companies:**
- **Cat Ba Express** — widely rated the best option, with connections from Ninh Binh/Hai Phong ([official site](https://catbaexpress.com))
- **Good Morning Cat Ba** — daily combined bus + boat service ([site](https://goodmorningcatba.com))

**Transport Details:**
- **Duration**: 3.5-4 hours total from Ninh Binh

- **Prices**: around €15-20 per person for the combined bus + ferry leg

- **Sea leg**: speedboat/ferry to Cat Ba, then a local bus to Cat Ba Town (30-45 minutes)


**How it works:**
1. Bus from Ninh Binh towards Hai Phong / the boarding point
2. Ferry or speedboat across to Cat Ba Island

3. Local bus to Cat Ba Town, then a transfer to the pier for the cruise


💡 **Insider tip**: Book the combined transfer through your Ninh Binh accommodation or the cruise company — it saves you wrestling your luggage through awkward changes


**Departing — towards Hanoi:**

Once the cruise is over, you head back from Cat Ba to Hanoi on the direct service (bus + ferry + bus) in about 3-4 hours. There are frequent morning departures arriving in the Old Quarter, and many companies (Cat Ba Express, Good Morning Cat Ba) will pick you up from the pier.


### Getting Around

Cat Ba Island is small and easy to get around. Here are your options:

**Motorbikes (scooters) - Recommended**
- **Price**: 150,000 VND/day (automatic scooter), 200,000 VND (manual) = around €6-8/day
- **Where to rent**: reliable shops all over town, and hotels often throw in the service
- **Documents**: an international driving licence is recommended, though not always required


⚠️ **IMPORTANT**: ALWAYS wear a helmet! The police run frequent checks in town


**Motorbike taxi (xe ôm)**
- **Price**: 30,000-100,000 VND per trip (1.5-4€)
- **Where to find it**: everywhere in town, on the street corners


**Car taxi**
- **Example price**: 50,000 VND from Cat Ba Town to Cai Beo Dock

- **Note**: watch out for covered meters — always agree the price up front


**Grab**
- Only works now and then on Cat Ba — don't count on it

**On foot**
- You can walk across Cat Ba Town in 15 minutes

- Cat Co 1, 2 and 3 and Tung Thu beaches are all within walking distance

### Connectivity & SIM

**Network coverage on Cat Ba:**
- Coverage is generally good, though it can dip a little during boat tours. Viettel performs best

- VinaPhone is especially reliable in the main resort areas such as Cat Ba


**Best operators for Cat Ba:**
1. **Viettel** — the widest coverage, even on the islands and in remote areas

2. **VinaPhone** — great for 5G speed, ranked second in the world for 5G speed in 2025

3. **MobiFone** — a good budget-friendly option that does well along the coast


**Prices and tourist plans:**
- **SIM cost**: 50,000-100,000 VND (2-4€)

- **Data plans**: 70,000-100,000 VND for 7 days, 150,000-200,000 VND for 30 days (3-8€)


**Where to buy:**
- At Hanoi airport before you set off
- Official operator shops in Cat Ba Town
- You'll need your passport to register (it's required by Vietnamese law)


**WiFi:**
- WiFi is widely available and generally free across Vietnam

- Available in all the recommended hotels and restaurants

### Safety

**General situation:**
Cat Ba is generally very safe for tourists, but take these precautions:

**Road safety (priority #1):**
- ALWAYS wear a helmet — the police run frequent checks in town

- Never drive after drinking, and don't speed

- Respect local rules — disrespectful behaviour by foreigners is straining relations


**Common scams to avoid:**
- Taxis with covered meters — always agree the price first

- Inflated prices for tourists — always ask the price up front

⚠️ **Typhoon season**: If you travel July-September, the early-warning systems give 3-5 days' notice
