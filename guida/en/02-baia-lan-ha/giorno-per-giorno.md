## Day 27 — October 27, 2026 — Hidden Pearls in the Emerald Bay

### Morning
**Departure from Ninh Binh (morning)**
- Take the combined transfer from Ninh Binh to Cat Ba (3.5-4 hours) via Hai Phong — bus, ferry/speedboat and local bus rolled into one — for around €15-20 per person

![Cruising in Lan Ha Bay](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Rick_and_Ruddy_afloat%3B_the_cruise_of_a_boy_and_his_dog_%28IA_rickruddyafloatc00gari%29.pdf/page1-960px-Rick_and_Ruddy_afloat%3B_the_cruise_of_a_boy_and_his_dog_%28IA_rickruddyafloatc00gari%29.pdf.jpg)

- Set off from Ninh Binh early so you reach Cat Ba town by midday and board the cruise in the early afternoon

- **Practical tip**: Ask the driver to drop you at Ben Beo Pier so you're already close to where the cruises depart
- **Breakfast**: Bring snacks for the journey — stops are limited

**Arrival and cruise check-in (12:00-13:00)**
- Board around 13:00, settle into your cabin and tuck into a fresh fish lunch

- **Recommended booking**: [Halong Bay Tours](https://www.halongbaytours.com) — 120-195 USD per person for 1-night cruises, 100-145 USD for budget ones

- **Direct link**: [Heritage Line Lan Ha Bay](https://heritage-line.com/cruise/lan-ha-bay/) — packages from 321 USD for 1 night, 837 USD for 2 nights (January-April, October-December 2026)

### Afternoon
**Exploring Cai Beo — Vietnam's Oldest Floating Village (14:00-17:30)**
- Cai Beo, reckoned to be the oldest fishing village in Vietnam at 7,000 years old, is a must-see on any trip to Lan Ha Bay

![Kayaking among the limestone stacks](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/H%E1%BA%A1_Long_Bay_05.jpg/1280px-H%E1%BA%A1_Long_Bay_05.jpg)

- The village stretches about 1 km to the east and north of the port and is home to over 500 families who live off fishing and aquaculture

- **Activity**: A boat tour through the village to nose out its hidden corners, weaving among floating fish farms, colourful floating houses and even floating restaurants

- **Cost of visiting a floating house**: When you call on a family, it's customary to offer a small gift of around 100,000 VND (4 USD) as a thank-you for their hospitality

**Kayaking Among the Limestone Stacks (15:30-17:00)**
- Lan Ha Bay is made for swimming in the crystal-clear water, snorkelling over its beautiful coral reefs and kayaking out to explore the rocky peaks

- **Rainy-day alternative**: With calmer, cleaner water than Ha Long Bay, Lan Ha is ideal for kayaking and swimming

### Evening
**Dinner on board and sunset (18:00-20:00)**
- Dinner on deck at sunset, orange-pink skies mirrored in the water, and no crowds jostling for the best spot

- **Typical cruise menu**: Budget cruises serve simple but decent fish, while mid-range ones offer more variety and better cooking

**Restaurants for dinner in Cat Ba town** (if you don't do the overnight cruise):
- **Quiri Pub Cocktail & Restaurant** — 135 Tung Dinh Street, in the centre of Cat Ba Town, now one of the island's best-loved spots

- **Green Mango** — a great choice for dinner, blending Vietnamese, Asian, international and fusion cooking

- **Quan Cat Ba** — 180 Một Tháng Tư Street, a must-visit seafood restaurant for fresh catch and authentic flavours

- **Average cost**: 250-400k VND (10-16 USD) for a main course

### Tips of the Day

**💰 Money-Saving Tricks**
- Book through your hotel rather than online — they get discounts of 20-30% you won't find anywhere else

- Bring 300-500 USD in Vietnamese Dong for 2-3 days, since most local activities take cash only

**🎒 Absolute Must-Brings**
- Swimsuit, water shoes, a waterproof bag for your phone and camera, SPF 50+ sunscreen, a hat, sunglasses, a light rain jacket, and seasickness medicine if you're prone to it

**⏰ Perfect Timing**
- The best time to visit Ba Trai Dao is September to November, when the weather is at its finest

- You can only swim off the beach for 2-3 hours before high tide covers it completely, so check the low-tide times carefully

**🌟 Insider Secret**
- Lan Ha Bay is quieter and less crowded than Ha Long Bay — perfect if you're after peace and quiet — whereas Ha Long is more famous, with its iconic landmarks, but far busier with boats

**🍽️ Local Specialities Not to Miss**
- Noodle soup with prawns (5-8 USD) — a local speciality with a sweet, fresh broth and locally caught prawns; grilled geoduck (8-12 USD) — a large saltwater clam, grilled or steamed; mantis shrimp (6-10 USD) — roasted with salt, a Cat Ba delicacy

**🚫 What to Avoid**
- Steer clear of June-September if you want to dodge the heavy rain and storms

- Kayaking isn't allowed in the Cai Beo floating village area

- Don't forget to respect the marine environment: travel responsibly, avoid plastic waste and never throw anything into the sea
