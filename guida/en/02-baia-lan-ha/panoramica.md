## Lan Ha Bay — Overview

Picture emerald waters dotted with limestone towers rising from the sea like sleeping giants, where time seems to flow to the rhythm of the waves and you can truly hear the water, the birds and the wind.

![Lan Ha Bay seen from above](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Lan_Ha_Bay_20.jpg/1280px-Lan_Ha_Bay_20.jpg)

 
The same dramatic limestone towers, pristine beaches and emerald-coloured waters — but with 80% fewer tourists. Your complete guide to Vietnam's most underrated bay.

While Ha Long Bay has been world-famous for years, Lan Ha Bay has only recently become a major tourist destination in Vietnam. Yet Lan Ha Bay is no less enchanting, thanks to thousands of limestone islands, many pristine white-sand beaches, crystal-clear water and the tranquil scenery of local villages. Winning travellers over with its unspoiled beauty and great sense of privacy, Lan Ha Bay is a must-see for anyone coming to Vietnam.

In 2026, the bay's appeal reached new heights after it was recognised by UNESCO as a Natural World Heritage Site (together with the Cat Ba Archipelago). This status doesn't just confirm its geological value; it has raised service standards to a truly international level.

### History & Culture

The story of Lan Ha Bay is written in water and stone, a thousand-year saga of people who learned to live in harmony with the sea. 
Its name translates to "the orchid flower that descended to earth", symbolising the poetic beauty of hundreds of limestone karsts.

![Limestone stacks in the bay](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d1/Karst_HaLongBay_Vietnam_%28pixinn.net%29.jpg)

But the bay's true historical gem is 
Cai Beo, also known as the Vu O Fishing Village, considered the oldest fishing village in Vietnam, with archaeological finds dating back over 7,000 years.
 
The story began in 1938, when the French archaeologist M. Colani first discovered the site and suggested it was home to a primitive seafaring community. Later, Vietnamese archaeologists confirmed this and continued to explore the area.

Archaeologists have collected more than 1,400 ceramic objects, 137 stone tools and hundreds of animal bones and shells. Most of these objects were made of granite and ceramic, reflecting the daily life of people who depended on the sea.
 It's not just cold archaeology: it's living proof that for millennia, generations of fishermen thrived here, developing a unique maritime culture that continues to this day.

Although it takes its name from an ancient village with millennia of history, modern-day Cai Beo is home to around 300 families who rely mainly on fishing and aquaculture. Instead of traditional net fishing, locals raise fish in floating wooden cages arranged close together, forming small communities on the water. Since 2015, with electricity and clean water from Cat Ba Island, many have branched out into tourism, offering homestays, fresh fish meals and boat tours.

### When to Go

**The perfect period: late October (your trip falls at just the right time!)**

October: the start of the dry season marks one of the best times of year to sail the bay. Temperatures settle at pleasant levels, humidity drops, skies turn clear and the sea stays calm with excellent visibility. The risk of typhoons, still present in September, decreases sharply.

October is the beginning of autumn as well as the dry season. Ha Long has cool weather in October. The average temperature is only 24.5°C. The lowest temperature is 21°C while the highest is 28°C. This makes travellers love being outdoors to take part in activities and enjoy the cool sea breeze without too much sweat.

**What weather to expect in late October:**
- **Temperatures:** 
21°C-28°C, averaging around 24.5°C. The intense summer heat is now behind you, creating a cool and pleasant environment for exploring.

- **Rainfall:** 
Average rainfall in October is just 54mm for the month compared to 190mm the previous month. There are only 11 rainy days on average.

- **Activities:** 
Lovely sea temperatures (27°C), very comfortable for swimming. Sea temperatures stay warm, typically around 26-27°C (79-81°F). This allows swimming, kayaking and paddleboarding without discomfort.

**What to pack:**
- 
For cool mornings or evenings, bring a light jacket.

- 
A raincoat and hat are still necessary for the trip.

- 
Seasickness pills: Even if you don't normally get seasick, bring them. Lan Ha Bay is generally calm but October-February brings stronger winds and rougher seas. Boats sway at anchor overnight. Take the medicine 1 hour before boarding.

### Orientation

**How the area is laid out:**

Lan Ha Bay lies on the Gulf of Tonkin and is within the city of Hai Phong. It's just 30 km from the centre of Hai Phong city and adjacent to Ha Long Bay. This emerging tourist destination spans over 7,000 hectares and has a relatively unspoiled archipelago of 400 densely clustered islands that form a massive image of fantastical beauty.

**Main zones of the bay:**

1. **Central Lan Ha Bay:** 
Located to the south of Ha Long Bay, Lan Ha Bay is by far the smallest of the three main bays in the area. It comprises around 400 limestone islets spread across 76 km².

2. **Cai Beo Floating Village:** 
The Cai Beo fishing village is a unique tourist site in Lan Ha Bay, inhabited by fishermen for many years and the largest floating fishing village in Vietnam. The boat trip into the bay will take you through the fishing village, where you'll see hundreds of cramped floating cottages, lively activity and huge fish-cage farming structures.

3. **Monkey Island (Dao Khi):** 
Monkey Island features two crystal-clear beaches, Cat Dua 1 and Cat Dua 2. Visitors can climb to the mountaintop and take in a panorama of Lan Ha Bay. Not only can you swim on the beautiful blue beaches, but you can also play with the mischievous yet friendly monkeys on this island.

4. **Ba Trai Dao (Three Peaches Beach):** 
Located in the heart of Lan Ha Bay, just southeast of Ha Long Bay, Ba Trai Dao (Three Peaches Islets) is a breathtaking natural wonder. The name Ba Trai Dao comes from its three limestone peaks, each 23 metres high, which resemble giant peaches on a white-sand beach.
 
However, the beach is only available for swimming 2-3 hours a day because of the tide, which rises and swallows the beach.

**Mental map to get your bearings:**
- **North:** Ha Long Bay (more famous and crowded)
- **Centre:** Cat Ba Island (your starting point)
- **South:** Lan Ha Bay proper (quieter)
- **East:** Towards the open sea
- **Main access:** Beo Pier on Cat Ba Island

Lan Ha Bay lies directly southeast of Ha Long Bay, separated only by a vague administrative boundary — geologically and visually, they are identical. The same imposing limestone towers rising from the emerald waters. The same hidden caves and grottoes. The same dramatic landscapes that look like a classic Chinese painting.

The secret to enjoying Lan Ha Bay is to understand that it's not a destination to rush through — it's a place to let the rhythm of the sea set your pace, where every sunrise and sunset paints a different picture on the jade-coloured waters.
