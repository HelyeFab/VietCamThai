## Lan Ha Bay — Overview

Picture emerald waters dotted with limestone towers that rise from the sea like sleeping giants, where time seems to move with the rhythm of the waves and you can actually hear the water, the birds and the wind.

![Lan Ha Bay seen from above](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Lan_Ha_Bay_20.jpg/1280px-Lan_Ha_Bay_20.jpg)

 
The same dramatic limestone towers, pristine beaches and emerald-coloured waters — but with 80% fewer tourists. Your complete guide to Vietnam's most underrated bay.

Ha Long Bay has been world-famous for years, but Lan Ha Bay has only recently taken off as a major destination in Vietnam. It's every bit as enchanting, with thousands of limestone islands, countless pristine white-sand beaches, crystal-clear water and the quiet charm of local villages. It wins travellers over with its unspoiled beauty and real sense of seclusion, and it's a must-see for anyone coming to Vietnam.

In 2026 the bay's appeal soared after UNESCO recognised it as a Natural World Heritage Site (together with the Cat Ba Archipelago). That status doesn't just confirm its geological value; it has pushed service standards up to a genuinely international level.

### History & Culture

The story of Lan Ha Bay is written in water and stone — a thousand-year saga of people who learned to live in harmony with the sea. Its name translates as "the orchid flower that descended to earth", a nod to the poetic beauty of its hundreds of limestone karsts.

![Limestone stacks in the bay](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d1/Karst_HaLongBay_Vietnam_%28pixinn.net%29.jpg)

But the bay's real historical gem is Cai Beo, also known as the Vu O Fishing Village — reckoned to be the oldest fishing village in Vietnam, with archaeological finds dating back over 7,000 years.

The story began in 1938, when the French archaeologist M. Colani first discovered the site and suggested it had been home to a primitive seafaring community. Vietnamese archaeologists later confirmed as much and carried on exploring the area.

Archaeologists have gathered more than 1,400 ceramic objects, 137 stone tools and hundreds of animal bones and shells. Most were made of granite and ceramic, and they speak to the daily life of people who depended on the sea. This is more than dry archaeology: it's living proof that for thousands of years generation after generation of fishermen thrived here, building a maritime culture all their own that survives to this day.

For all its ancient roots, present-day Cai Beo is home to around 300 families who live mainly from fishing and aquaculture. Rather than casting nets in the traditional way, locals raise fish in floating wooden cages moored close together, so that whole communities take shape on the water. Since 2015, with electricity and clean water piped in from Cat Ba Island, many have branched out into tourism, offering homestays, fresh fish meals and boat tours.

### When to Go

**The perfect period: late October (your trip falls at just the right time!)**

October, the start of the dry season, is one of the best times of year to sail the bay. Temperatures settle into a pleasant range, the humidity drops, the skies clear and the sea stays calm with excellent visibility. The risk of typhoons, still real in September, falls away sharply.

October marks the beginning of both autumn and the dry season, and Ha Long turns cool: the average temperature is just 24.5°C, ranging from a low of 21°C to a high of 28°C. It's weather made for the outdoors — perfect for getting out on activities and enjoying the cool sea breeze without breaking a sweat.

**What weather to expect in late October:**
- **Temperatures:** 21°C-28°C, averaging around 24.5°C. The fierce summer heat is behind you now, leaving cool, pleasant conditions for exploring.

- **Rainfall:** October sees just 54mm of rain for the whole month, against 190mm the month before, and only 11 rainy days on average.

- **Activities:** The sea is a lovely 27°C and very comfortable for swimming. It stays warm, typically around 26-27°C (79-81°F), so you can swim, kayak and paddleboard in comfort.

**What to pack:**
- A light jacket for cool mornings and evenings.

- A raincoat and a hat are still worth bringing along.

- Seasickness pills — pack them even if you don't normally suffer. Lan Ha Bay is usually calm, but from October to February the winds pick up and the seas get rougher, and boats rock at anchor overnight. Take the medicine 1 hour before boarding.

### Orientation

**How the area is laid out:**

Lan Ha Bay sits on the Gulf of Tonkin, within the city of Hai Phong — just 30 km from the centre of Hai Phong and right alongside Ha Long Bay. This up-and-coming destination spans over 7,000 hectares, and its relatively unspoiled archipelago of 400 tightly clustered islands adds up to a scene of almost fantastical beauty.

**Main zones of the bay:**

1. **Central Lan Ha Bay:** Lying to the south of Ha Long Bay, Lan Ha is by far the smallest of the area's three main bays, made up of around 400 limestone islets spread across 76 km².

2. **Cai Beo Floating Village:** The Cai Beo fishing village is one of Lan Ha Bay's most distinctive sights — worked by fishermen for generations and the largest floating fishing village in Vietnam. Your boat trip into the bay threads right through it, past hundreds of cramped floating cottages, bustling activity and vast fish-cage farms.

3. **Monkey Island (Dao Khi):** Monkey Island has two crystal-clear beaches, Cat Dua 1 and Cat Dua 2. You can climb to the summit for a sweeping view over Lan Ha Bay, swim off the beautiful blue-water beaches, and even play with the island's cheeky but friendly monkeys.

4. **Ba Trai Dao (Three Peaches Beach):** In the heart of Lan Ha Bay, just southeast of Ha Long Bay, Ba Trai Dao (Three Peaches Islets) is a breathtaking natural wonder. The name comes from its three limestone peaks, each 23 metres high, that rise like giant peaches above a white-sand beach.

Bear in mind that the tide rises and swallows the beach, so it's only open for swimming 2-3 hours a day.

**Mental map to get your bearings:**
- **North:** Ha Long Bay (more famous and crowded)
- **Centre:** Cat Ba Island (your starting point)
- **South:** Lan Ha Bay proper (quieter)
- **East:** Towards the open sea
- **Main access:** Beo Pier on Cat Ba Island

Lan Ha Bay lies directly southeast of Ha Long Bay, divided from it only by a fuzzy administrative line — geologically and visually, the two are identical. The same imposing limestone towers rising from emerald waters. The same hidden caves and grottoes. The same dramatic landscapes straight out of a classical Chinese painting.

The trick to enjoying Lan Ha Bay is to remember that it's not somewhere to rush through — it's a place to let the rhythm of the sea set your pace, where every sunrise and sunset paints a fresh picture across the jade-coloured water.
